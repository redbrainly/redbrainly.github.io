KERCHER MURDER (2007)

Date and Location: November 1st, 2007 | Perugia, Italy

Suspects: Amanda Knox, Raffaele Sollecito, Rudy Guede

Outcome: Rudy Guede's conviction

Result: Miscarriage of justice, flawed forensic practice are two topics of interest.

OVERVIEW

Meredith Kercher, a British exchange student, was attacked and murdered in her apartment in Perugia, Italy, on November 1, 2007.

Amanda Knox, Kercher's flatmate, allegedly returned home the next morning after spending the night with her boyfriend. The apartment had been left open, there was blood in the bathroom, and Kercher's bedroom door had been locked. She summoned her boyfriend, Raffaele Sollecito, in order to gain access to Kercher's room. After several failed attempts to force open the door, the police were summoned and allowed entry. Kercher's body was discovered partially clothed and covered by a duvet in the bedroom. She had severe knife wounds, which were discovered.

Knox and Sollecito were convicted of the murder of British student Angela Kercher in 2009. They were acquitted by Italy's Supreme Court in 2015 after successfully appealing their convictions. Their eventual acquittal resulted from several flaws in the forensic evidence being identified.

COURT TRIALS

Knox, Sollecito, and Guede were formally charged with murder on July 11, 2008.
