1. What are the characteristics and competencies that made Edgar Sia successful? Why?


Answer:

Edgar Sia is a Filipino businessman who is the founder and CEO of the Mang Inasal chain of fast-food restaurants. Some of the characteristics and competencies that contributed to his success include:

Entrepreneurial vision: Sia had a clear vision for his business from the beginning, which was to offer affordable and delicious Filipino food to the masses. He recognized an untapped market for this type of cuisine and worked to fill that gap.

Risk-taking: Sia was willing to take calculated risks to achieve his goals, such as investing his life savings to start his business. He also expanded his business rapidly, opening multiple stores in a short period of time.

Strategic thinking: Sia made strategic decisions that helped his business grow, such as focusing on a specific niche market, adopting a franchising model to expand more quickly, and prioritizing employee training and development to ensure quality service.

Customer-centric approach: Sia prioritized the needs and preferences of his customers, which helped him build a loyal following. He also listened to feedback and made changes to his menu and service offerings based on customer input.

Financial acumen: Sia had a deep understanding of financial management, which allowed him to make informed decisions about investments, pricing, and budgeting.

Explanation:

Overall, Sia's entrepreneurial vision, risk-taking, strategic thinking, customer-centric approach, and financial acumen are some of the characteristics and competencies that contributed to his success in building the Mang Inasal chain of fast-food restaurants.

16. who or what inspired edgar sia to become a entrepreneur​


Answer:

Edgar ‘Injap’ Sia II was born in Iloilo City in 1977 and grew up in Roxas City, Capiz province. He is the eldest of three siblings. Despite Injap

being a true-blue provincial guy and unfamiliar with the complexities of the big business environment of the Philippine capital, he made sure he is able to learn and adapt fast. In 2011, Sia become the youngest billionaire in the Philippines at the age of 34 after selling his 70% stake in Mang Inasal to Jollibee Foods Corp.

He had his elementary, high school, and college education all in the province. He originally planned to become an architect but then dropped out of college (University of San Agustin in Iloilo City) to focus more on his business, but he strongly believed that his provincial background having the grassroots knowledge that he learned on the ground can become his advantage.

Sia co-founded the barbecue chain Mang Inasal in Iloilo City in 2003.In seven years, Mang Inasal grew to 338 branches nationwide, before being acquired by Jollibee in 2010.

In 2009, Sia founded Injap Land Corporation (now DoubleDragon Properties Corporation), developer of CityMall chain of malls. In 2014, DoubleDragon Properties became a public company. He also listed his own grocer Merrymart Consumer Corp this 2020 in the Philippine Stock Exchange.

27. what is the structure of the story of tell tale heart by edgar allan poe


Tell-Tale Heart was a short story written by Edgar Allan Poe. It tells the story of the narrator who just murdered his old man because of the fact that the old man possesses the ''vulture eye''.

The story follows a traditional structure.

It starts with an exposition, the narrator informs the readers that he and his old man live together inside an apartment. He also mentions that he is quite annoyed with the eye of the old man and even calls it ''evil''. He also explicitly mentions on how he wants to murder the old man so he could ''free'' himself. The narrator also insist that he is not insane.

Next, is the rising action, which was him planning to do the murder but failed to do so, it was when he attempted to murder the old man in his room for the span of seven nights, in which he failed because he believed that the old man was watching him.

His act of committing the murder was the climax of the story, it was at its peak. He fulfilled his purpose and goal, which was to get rid of the old man and to free himself from the eye. At this point of the story, he felt victorious, triumphant and he refers himself as to wise

After the climax comes the falling action of the story and the falling action of the story was when he deliberately hides the evidence by chopping up the body parts of the old man and hiding them under the floorboard. Then, policemen came to check on his house, because of his neighbor's complaint regarding the noise. The narrator invited the cops inside his house with utmost hospitality to avoid suspicion. He offered them food, drinks, and a chair to relax. He got a chair for himself and purposely placed it directly above the floorboard the hid the body parts in. It was then that he started overthinking and guilt was eating up on him, till the point that he mentally broke down and confessed to the police about his crime.

The story ended with him becoming completely insane.
