14. If quantitative research is all about the quantity, how about qualitative research?​


Answer:

qualitative research generates “textual data” (non-numerical). Quantitative research, on the contrary, produces “numerical data” or information that can be converted into numbers.

If quantitative research is all about the quantity, how about qualitative research?​

What is qualitative research? Data for qualitative research is acquired from a variety of sources, including direct observation, interviews, questionnaires (on which participants write descriptively), focus groups, participant observation, recordings made in natural settings, documents, and artifacts. The majority of the data is non-numerical. Qualitative techniques include ethnography, grounded theory, discourse analysis, and interpretative phenomenological analysis. Qualitative research approaches have been used in sociology, anthropology, political science, psychology, social work, and educational research. Qualitative researchers look into how people see their social surroundings.

Qualitative research investigates elements of human existence such as culture, expression, beliefs, morals, life stress, and imagination, and is informed by numerous strands of philosophical thinking. A number of philosophical disciplines, such as positivism, postpositivism, critical theory, and constructivism, have inspired contemporary qualitative research.

This research opted to provide information, as means to gain insight, on how the municipality of

Schiedam is changing, and, subsequently, how the organization deals with that change. It ended

up demonstrating how different organizational change management elements are present, or

absent, in the municipality’s process of change. On a scientific level, this information is relevant to

scholars studying organizational change management and/or the changes Dutch municipalities

face, as it provides a link between the two topics in the context of a real-life case. This is also of

interest to anyone wanting to get a deeper understanding of the subjects.

On a practical level, this research’s relevance depends on how well the results for this one specific

case can be generalized. Generalization concerns the question whether the results of a study are

applicable in a broader context beyond the one in which the research was conducted (Bryman,

2012, p. 711). Regarding generalization, Bryman (2012, p. 205) states that any findings can only

be generalized to the whole from which the sample was taken. Therefore, the results of this

research can only be generalized to municipalities experiencing change. Subsequently, some

conclusions are better generalizable than others.
