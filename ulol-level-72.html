14. a submarine was situated 850 meters below sea level .if it ascends 72 meters,whatis it new position?


A moving objects position is sometimes just measured by subtracting the distance traveled thereafter from the initial traveled distance.  Looking at the problem, we can interpret the phrase "850 meters below sea level" as a vertical distance traveled by the submarine.

Just imagine you have the y-axis, zero as the reference point (sea level), going up will be above sea level, and going down will be below sea level. However, we will just ignore the negative sign on the lower part of the axis since distance will always be positive.  

If the submarine has already traveled 850 meters below sea level (from zero you go down the axis by 850), then its position will be at 850 meters below sea level. If it ascends (meaning to go up the axis) by 72 meters, then finding its position will just be subtracting 72 from 850

[tex]850 meters - 72 meters = 778 meters[/tex]

This means that the submarine is at 778 of the axis or it is "778 meters below sea level".

20. a cannon ball is fired from groun level at an angle of 60 degress with the groun at a speed of 72 meter per second. What is the horizontal velocity at the time of launch?


To solve for the horizontal velocity, identify your given first. You have 60° which is your angle or Theta and your speed, which is your Vi or Initial Velocity,  is 72m/s.  

You are trying to solve for the horizontal velocity and the formula you are going to use is Vx or Horizontal Velocity = Vi cos Theta. So, the solution will be Vx = 72m/s cosine 60 and the answer will be Vx = 36m/s.

Apply SIGNIFICANT FIGURES, the answer will be Vx = 40m/s.

Given:

θ = 60°

Vi = 72 m/s

Find:

vx

Formula:

vx = vicosθ

Solution:

vx = vicosθ

vx = 72 (cos60)

vx = 36

vx = 40

Final Answer:

vx = 40 m/s

*In using significant figures in problems like this, the number of significant figures that you will use in your final answer is the least number of significant figures in the given. In this problem, two givens have one significant figure while the other hase three, therefore, we will use one in the final answer.

26. level1 cache is the smallest but the fastest among the different cache levels​. true or false


True

Cache is graded as Level 1 (L1), Level 2 (L2) and Level 3 (L3):

L1 is usually part of the CPU chip itself and is both the smallest and the fastest to access. Its size is often restricted to between 8 KB and 64 KB.

L2 and L3 caches are bigger than L1. They are extra caches built between the CPU and the RAM. Sometimes L2 is built into the CPU with L1. L2 and L3 caches take slightly longer to access than L1. The more L2 and L3 memory available, the faster a computer can run.
