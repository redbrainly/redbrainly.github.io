20. Compute the weight of a body on the moon if the mass is 65Kg? Gravity is given as 1.75 m/s2


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The weight of an object is

[tex]\boxed{W = mg}[/tex]

where:

W = weight

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for W

W = mg

W = (65 kg)(1.75 m/s²)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{113.75 N}}[/tex]

Therefore, the weight of the body on the moon is 113.75 N.
