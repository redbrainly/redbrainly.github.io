8. Explain - To see how inflation adversely affects the present education system especially the Grade 12 ABM student in st. Clare college ​


Answer:

Inflation is a sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services over time. This can have adverse effects on various aspects of the economy, including the education sector.

One group that may be particularly affected by inflation in the education sector is Grade 12 ABM (Accountancy, Business, and Management) students in St. Clare College. This is because the cost of education, including tuition fees, textbooks, and other school-related expenses, may increase as a result of inflation.

As prices increase, families may have to spend more money to cover their basic needs, such as food, housing, and healthcare. This may leave them with less money to spend on education-related expenses, including tuition fees and textbooks. As a result, some students may struggle to pay for their education, which could lead to a decline in the quality of their education or even drop out of school entirely.

Moreover, inflation can also lead to a decrease in the purchasing power of money. In other words, the value of money decreases over time, which means that the same amount of money can buy fewer goods and services than before. This can make it more challenging for students and families to afford the same quality of education that they could in the past.

In conclusion, inflation can have negative impacts on the education system, particularly for Grade 12 ABM students in St. Clare College who may face increased costs for tuition and other school-related expenses. This can lead to a decline in the quality of education or even force some students to drop out of school entirely, thus limiting their future opportunities.

13. "Explain"To see how inflation adversely affects the present education system especially the Grade 12 ABM student​


Answer:

Let us say that there are 100 Grade 12 ABM students in a given school district. Each student needs to purchase a specific set of textbooks, workbooks, and other course materials in order to complete their course of study. The total cost of these materials is $1,000 per student.

Now, let us assume that the rate of inflation is 10%. This means that the cost of the same set of materials will now be $1,100 per student. This represents an increase of $100 per student, or 10%.

This increase in the cost of materials will have a direct impact on the students' ability to afford these materials. Some students may be forced to go without the materials they need in order to complete their course of study. This will have a negative impact on their education and may ultimately lead to them not graduating from high school.

5. What is the difference between career college and university?

A career college is a post-secondary institution that offers specialized education required to enter a specific field of work. This is different from university degrees, which offer a broad base of knowledge and require students to commit to a full four years of education before entering the workforce. Career college education provides a more specialized, career-focused training program that can be completed in a matter of months — typically less than one year. This also means students will usually pay much less in overall tuition at a career college, making their educational investment more worthwhile. With a strong focus on career-specific requirements and practicums, career colleges like ABM College can help bring you closer to your dream job more quickly.
