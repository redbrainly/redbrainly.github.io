26. Why is the eyes not a functional organ in the absence of light rays?


Answer:

Because the image that we see is because of light rays.

Explanation:

How does the eye work?

This is the process on how the eye lets us perceive or see the objects around us with the help of light rays.

Light rays will bounce off objects we are looking at and will enter our eyes through the cornea. Cornea then refracts and bends the light rays that will pass through the pupil which is the opening in the center of the iris. The part of the eye called the iris works like a shutter in the camera.

This process enables us to see the objects around us. Without the light rays, there won't be anything that will enter our eye that will be refracted in order to see the images. Therefore, our eyes won't function.
