11. A sampie of oxygen at 24 °C and 745 torr has a volume of 455 ML. How many grams of oxygen were in the sample ?​


Answer:

0.586 grams

Explanation:

Assume Ideal Gas condition

Thus,

PV = nRT or

PV = m/M (RT)

PVM = mRT

m = PVM/RT

where R = 62.36367 L-Torr/mol-K

mass Oxygen (O2) = (745 Torr * 0.455Li * 32 g/mol)/(62.36367 * (24+273))

mass Oxygen (O2) = 0.5856 grams

13. The ratio of Maybel’s money to Nove’s money was 4:7 when Nove gave mabel ₱240.00, the ratio of their money became even. How much money did Maybel have at first?


Answer:

₱640.00

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's assume that Maybel's money at first was 4x, and Nove's money was 7x.When Nove gave Maybel ₱240.00, Maybel's money became 4x + ₱240.00, and Nove's money became 7x - ₱240.00.Since the ratio of their money became even, the new ratio of their money should be 1:1 or 4x + ₱240.00 : 7x - ₱240.00 = 1:1.Expanding the right side of the equation, we have 4x + ₱240.00 = 7x - ₱240.00.Combining like terms, we have 3x = ₱480.00.Dividing both sides by 3, we have x = ₱160.00.

So Maybel had 4x = 4 * ₱160.00 = ₱640.00 at first.

18. Why creating an effective playbill is important? *​


Answer:

Receiving a Playbill as you enter the Theater is not only expected… it is tradition! The small pamphlet or Program of informative goodies includes details on the presentation along with the Director, Cast and Crew. Biographies and interesting tid bits that serve as a prelude to the unfolding story is a custom read before and after the play. This has been practiced since the mid Eighteenth Century. Without printing options, Theaters had a few smaller pages on which they hand wrote a scarcity of notes. Playbills evolved into the latter Eighteenth into the Nineteenth Century. More information was given, and the biographies began.

all the gas samples have the same temperature and mass. in which of the following conditions will the gas sample have greater volume? a. 0.5atm​

According to Boyle's Law

Volume is inversely proportional to the pressure of the gas when temperature and amount of gas are constant. When volume increases, pressure decreases.

The formula of Boyle's Law is;   P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

The lowest pressure  will have the greater volume.
