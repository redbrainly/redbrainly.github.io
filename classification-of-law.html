2. fourfold classification of law​


Aquinas establishes four types of laws: eternal law, natural law, human law, and divine law

Eternal Law is the Divine Wisdom of God which oversees the common good and governs everything

Divine Law is the historical laws of Scripture given to us through God’s self-revelation

Natural Law is “the rational creature’s participation in the eternal law” (ST I-II, Q. 91, A. 2.)

Human Law is the interpretation of natural law in different contexts (ST II.I.95–97) Government laws are dictates of practical reason from the precepts of Natural Law


3. classification of Aquinas law​


Answer:

Aquinas distinguishes four kinds of law: (1) eternal law; (2) natural law; (3) human law; and (4) divine law.
