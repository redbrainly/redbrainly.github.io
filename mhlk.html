9. milk solution milk solution


MILK SOLUTION

Milk is a liquid containing white nutrients that are produced from the mammary glands of mammals, such as cows and goats. Based on the chemical aspect, milk is a fat emulsion in which there is an aqueous solution of sugar and mineral salts with protein in a colloidal state. Milk as one of the drinks with high nutritional value has good properties to support health.

Milk is a heterogeneous mixture, because it consists of two or more, namely fat and protein which are dispersed in water, and the substances are not mixed evenly. Milk is considered a colloidal solution of fat globules in water, both of which are liquid phases that cannot be mixed together.

In order to better understand the explanation above, consider the following explanation:

A mixture is a substance made from a combination of two or more different substances without going through a chemical reaction. The combination of these substances can be in the form of elements with elements, compounds with compounds, or elements and compounds. A substance cannot combine or unite because it still retains its original properties. The mixture is divided into two, among others:

Heterogeneous mixture

Occurs when all the materials that make up the mixture and their properties can still be physically distinguished from one another. If filtered, it will produce remnants of particles.

Example: milk, coconut milk, and a mixture of water with oil for frying.

Heterogeneous mixtures are divided into two, namely:

Colloid: a heterogeneous mixture consisting of two or more constituent substances, in which one of the substances spreads over other substances but not evenly.Suspension: a heterogeneous mixture consisting of a solid and a liquid or gas, and if allowed to stand for a long time will produce a precipitate. This is because the solid is not dissolved in the solvent.Homogeneous mixture

Occurs when all the materials that make up the mixture cannot be distinguished from one another. However, the properties of each of its constituent materials can still be seen.

Example: a mixture of water with cocoa powder milk.
