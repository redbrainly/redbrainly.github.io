13. What's In Identify the following types/classification of sandwiches. 1. Tea sandwiches 2. Multidecker sandwich 3. Toasted sandwiches 4. Deep fried sandwich 5. Quesadillas 6. Pinwheel sandwiches 7. Grilled sandwich 8. Regular cold sandwich​


1. Tea sandwiches - is a small prepared sandwich meant to be eaten at afternoon teatime to stave off hunger until the main meal.

2. Multi decker sandwich - Are made with more than two slices of bread (or rolls split into more than two pieces) and with several ingredients in the filling.

3. Toasted sandwich - is a sandwich in which the filling between two slices of bread is itself a thin slice of toasted bread, which may be buttered.

4. Deep fried sandwich - is any combination of ingredients enclosed by slices of bread, rolls or batter before being submerged in oil and cooked until crisp on the outside and warm on the inside.

5. Quesadillas - is a Mexican dish consisting of a tortilla that is filled primarily with cheese, and sometimes meats, spices, and other fillings, and then cooked on a griddle or stove.

6. Pinwheel sandwich - are mini-sandwiches that have been rolled up in either a flour tortilla (or tortilla wrap) or flattened bread.

7. Grilled sandwich - is a hot sandwich typically prepared by heating one or more slices of cheese between slices of bread, with a cooking fat such as butter, on a frying pan, griddle, or sandwich toaster, until the bread browns and the cheese melts.

8. Regular cold sandwich - A plain sandwich is made up of two slices of bread, preferably a day-old bread, toasted if desired, and on which butter can be readily spread.

Brief History of Sandwich

A sandwich is a type of food typically consisting of vegetables, cheese, meat which are placed between slices of bread. Originally, the name sandwich was used for a thin sliced of meat between the slices of bread and butter. It was introduced in the 18th century and the credit was given to Mr. Early Sandwich.

Types of Sandwich

There are two types of sandwiches. They are hot sandwich and cold sandwich. Each type is categorized according to its substantial look and ambiance. Below are the kinds of sandwiches that belong to each type:

Hot Sandwiches

Regular Hot Sandwich - consists of hot fillings like meat, fish, grilled vegetables, and other hot items.

Hot Open-Faced Sandwich - made by placing the bread on a serving plate and covered by hot meat with sauce or gravy.

Grilled Sandwich - simple sandwich that got buttered and browned on the griddle or in a hot oven.

Deep-Fried Sandwich - made by dipping sandwiches in beaten egg, bread drums, and then deep-fry.

Filled Rolls or Pitta Bread - flavored bread served with dips such as quesadillas and burritos.

Cold Sandwiches

Open-Faced Sandwich - an open bread with some fillings on top.

Regular Cold Sandwich - a plain sandwich which is made up of two slices of bread, specifically a day-old bread with butter, mayonnaise, or any prepared sandwich spread.

Pinwheel Sandwich - made of rolled-up bread which was cut lengthwise.

Tea Sandwich - a small fancy sandwich made ahead of time and frozen.

Multi-Decker Sandwich - made with two or more slices of bread with several ingredients in the filling such as a club sandwich.

Wrap Sandwich - a sandwich which fillings are wrapped like tortillas.
