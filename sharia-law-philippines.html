2. why is sharia law important to muslims?​


Answer:

Within Islamic discourse, šarīʿah refers to religious regulations governing the lives of Muslims. For many Muslims, the word means simply "justice," and they will consider any law that promotes justice and social welfare to conform to Sharia.

Answer:

Explanation:

11. 5 major sections of sharia​


Answer:

The Sharia regulates all human actions and puts them into five categories: obligatory, recommended, permitted, disliked or forbidden. Obligatory actions must be performed and when performed with good intentions are rewarded. The opposite is forbidden action.
