Is SF6 polar or nonpolar?

SF6 is a nonpolar molecule.

Explanation:

This is because the VSEPR theory says that when six fluorine atoms are arranged symmetrically around the sulfur atom, the bond dipoles are cancelled. As a result, it is a nonpolar molecule.

Fluorine is highly electronegative so each atom is trying to pull electrons towards itself. However, look at the above figure for once. Since they all are trying to pull electrons towards themselves, it must mean that opposite fluorine atom is trying to pull it as well. Problem:

A sample of 6.0 mole of sulfur hexafluoride (SF6) is present in a container with a volume of 22.4L. what is the pressure (in atm) of the gas in the temperature is 130°C?​

Note:

130°C + 273 = 403 K

Given Data:

V (volume) = 22.4 L

n (moles of gas) = 6 moles

T (Ktemperature) = 403 K

R (constant) = 0.0821 L•atm/mol•K

P (pressure) = Unknown

Solution:

We can solve this problem using the ideal gas law formula (PV=nRT), and to find P, we can use the formula P = nRT/V

P = nRT/V

  = 6 × 0.0821 × 403 / 22.4

  = 198.5178 atm / 22.4

  = 8.86 atm

Answer:

the pressure (in atm) of the gas in the temperature is 130°C is 8.86 atm

18. at 2.50 g of SF6 is introduced into an evacuated 5-L container at 83 degrees celsius, what pressure in atmospheres is developed


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V = \text{5 L} \\ & \text{mass} = \text{2.50 g} \\ & T = 83^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{356.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of the gas.

The molar mass of SF₆ is 146.1 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{\text{mass}}{\text{molar mass}} \\ & = \frac{\text{2.50 g}}{\text{146.1 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.017112 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Solve for the pressure of the gas by using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P & = \frac{nRT}{V} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.017112 mol})\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{356.15 K})}{\text{5 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.100 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pressure developed in atm is 0.100 atm.
