18. Why is technology considered as the ""silver lining of our education""?


Answer:

Silver lining means a sign of hope or a positive aspect one sees while in a negative situation. This comes from the silver-like color of the edges of the clouds when the sun is about to come out on the cloudy day.

With the lockdowns and other restrictions, such as social distancing and limited face to face interactions brought about by the pandemic, many students initially lost their opportunity to continue with their education as preparation for their respective employment. Technology, however, stepped in, and like the silver lining seen on a cloudy day as the sun is about to come out, technology offered unprecedented education access to students all over the country. With the use of high-speed internet connection, ordinary devices such as smartphones and tablets that are a common fixture nowadays are transformed into tools that allows student to continue learning, even at the comfort of their own homes. Despite the restrictions, technology provided students anywhere to attend classes, take examinations, submit their work, and get assistance from their teachers, all from the comforts of their own homes. This also mean reduced cost in the long run, as students don't have to spend a lot on transportation fees, food allowances and uniform, and for the schools as study from home means less expenses on the maintenance of school buildings, less utility consumption such as water and electricity, and it allows schools to have more enrollees as they won't need to construct more classrooms to accommodate more students. The abundance of free applications that allows screen sharing and videoconferencing make education affordable and available to many students.
