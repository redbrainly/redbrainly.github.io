10. Square Root of  (3h²-147=0)


SOLVING QUADRATIC EQUATIONS WITH THE SQUARE ROOT METHOD

The solutions that satisfies the quadratic equation 3h²-147=0 are h = 7 and h = -7 .

What is Quadratic Equation?

Mathematical equations with a greatest variable of two are called quadratic equations. In addition to its roots, the quadratic equation also has types.

The Standard Form of a Quadratic Equation:

ax² + bx + c = 0

where:

x represents an unknown (variable)

a, b, and c represent known numbers, where a ≠ 0

What is Square Root Method?

Quadratic equations of the form "[tex]x^{2}[/tex] = b" can be resolved using the square root method. Since a number's square root can be either positive or negative, this procedure can produce two results. Finding the square roots of x will allow you to answer any equation that can be written in this way.

How could you solve such a quadratic equation?
There are three basic ways to solve the quadratic equations:

factoring, using the quadratic formula, and completing the square (Square Root Method).

Usually, you will not be told which method to use. You will have to make that decision yourself.

To solve a quadratic equation by SQUARE ROOT METHOD

Put the equation into the form ax 2 + bx = – c.Make sure that a = 1 (if a ≠ 1, multiply through the equation before proceeding).Using the value of b from this new equation, add to both sides of the equation to form a perfect square on the left side of the equation.Find the square root of both sides of the equation.Solve the resulting equation.
