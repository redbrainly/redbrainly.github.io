Answer:

The poem "Hanggahan" by Genaro R. Gojo Cruz describes the speaker's home, inviting the reader to enter and experience its various rooms and sounds. The speaker warns the reader not to look up at the vast sky, as it might disorient them. The speaker also mentions the neighboring homes and their occupants, suggesting that the reader should listen carefully to their sounds as they move through the home. The poem seems to convey a sense of longing and nostalgia for the speaker's home and the memories it holds. The use of the term "hanggahan" (which can be translated as "boundaries" or "limits") suggests the idea of a physical and emotional space that is defined and personal to the speaker.
