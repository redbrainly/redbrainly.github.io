19. Why do student vandalize school facilities?​


Answer:

There are several reasons why students vandalize school facilities and property. Let's talk about why it happens.

School facilities exist to make sure students have an environment conducive to learning. Facilities and properties are paid for by the student's parents or the taxpayers' money. This means that students must have the conscience or common sense not to do anything destructive because, in the end, the real victims are themselves.

So why are they still doing it? The long explanation here is that students are young people with still developing brains which means that their ability to make decisions is not at its best. Children will often do something that will instantly please themselves or gain the attention they want even if it is irrational. They do not think a lot about their actions and the consequences that may follow.

If a child is not taught to respect the property that is not theirs, they will not see its value. It becomes easy for them to take it for granted and to destroy it. If they want to rebel against authorities or if they want to be accepted by their peers, they may do something outrageous and forbidden. One of these acts is vandalism. If they are not caught, it makes them cooler in front of their peers, and it gives them a feeling of being better than others.

Some acts of vandalism are not acts of rebellion. Some are just mindless acts that they did not realize they were doing. Like doodling on desks and chairs absentmindedly, removing important school posters or notices from bulletin boards, or even replying to funny writings on the school's bathroom walls. Again, we can chalk this up to a lack of foresight on the student's part.
