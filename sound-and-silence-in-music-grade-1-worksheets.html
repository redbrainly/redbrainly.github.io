20. what is composed of sound and silence and emphasis in music?​


Answer:

In music, sound is what you hear when a musical instrument or voice makes a noise. Silence is the absence of sound, when there is no noise at all. Emphasis is when a particular note or beat is highlighted in a piece of music, often by playing it louder or with more emphasis than other notes.

Together, sound, silence, and emphasis form the basic elements of musical expression. Musicians use these elements to create different moods and emotions in their compositions, from calm and soothing to energetic and intense.
