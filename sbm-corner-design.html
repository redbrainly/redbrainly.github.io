2. which countries apply SBM? did sbm implementation help improve their schools? Share your findings​


SCHOOL-BASED MANAGEMENT (SBM)

School-based management (SBM) is a strategy to improve education by transferring significant decision-making authority from state and district offices to individual schools.

School-based management provides principals, teachers, students and parents control over the education process by giving them the responsibility for decisions about the programs/projects. SBM can create a learning environment for the students.

El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, and Nicaragua introduced education decentralization reforms, including school-based management (SBM) in some schools.

School-based Management (SBM) has been implemented in Indonesia since 2000, but after 16 years of implementation, there are still many schools that have not succeeded in implementing it. Implementation of school-based Management so far has met with limited success. Although most principals reported that they had the autonomy to make school decisions, they also said that they did not take advantage of it by making significant programmatic and instructional changes.
