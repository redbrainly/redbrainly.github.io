12. Specialist (or data communications analyst), which is a job title in the operations area of an it department, evaluates, installs, and monitors data and/or voice communications equipment and software and maintains connections to the internet and other wans.


To keep information secure, a data communications analyst updates and maintains a network system. They are referred to as computer network architects at times.

You must have in-depth understanding of networks and expertise developing hardware and software systems for all types of enterprises or organizations if you want to succeed as a data communications analyst. LAN (local area networks) and WAN (wide area networks) design, monitoring, maintenance, and testing are among your job responsibilities (wide area networks). When working on a project, you frequently look up expenses, assess hazards, and identify technology that the business may use to boost productivity.

A bachelor's degree in information technology, computer science, or a similar discipline is required to work as a network systems and data communications analyst, while some employers will also consider applicants with several years of relevant job experience. Although specialist training and networking certification are recommended, certification is not necessary.

Data communications analysts design dynamic and effective systems for usage by corporations and organizations using their understanding of network structure and operations. These information technology specialists upgrade and maintain systems to keep them functional and secure. Data communication analysts typically have a bachelor's degree and prior network system experience.
