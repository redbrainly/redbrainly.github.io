Disk plow

The disc plow is designed to perform duties such as soil breaking, soil lifting, soil turning, and soil mixing in all types of soil. It's used to clear fresh fields and treat stony terrain. It is simple to utilize in rocky and rooted locations. To reach maximum depth, disk plows often contain three or more independently placed concave disks that are tilted rearward.

Water pump

A water pump is an electromechanical device that raises the pressure of water in order to transport it from one location to another. Modern water pumps are utilized to supply water for municipal, industrial, agricultural, and residential purposes all over the world. Water pumps are machines that carry water from one location to another. They are essential in agriculture since they transport water from the source to the fields and crops. Water pumps can be used with a variety of irrigation systems, including drip irrigation, sprinklers, and hose irrigation.

Hand tractor

A hand tractor is a tilling machine driven by hand that is used to cultivate a larger area of land. It's also known as a power tiller, and it's a 2-wheel tractor with a high-powered motorized horsepower engine that's driven by a person walking behind it to propel the tractor forward. A hand tractor is used to prepare a big area of land by pulling a plow and harrow.

Four-wheel tractor

A four-wheel tractor is a self-propelled vehicle used to transport, haul, and move agricultural machinery and tools. This machine can help you do your land preparation tasks more quickly and efficiently. It offers higher traction and is flexible in both dry and wet soil conditions. Tractors are well-suited to undulating and sloping terrain. It's particularly effective for wet terrain puddling because it eliminates the need for cage wheels, which can harm roadways. It has a heavier front axle, which improves hauling stability and reduces front lifting while transporting heavy loads.

20. How much power does a water pump expend when a 3920 J water is pumped in 3 minutes?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The power of an object doing a work W in a given time t is given by

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{W}{t}}[/tex]

where:

P = power

W = work

t = time

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Converting minutes to seconds

t = 3 min × (60 s / 1 min)

t = 180 s

Solving for P

[tex]P = \dfrac{W}{t}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{\text{3920 J}}{\text{180 s}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{21.78 W}}[/tex]

Therefore, the amount of power expended by the water pump is 21.78 W.
