8. classify the following mixtures in their proper column-iron -smoke. -batchoy-alcohol -spaghettie -halohalo-books -clothesin the library inside the cabinet​


Answer:

Iron - homogenous

Alcohol - homogenous

Zonrox - homogenous

Books in the library - heterogenous

Smoke - heterogenous

Spaghetti - heterogenous

Clothes inside the cabinet - heterogenous

Batchoy - heterogenous

Halo-halo – heterogenous

Explanation:

Homogenous means that the item is consists of parts, elements, or pieces that are all the same. In other words, a homogenous substance is uniform in composition. In the items above, Iron is homogenous, as it is an element, meaning it cannot be chemically broken down further into basic substances. Alcohol and Zonrox, as cleaning agents are homogenous unless water is added to these solutions. Both can also be classified as heterogenous substances if we break down their compositions. Example is, Alcohol is only up to 70% isopropyl or ethyl alcohol.

Heterogenous, on the other hand is the opposite of homogenous. These are composed of two or more different stuff. The food examples above are a mixture of different ingredients, making them heterogenous. Smoke is a mixture of gases from a burning matter, is also heterogenous.

19. classify the following mixtures in their proper column​ kailangan ko na ngyon


Answer:

Homogeneous Mixtures

alcoholzonroxwine

Heterogeneous mixture

books in the librarysmokespaghetticlothes in the cabinetbatchoyhalo-halo

Explanation:

Mixture is a classification of matter wherein components are combined physically, there is no chemical bond formed, and no new substance is formed. There is no definite proportion of the components also.

A homogeneous mixture is a kind of mixture wherein the components are uniformly distributed. You would usually see just a single phase of the material. You can not distinguish the individual components. Solutions are homogeneous mixtures wherein solute is completely dissolved in a solvent. Examples are alcohol zonrox and wine. What you see here is one phase of material because components are evenly distributed.

A heterogeneous mixture, on the other hand, is a kind of mixture wherein the individual components can still be distinguished. Like for instance books in the library, you can still identify the different books in the library. Same also with spaghetti wherein you can still identify the ingredients such as pasta noodles, sauce, cheese, and meat. Other examples include smoke, clothes in the cabinet, batchoy, and halo-halo.

Iron was not included in the classification since iron alone is an example of an element that is a pure substance. But if we are referring to iron ore, it is a rock where we extract iron. This iron ore can be considered a heterogeneous mixture because it is made up of several substances that are not evenly distributed.
