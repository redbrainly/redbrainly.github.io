12. The Scores Of Individual Students On An A National Test Have A Normal Distribution With Mean 18.6 And Standard Deviation 5.9​ at Federico rural high school 76 students took the test. If the scores at this school have the same distribution as national scores, what are the mean and standard deviation of the sample mean for 76 students?


Answer:

The sample mean is 18.6 and the sample standard deviation is 0.68

Step-by-step explanation:

1. In any math problem, it is very important to identify what the given information are.

Based on what we have:

The population mean (represented by the Greek letter μ) is 18.6.

The  population standard deviation (represented by the Greek letter σ) is 5.9.

The size of the sample/sample size of test taker from Federico Rural High School is 76.

2.Let's answer the question.

a. Sample mean

For the mean of the sample mean of the 76 students, the mean is equal to the population mean.

That is, μ (population mean) = μ[tex]_x[/tex] (sample mean)

Since μ = 18.6, then μ[tex]_x[/tex] = 18.6

b. Sample Standard deviation

To solve for the standard deviation (σ[tex]_x[/tex]), use the formula σ[tex]_x[/tex] = σ/[tex]\sqrt{n}[/tex]

σ[tex]_x[/tex] = 5.9/√76 = 0.68

The sample mean and sample standard deviation of the 76 students who took the National Test from Federico Rural High School are 18.6 and 0.68, respectively.
