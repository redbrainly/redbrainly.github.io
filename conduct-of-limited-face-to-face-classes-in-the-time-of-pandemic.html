16. argumentative essay for conduct of limited face to face classes in the time of pandemic​


Answer:

The novel coronavirus pandemic has disrupted life as we know it. In the face of this global health crisis, many educational institutions have had to pivot to online learning to protect the safety of their students, staff, and faculty. However, some argue that online learning is not an effective substitute for in-person instruction and that schools should instead be focusing on ways to conduct limited face-to-face classes safely.

There are a few key reasons why face-to-face classes are important. First, they provide students with an opportunity to interact with their peers and mentors in a way that is not possible online. Second, face-to-face classes allow for more immediate feedback and collaboration between students and instructors. And finally, research has shown that face-to-face instruction is generally more effective than online instruction, particularly for certain subject areas such as math and science.

Despite these advantages, there are also some significant risks associated with conducting face-to-face classes during a pandemic. The most obvious risk is the potential for virus transmission. However, there are also risks associated with the increased stress and anxiety that come with having to worry about contracting the virus. In addition, face-to-face classes can place a strain on already overburdened healthcare systems.

Given the risks and benefits of conducting face-to-face classes during a pandemic, educational institutions must carefully weigh the pros and cons before making a decision. There is no easy answer, and each situation is unique. However, what is clear is that any decision should be based on the best available science and the needs of the students, staff, and faculty.
