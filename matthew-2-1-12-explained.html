Answer: They were an astrologers, possibly sorcerers who are very interested to any magical interpretation presented by the stars. This work actually prohibits by God to Israelite.

2. Is the star came from the true God?

Reference:   Matthew 2:2,3 saying: “Where is the one born king of the Jews? For we saw his star when we were in the East, and we have come to do obeisance to him.” At hearing this, King Herod was agitated, and all Jerusalem with him.

Answer: By saying star, it is surely different from other stars in heaven which are very familiar with astrologers. But we knew that God will never use an astrologers to proclaim the good news about the birth of Jesus. He already gave this privilege to some shepherds during the actual night of his birth. In addition, the stars guided the astrologers not directly to Jesus but the King Heron who was agitated that someone will take over his position. These scenarios will let you know that this star is not a normal one neither from God. But from whom the star came? From Satan himself who wanted to kill the baby.

But God obstructed his evil plan. Jesus had flee before the massacre.

23. What is the reflection of Matthew 3:1-12?​


Reflection of Matthew 3: 1-12

There are many points considered in the reflection of Matthew 3: 1-12. One of them is repentance. Matthew 3: 1-12 is about John the Baptist who, as Isaiah the prophet described, called the people to prepare the way of the Lord Jesus Christ before he was born as a human. John is calling the Israelites to prepare the way of the Lord by repenting and turning away from sins. Consequently, he baptized with water those who repented as a symbol of cleansing from the sins they committed.

More so, John the Baptist affirmed that those who claim to be descendants of Abraham and do not need cleansing are not exempted from the final judgment. Everyone is subject to final judgment. As such, it also meant that salvation is not only for those who are descendants of Abraham. It is available to everyone who is willing to receive it.

Another interesting reflection of Matthew 3: 1-12 is the humility of John the Baptist. He was appointed by God to become a prophet and a preacher - both noble tasks. In humility, he accepted his calling. Interestingly, even if it's God who has appointed him, he also knew that Someone mightier than him is coming. He even revealed that he is not worthy to even carry this One's sandals. Humility is a virtue that is often forgotten and overshadowed by pride and vain. It keeps a person's eyes and heart away from necessary repentance and from learning obedience, kindness, and wisdom.

Explanation:

English:

"But if you do not let men have forgiveness for their sins, you will not have forgiveness from your Father for your sins."

Clearly, forgiveness is important. It works two ways, in that we need forgiveness from the Lord for our sins, and... that we need to forgive others. We need to make a supplication.
