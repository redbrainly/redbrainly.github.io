10. JADC is conducting a survey on grade 11 student of Taguig City University. She found our that there are only few students who knew about makers of the philippine flag consisting of 1,2,3,4 and 5 SHS student from 5 sections, Suppose that the sample size of 2 sections were drawn from this population.1.find the variance of the sampling distribution of the sample mean​


[tex] \large \mathcal\colorbox{teal}{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

Let [tex]A, B, C, D,[/tex] and [tex]E[/tex] be the 5 sections.

[tex] \quad \begin{array}{l c c c c c} \hline \textsf{Section} & A & B & C & D & E \\ \hline \textsf{No. of students} & 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 \\ \hline \end{array} [/tex]

Since we know the number of students who knew about the makers of the Philippine flag from each section, we can find the population mean.

[tex] \quad \mu = \dfrac{1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5}{5} = 3\textsf{ students} [/tex]

Now, obtain all of the possible samples of size [tex]n=2[/tex] (sections) from the population (without replacement) so we can demonstrate the sampling distribution.

No. of sample of size [tex]2 = {}^5C_2 = 10 [/tex]

The table below shows all the possible samples, the number of students of each section who knew the makers of the Philippine flag, the sample mean, and the probability of obtaining each sample. Since we are drawing at random, each sample will have the same probability of being chosen, which is equal to [tex] \frac{1}{10} = 0.10. [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{| c | c | c | c |} \hline \textsf{Sample} & \footnotesize \textsf{No. of students} & \overline{x} & \textsf{Probability} \\ \hline A, B & 1, 2 & 1.5 & 0.10 \\ \hline A, C & 1, 3 & 2 & 0.10 \\ \hline A, D & 1, 4 & 2.5 & 0.10 \\ \hline A, E & 1, 5 & 3 & 0.10 \\ \hline B, C & 2, 3 & 2.5 & 0.10 \\ \hline B, D & 2, 4 & 3 & 0.10 \\ \hline B, E & 2, 5 & 3.5 & 0.10 \\ \hline C, D & 3, 4 & 3.5 & 0.10 \\ \hline C, E & 3, 5 & 4 & 0.10 \\ \hline D, E & 4, 5 & 4.5 & 0.10 \\ \hline \end{array} [/tex]

We can combine all of the values and create a table of the possible values and their respective probabilities.

[tex] \qquad \begin{array}{| c | c |} \hline \overline{x} & \textsf{Probability or }p(\overline{x}) \\ \hline 1.5 & 0.10 \\ \hline 2 & 0.10 \\ \hline 2.5 & 2(0.10) = 0.20 \\ \hline 3 & 2(0.10) = 0.20 \\ \hline 3.5 & 2(0.10) = 0.20 \\ \hline 4 & 0.10 \\ \hline 4.5 & 0.10 \\ \hline \end{array} [/tex]

The table is the probability table for the sample mean and it is the sampling distribution of the sample mean of the number of students when the sample size is 2 (sections). It is also worth noting that the sum of all the probabilities equals 1.

Now that we have the sampling distribution of the sample mean, we can calculate the mean of all the sample means. In other words, we can find the mean (or expected value) of all the possible [tex]\overline{x}'s[/tex] and then the variance.

Computing for the mean and variance,

[tex] \begin{array}{| c | c | c | c |} \hline \overline{x} & p(\overline{x}) & \overline{x}\cdot p(\overline{x}) & \overline{x}^2\cdot p(\overline{x}) \\ \hline 1.5 & 0.10 & 0.15 & 0.225 \\ \hline 2 & 0.10 & 0.20 & 0.40 \\ \hline 2.5 & 0.20 & 0.50 & 1.25 \\ \hline 3 & 0.20 & 0.60 & 1.80 \\ \hline 3.5 & 0.20 & 0.70 & 2.45 \\ \hline 4 & 0.10 & 0.40 & 1.60 \\ \hline 4.5 & 0.10 & 0.45 & 2.025 \\ \hline & & \displaystyle \scriptsize \!\sum\! \big[\overline{x}_i\!\cdot\! p(\overline{x}_i)\big]\! =\!3\! & \displaystyle \scriptsize \!\sum \!\big[{\overline{x}_i}^2\!\cdot\!p(\overline{x}_i)\big] \!= \!9.75\! \\ \hline \end{array} [/tex]

Mean of the sampling distribution of the sample mean:

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \displaystyle \mu_{\overline{x}} = \sum \big[\overline{x}_i\cdot p(\overline{x}_i)\big] = 3 \end{array}[/tex]

Variance of the sampling distribution of the sample mean:

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \displaystyle \sigma_{\overline{x}}^2 = \sum \big[{\overline{x}_i}^2\cdot p(\overline{x}_i)\big] - (\mu_{\overline{x}})^2 \\ \\ \sigma_{\overline{x}}^2 = 9.75 - 3^2 = \boxed{0.75} \quad \textit{Answer} \end{array} [/tex]


11. essay about argumentative essay about technology. advantage and disadvantages of having a technology in the campus​


Technology has become an indispensable part of our daily lives, including in the field of education. With the rapid advancement of technology, schools and universities have integrated technology into their curriculum and campus infrastructure. However, the use of technology in the campus has been the subject of debate, as it has both advantages and disadvantages. In this essay, I will argue for and against the use of technology in the campus.

One of the major advantages of technology in the campus is its ability to enhance the learning experience. With the help of technology, students can access a vast array of educational resources, including online libraries, educational videos, and interactive software. This allows for a more personalized and interactive learning experience. Moreover, technology enables collaboration between students and teachers through online discussion forums and video conferencing.

Another advantage of technology in the campus is its ability to increase efficiency. With the use of technology, students can submit their assignments online, eliminating the need for printing and physically submitting papers. This saves time and resources for both students and teachers. In addition, technology can be used to automate administrative tasks, such as attendance tracking and grade recording, reducing the workload of teachers.

However, the use of technology in the campus also has its disadvantages. One of the major drawbacks is its potential to distract students from their studies. With the widespread use of social media and other entertainment applications, students may become more interested in browsing the internet than focusing on their studies. Moreover, the over-reliance on technology may lead to a decline in traditional skills, such as handwriting and critical thinking.

Another disadvantage of technology in the campus is its cost. The integration of technology requires a significant financial investment, including the purchase of hardware and software, as well as the cost of maintenance and upgrades. This may not be feasible for all educational institutions, particularly those in developing countries or those with limited resources.

In conclusion, while technology has brought significant advantages to the campus, it is not without its drawbacks. Therefore, it is important for educational institutions to strike a balance between the use of technology and traditional teaching methods, and to be mindful of its potential impact on students and the learning process. The proper integration of technology in the campus can enhance the learning experience and increase efficiency, but it must be done in a way that is responsible and sustainable.
