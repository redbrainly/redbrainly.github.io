BANANA MUFFINS

INGREDIENTS: 1/2 cup unsalted butter, (8 Tbsp), softened2/3 cup granulated sugar2 large eggs, room temp, lightly beaten3 bananas, very ripe, coarsely mashed with a fork1 tsp vanilla extract1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, measured correctly1 tsp baking soda1/2 tsp salt1 cup semisweet chocolate chips, divided

US Customary - Metric

INSTRUCTIONPreheat oven to 350˚ F and line a 12-count muffin tin with paper liners.In a mixing bowl, cream together 8 Tbsp softened butter with 2/3 cup sugar. Add 2 lightly beaten eggs.Add 3 mashed bananas and 1 tsp vanilla extract and mix until blended.

In a separate bowl, whisk together dry ingredients: 1 1/2 cups flour, 1 tsp baking soda and 1/2 tsp salt. Mix the dry ingredients into wet ingredients until incorporated.Fold in 3/4 cup chocolate chips. Divide batter evenly into your 12-count muffin tin and sprinkle the remaining 1/4 cup chocolate chips evenly over the tops. Bake at 350˚F on the center rack for 25-30 minutes or until the top is golden and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out.

16. Directions: List the needed ingredients and recipes in making banana turon. Write your answers on activity sheet. ​


Answer:

•Bananas– turon use saba or cardaba which are a type of cooking bananas indigenous to the Philippines. If you’re in the U.S. and can’t find them, plaintains will work in a pinch. Do not substitute cavendish (Dole) bananas as they’ll turn mushy.

•Jackfruit– adds another layer of sweetness. For convenience, use sweetened langka sold sold at most Asian supermarkets

•Lumpia wrappers– thin spring roll wrappers that come in round or square shape

•Sugar– use granulated white sugar to coat the bananas for a touch of sweetness and the wrappers to caramelize during frying

•Oil– use oil with a neutral taste and high smoke point such as canola, safflower, corn, or grapeseed oil

How many cups of banana are needed in the recipe?​

Many recipes ask for "1 cup of mashed bananas" or "1 cup of sliced bananas," but figuring out how many bananas are in a cup can be difficult. To make cooking a little easier, we conducted some experiments to determine how many bananas you'll need. To find out how many bananas are in a cup, we went out and bought many pounds of bananas. We opted to utilize a medium banana as our standard of measurement because each pound of bananas comprises roughly 3 or 4 medium bananas. The bananas were then chopped and mashed to determine how many bananas were required for a cup. We calculated that 3 bananas are required for a cup of mashed bananas. 1.5 bananas are required for a cup of sliced bananas.
