The methodology of a quantitative research is the set of procedures and techniques used to conduct the research. When designing a methodology for your research, you should consider the following:

The research question or problem: What is it that you want to study? What is the research question or problem that you are trying to answer or solve?

The research design: How will you go about answering the research question or problem? Will you use a survey, an experiment, a case study, or some other research design?

The sampling method: How will you select the participants for your study? Will you use a random sample, a convenience sample, or some other sampling method?

The data collection method: How will you collect data from your participants? Will you use questionnaires, interviews, observations, or some other method?

The data analysis method: How will you analyze the data that you collect? Will you use statistical techniques like the Z-test, regression analysis, or ANOVA?

A Z-test is a statistical test that is used to compare the mean of a sample to a known population mean. It is often used when the sample size is small and the population standard deviation is known. In your research, you can use the Z-test to compare the mean scores of the two sample groups (e.g., those who struggled and those who made adjustments) on each of the topics (e.g., personal, emotional, intellectual).

To use the Z-test, you will need to calculate the Z-score for each sample group on each topic. The Z-score is a measure of how many standard deviations a sample mean is from the population mean. If the Z-scores are significantly different from each other (e.g., if the p-value is less than 0.05), then you can conclude that there is a significant difference between the two sample groups on that topic.
