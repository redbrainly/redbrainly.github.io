3. title:"THE STORY OF AN HOUR" what is the settings​


Answer:

“The Story of an Hour” is a short story written by Kate Chopin. In the story, Louise receives the bad news that her husband, Brently, has died in a train accident. The short story shows the reader how Louise is dealing with these sad news and how she is trying to come to terms with it. However, at the end of the story, there is an unexpected twist: Brently returns home, alive and well. Instead, Louise dies of a heart attack.

The setting of the short story is in Louise’s house, presumably in the living room, as Louise is not on her own. She is with her sister, Josephine, who delivers the bad news, and with Richards, “her husband’s friend.” Louise’s house is located on a road, as we learn that “in the street below a peddler was crying his wares.” This suggests that Louise does not live somewhere remote or rural but that she lives in a town. 1. The news that Mrs. Mallard received was that her husband, Brently Mallard, had died in a train accident.

2. The news needed to be broken to her gently because her husband's death would be a great shock to her, and her husband's sister, Josephine, is afraid of the news would cause her to have a heart attack.

3. When she received the news, she initially responds with a physical reaction of weakness and cries.

4. When Mrs. Mallard entered her room, she could see the open window which symbolizes freedom and new possibilities.

5. The dull stare in her eyes could mean that she is in a state of shock and disbelief and is not processing the information fully yet.

6. Her realization of freedom tells that she has lived a repressed life as a married woman, and now she has the opportunity to live her life as she pleases.

7. It was hard for her to consider it a monstrous joy because of the guilt she feels over being happy about her husband's death.

8. Throughout the story, specific details give hints about Mrs. Mallard's true feelings about the death of her husband, such as her initial reaction of physical weakness, her realization of freedom, and her feeling of guilt about her newfound freedom.

9. The ending of the story is unexpected and tragic. It is suggested that the shock and joy of her newfound freedom, combined with the guilt and conflicting emotions she felt, could have caused her death.
