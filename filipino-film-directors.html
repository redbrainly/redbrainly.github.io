4. in the philippine film industry, name at least three outstanding film directors


Three outstanding film directors in the Philippines:

1. Lino Brocka

Catalino Ortiz Brocka (April 3, 1939 – May 22, 1991) was a Filipino film director. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential and significant Filipino filmmakers in the history of Philippine cinema. He co-founded the organization Concerned Artists of the Philippines (CAP), dedicated to helping artists address issues confronting the country, and the Free the Artist Movement and was a member of the Coalition for the Restoration of Democracy.

He directed landmark films such as Tinimbang Ka Ngunit Kulang (1974), Maynila sa mga Kuko ng Liwanag (1975), Insiang (1976), Bayan Ko: Kapit sa Patalim (1984), and Orapronobis (1989). After his death in a car accident in 1991, he was posthumously given the National Artist of the Philippines for Film award for "having made significant contributions to the development of Philippine arts."

2. Peque Gallaga

Peque Gallaga (born Maurice Ruiz de Luzuriaga Gallaga on August 25, 1943) is a multi-awarded Filipino film-maker. His most significant achievement in film is "Oro, Plata, Mata", which he directed after winning a script writing contest sponsored by the Experimental Cinema of the Philippines. He has received an award from the International Film Festival of Flanders-Ghent, Belgium in 1983; a Special Jury Award from the Manila International Film Festival; and the 2004 Gawad CCP Para sa Sining.

3. Carlo J. Caparas

Carlo Magno "Carlo" Jose Caparas (born December 15, 1958), widely known as Carlo J. Caparas, is a Filipino comic strip creator/writer-turned director and producer, who is best known for creating such Filipino superheroes and comic book characters as Panday, Bakekang, Totoy Bato, Joaquin Bordado, Kamagong, Kamandag, Elias Paniki, Tasya Fantasya, Gagambino, Pieta and Ang Babaeng Hinugot Sa Aking Tadyang, among others. He is also known as a director of numerous massacre movies such as Kuratong Baleleng and The Cory Quirino Kidnap. Caparas was awarded the 2008 Sagisag Balagtas Award.

What is a film director?

A film director is a person who directs the making of a film. A film director controls a film's artistic and dramatic aspects and visualizes the screenplay (or script) while guiding the technical crew and actors in the fulfillment of that vision. The director has a key role in choosing the cast members, production design, and the creative aspects of filmmaking.

Outstanding Film Directors in the Philippine Film Industry

The Philippine Film Industry has been around for 100 years. With its age are the increasing number of directors hailed from different walks of life. However, some are truly outstanding in their works that they become critically-acclaimed in both local and international industry. Three of the most outstanding film directors in the Philippine film industry include Auraeus Solito, Brillante Mendoza, and Lino Brocka. Additionally, Marilou Diaz-Abaya, Susana C. 28. What is the function of a film director


Answer:

a Film Director is the person in charge of the written (scripts) and visual (composition) creative elements of a film. As the root word of the Director(direct) implies, a Film director's role is to direct the production on how the movie should be shown to the movie theaters.

Explanation:

A Film Director guides other artists in the production team throughout the process of producing a film. He is also in charge of the film composition. This would mean that a Film Director is required to have a good creative mind and imagination to accomplish the task. In other words, a Director should have a good vision of the project.

In film production, the Film Director is second to the highest position, next to the producer and assistant producer. This gives the Director the power to decide on choosing what creative elements to use in the film.
