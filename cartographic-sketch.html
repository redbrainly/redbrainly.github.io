Answer:

Understanding the theory of continental drift

Continental drift theory or the theory of continental drift was put forward by a scientist named Alfred Wegener in 1912. This opinion was expressed in a book entitled The Origin of Continents and Oceans which stated that the continental landmass crossed the Earth by 'drifting'. Wegener describes this movement as a process of continental drift.

Alfred Wegener, an astronomer, used the study of biology, botany, and geology to explain the origin of the Earth's surface. He believed that in the distant past, Earth consisted of only one supercontinent called Pangea. The term pangea itself comes from the Ancient Greek language which means the whole earth.

According to Wegener, Pangea existed about 240 million years ago or at the end of the Triassic Period. This huge landmass began to fragment 200 million years ago. Over the next million years, Pangea continued to separate into continents that drifted apart.

Detail of theory

In more detail, the continental drift that has shifted westward from America opens the Atlantic Ocean. The Indian block 'floats' across the Equator and coincides with Asia. This piece is the forerunner of the 7 continents of Africa that exist today.

To strengthen the theory that he proposed, Wegener presented a number of evidences. Including how the existing pieces of the continent can be 'put together' like a puzzle.

This is because of the shape of the edge of the continent, especially which shows that the continental shelf has similarities between parts. Reported by the Britannica.com page, the most easily identifiable relationship is eastern South America with the Gulf of Africa.
