10. What food chain does occur in the forest ecosystem? A. grass, mouse, birds B. grass, caterpillar, snake C. tress, snake, lion D. tress, rabbit, owl


FOOD CHAIN THAT OCCUR IN THE FOREST

The food chain depicts the process by which energy is transferred from one living organism to another via food. It is critical that we understand how the food chain works so that we can identify the important living organisms that comprise the food chain and how the ecology is balanced. The food chain describes how energy and nutrients circulate in an ecosystem. Plants produce energy at the most basic level, and then it progresses to higher-level organisms such as herbivores. When herbivores eat carnivores, energy is transferred from one to the other. Energy is transferred from one living organism to another in the form of food along the food chain.

GROUP

Which choice has a group of only consumers?

a. grass, cows, birds

b. owl, snake, bear

c. tigers, turtles, seaweeds

d. cactus, sun, mushroom

ANSWER

Amongst the given choices above the correct answer is letter B. owl, snake, bear.

The question is fairly easy, because letter B. only includes live animals, owls, snake and bear are all consumer, they eat or prey plants, small animals and other potential foods.

EXPLANATION

The three other choices include at least 1 plant, that is why they all are invalid, because plants (seaweeds, cactus, mushrooms and grass) are not consumers but rather a producer.
