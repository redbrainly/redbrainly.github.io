Anti-rabies vaccination in pet cats is generally considered to be an important and responsible step for pet owners to take in order to protect their cats from the potentially deadly rabies virus. Rabies is a highly infectious and fatal disease that can be transmitted to both animals and humans. Vaccinating cats against rabies not only protects them from the disease, but it also helps to prevent the spread of rabies to other animals and humans.

One of the main benefits of anti-rabies vaccination in pet cats is that it provides a high level of protection against the disease. Rabies vaccinations are highly effective in preventing the disease, and they are considered to be one of the most important medical interventions for cats. Regular vaccinations are also important to maintain the immunity level of the cats.

Another benefit of anti-rabies vaccination in pet cats is that it is relatively easy and inexpensive to administer. Vaccinations can be given by a veterinarian, and they are typically administered as a single injection. The cost of the vaccination is generally quite low, and it is considered to be a small price to pay for the peace of mind that comes with knowing that your cat is protected from this deadly disease.

Furthermore, pet cats that are vaccinated against rabies are also more likely to be accepted by animal shelters, boarding facilities, and pet-sitting services. This is because many of these organizations have strict vaccination requirements for the cats they accept.

However, it is important to note that, just like any other medical intervention, there may be some potential risks and side effects associated with anti-rabies vaccinations. These can include pain or swelling at the site of the injection, fever, or allergic reactions. These risks are considered to be very low, and they are generally outweighed by the benefits of vaccination in protecting cats from rabies.

8. NAME THE REPUBLIC ACT FOR "ANTI- BULLYING ACT OF 2013"​


Answer:

The Anti-Bullying Act of 2013 (RA 10627)

This law finds applicability in school-related bullying, student-student bullying in particular, which covers those uttered in social media. “Bullying”, as defined, refers to any severe or repeated use by one or more students of a written, verbal or electronic expression, or a physical act or gesture, or any combination thereof, directed at another student that has the effect of actually causing or placing the latter in reasonable fear of physical or emotional harm or damage to his property; creating a hostile environment at school; infringing on the rights of another; or materially or substantially disrupting the education process. (Sec. 2, RA 10627) These acts are collectively called “cyber bullying” when committed online. (Sec. 2-D, RA 10627) This covers social bullying aiming to belittle another individual or group or gender-based bullying that humiliates another on the basis of perceived or actual sexual orientation and gender identity. (Sec. 3, B-1, RA 10627, Implementing Rules).
