An interval joining two points on the circle is called a chord. A chord that passes through the centre is called a diameter. Since a diameter consists of two radii joined at their endpoints, every diameter has length equal to twice the radius.

28. 3. two chords that are not diameters​


Answer:

If in a circle two chords (which are not diameters) cut one another, then they do not bisect one another. In the words of Euclid: If in a circle two straight lines cut one another which are not through the centre, they do not bisect one another.
