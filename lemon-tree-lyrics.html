9. what is the musical for of lemon tree​


"Lemon Tree" is a song by German band Fool's Garden from their third album, Dish of the Day (1995). The band's lead vocalist, Peter Freudenthaler, said that he wrote the song on a Sunday afternoon when he was waiting for his girlfriend.[citation needed] "Lemon Tree" was released as a single in November 1995 and became an international hit the following year. • Problem:

The ratio of apple trees to lemon trees in an orchard is 7 to 6 If there are 91 apple trees, how many lemon trees are there?

• Solution:

We need to apply what we've learned about ratio and proportion. First is to write the given ratio in fraction form. Then try the cross multiplication to get the value of the unknown number.

[tex] \large\tt \frac{7}{6} = \frac{91}{x} \\ \large\tt7x = 91(6) \\ \large \tt7x = 546 \\ \large \tt x = 546 \div 7 \\ \large\tt x = 78[/tex]

Thus, the answer is 78.

You can also try solve for the value of the unknown when you write the given ratios in colon form. With that form, you need to equate the product of the extremes to the product of the means. You'll arrive on the same answer.

There are 78 lemon trees.

30. A lemon tree grows 3/4 times as much as Santan. If a Santan grows 2/3 cm in 2 months, what is the total length of the lemon tree and the Santan?​


Answer:

1 1/6 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Finding the length of the lemon tree:

3/4 x 2/3

Multiply the numerators and denominators together

(3)(2)/(4)(3)

6/12

Reduce the fraction into lowest terms

= 1/2

The lemon tree's length is 1/2 cm in 2 months.

Finding the total length of both the santan and lemon tree:

1/2 + 2/3

Change dissimilar fractions into similar fractions

3/6 + 4/6

Add the numerators, copy the denominator

7/6

Simplify

= 1 1/6

The total length of both plants is 1 1/6 cm.
