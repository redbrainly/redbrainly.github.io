Answer:

FIRST, there was a cruel ruler of the land who despised anything related to deteriorating health and strength, particularly in the elderly. He issued a proclamation to have the elderly sent to Obatsuyama, or the mountain where "the aged are abandoned," and left to die.

NEXT: A man and his elderly widowed mother resided in that country at the base of the mountain. The man was moved to send his cherished mother to the mountain after hearing the announcement.

His mother instructed him to follow the twigs she had laid on the ground while ascending the mountain right before he reached the summit and before climbing down.

He returned home with his mother and hid her beneath the floor where he cared for her since he could not bear to part with her.

THEN: One day, the monarch wanted to show off his strength and ordered everyone, even his wisemen, to create an "ash rope." The son followed his mother's advice and used a twisted straw to create the rope, which he then burned on a row of flat stones on a windless night.

Knowing that he had help, he told the monarch that his mother's age and wisdom had enabled him to accomplish such an impossible task.

Last but not least, the emperor initially imprisoned the guy but then changed his mind and quoted the proverb "With the crown of snow, there cometh wisdom! ”. As a result, the proclamation was revoked and the custom was discontinued.
