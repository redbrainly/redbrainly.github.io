12. Why must a mother's dna match that of her biological children? why would damien expect half of the children's dna to come from their mother?


A mother's DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) must match with her biological children because this contains her genetic information and will prove if the children is hers or not through several laboratory analysis.

Half DNA of a mom is passed on to her offspring. It needs to suit, but not 100%, since the DNA of the mother includes the genetic material of the children.

And since the sperm cell and egg cell have the same number of chromosomes, they produce the same amount of DNA, which is why half of children's DNA has to come from their mother, while the other half comes from their father.
