1. differentiate the holistic rubric and analytic rubric​


Holistic rubric is a single criterion rubric (one-dimensional) used to assess participants' overall achievement on an activity or item based on predefined achievement levels. On the other hand, analytic rubric is a two-dimensional rubric with levels of achievement as columns and assessment criteria as rows.

PROPERTIES OF A WELL WRITTEN TEXT

A writer should know the importance of the properties of a well-written text. Having a well-written text makes it easier for you to express your work to your readers. Listed below are the properties needed to achieve a well-written text.

Unity

Unity is achieved when a composition contains one focused idea. Having unity in your writing helps the readers to easily understand what the composition is about without having to re-read it.

Coherence and Cohesion

Coherence refers to how easy it is to understand the writing and cohesion is the connectivity of ideas in a text. When writing is coherent and cohesive, the readers will not get lost in the point the author is making because of the connected ideas in the text.

Organization

An organized piece of writing is clear and easy in the eyes of the readers.

Language Use

Using clear sentences and vocabularies that can be easily understood, having consistency in using pronouns, avoiding redundancies, wordiness is needed to achieve a well-written text.

13. analytic rubric/point system sample​


Answer:

An analytic rubric, also known as a point system, is a tool used to evaluate the quality of a student's work or performance. It typically consists of a set of criteria, or standards, that the work is evaluated against, and a corresponding set of points or scores that are assigned to each criterion.

In this example, the essay is being evaluated on five criteria: the introduction, body paragraphs, conclusion, grammar and spelling, and style and tone. Each criterion is assigned a certain number of points, and the total number of points possible is 50. The student's essay will be evaluated based on how well it meets each of the criteria, and the total number of points earned will be used to determine the final grade for the essay.

An analytic rubric can be useful for evaluating a wide range of work, including essays, research papers, presentations, and even artistic performances. It allows for a more detailed and nuanced evaluation of the work, as it allows the evaluator to consider multiple aspects of the work rather than just a single overall score.


14. What is Informative Essay, Analytical Essay, Persuasive Essay, and Argumentative Essay?​


Answer:

Informative Essay

This type of essay explains a short theme, idea, or an issue. It is a personal response to anything and is usually three paragraphs long.

Analytical Essay

This type of essay analyzes, interprets, or examines an art work, a film, a literary work, a song, a composition, or a media text. It is usually written in four parts:

a. the introduction tells you the what text you will be discussing and why;

b. the textual analysis which is closely related to your main argument or idea in the introduction;

c. your personal reading of the text that must show critically; and,

d. the conclusion which shows the connection between your argument and the text.

Persuasive Essay

This type of essay convinces a reader to believe or adopt a viewpoint on a certain issue. It is sometimes written in three to five paragraphs.

Argumentative Essay

This type of essay argues in a sense that it proves a hypothesis, a proposition, a theory, or an opinion to an opposite hypothesis, propositon, theory, idea, or opinion. It is usually writen in five paragraphs to show the pros and cons with claims and subpoints for each.

18. when do you use analytic rubrics


Answer:

Analytic rubrics are used to assess the quality of student work on a task or assignment. The rubric breaks down the task into several components or criteria and evaluates each one separately. This type of rubric provides a more detailed and nuanced assessment of the work than a holistic rubric, which evaluates the work as a whole based on a single criterion.

Analytic rubrics are commonly used in educational settings to assess student performance in areas such as writing, problem solving, critical thinking, and presentation skills. They are also used to evaluate performance in competitions, exhibitions, and other performance-based events.

In general, analytic rubrics are used when the task being evaluated is complex and requires the assessment of multiple skills, abilities, or aspects of the work. They allow teachers and evaluators to provide specific feedback on individual components of the work, which can help students improve their skills and achieve higher quality results.
