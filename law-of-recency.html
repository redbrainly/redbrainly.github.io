17. Discuss how stereotyping, attribution, self-fulfilling prophecy, halo, false-consensus, primacy, and recency effects influence the perceptual process


Stereotyping, attribution, self-fulfilling prophecy, halo, false-consensus, primacy, and recency effects are several things or factor that can influence the individu preceptual process.

Explanation :

Stereotyping

When we look at other people, stereotypes will influence our behavior. Stereotypes can lead to self-fulfilling prophecies. This creates a tendency to pay selective attention to environmental aspects and ignore information that is inconsistent with what we believe.

Attribution

Attribution is what happens when someone takes information they feel to determine the reason for what happened. Although it may be wrong for being unrealistic or having the wrong information for the situation.

Self-Fulfilling

Self-fulfilling prophecies are important for understanding a society's relationships. Under right (or wrong) conditions, inaccurate social stereotypes can lead to their own validation.

Halo

The halo effect can create a biased perception or judgment of quality. The way someone says "hello" can give an initial impression of that person's character.

False-Consensus’Primacy

It is an individual's tendency to overestimate the level of another's attitude or behavior. The false consensus effect is a type of biased judgment in which we think of an opinion, attitude or belief are common and appropriate, so that others should feel the same way.

Recency

The recency effect is the tendency to remember the most recently presented information.

18. If you will apply the idea of performance appraisal problems in the classroom. Which do you think your professor should avoid? Why? (Performance appraisal problems: Halo effect, Recency effect, Previous performance Bias, Recency and Strictness error, central tendency, Bias, and Carelessness)​


There are several performance appraisal problems that a professor should try to avoid in the classroom. These include:

Halo effect: This is the tendency to let one outstanding characteristic of a student influence the overall evaluation of their performance. For example, if a student is very outgoing and charismatic, a professor might be more likely to give them a positive evaluation even if their other work is not as strong.Recency effect: This is the tendency to let recent events or performance influence the overall evaluation of a student's work. For example, if a student does particularly well on the last assignment of the semester, a professor might be more likely to give them a higher evaluation overall, even if their earlier work was weaker.Previous performance SMGConline: This is the tendency to let a student's previous performance influence their current evaluation. For example, if a student has consistently performed well in the past, a professor might be more likely to give them a positive evaluation, even if their current work is not as strong.Recency and Strictness error: This is the tendency to be more strict and critical with recent work, while being more lenient with earlier work. This can lead to inconsistencies in evaluations and create unfairness among students.Central tendency: This is the tendency to give all students average or middle-of-the-road evaluations, even if their performance is significantly better or worse than average. This can be a result of the professor not wanting to single out any students for praise or criticism.Bias: This is the tendency to evaluate students based on factors that are unrelated to their performance, such as their gender, race, or socioeconomic background.Carelessness: This is the tendency to not put enough effort or thought into the evaluation process, resulting in evaluations that are not accurate or fair.

In general, it is important for a professor to be aware of these potential biases and to take steps to avoid them in order to ensure that their evaluations are accurate and fair. This may involve using multiple evaluators, using a standardized evaluation system, and regularly reviewing and adjusting the evaluation process to ensure its fairness and effectiveness.
