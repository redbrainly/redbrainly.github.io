13. what is an irregular nouns


Irregular noun is a noun whose plural form is not simply adding -s or -es.  The spelling of the noun changes in plural form, or stay the same.

Examples (Singular - Plural):
man - men
ox - oxen
child - children
sheep - sheep (no changes)
alumnus - alumni
radius - radii
axis - axes
parenthesis - parentheses

22. What are the irregular verbs?​


Answer:

Irregular verbs are verbs that don’t take on the regular –d, -ed, or -ied spelling patterns of the past simple (V2) or past participle (V3). Many of the irregular V2 and V3 forms are the same, such as: cut – cut, had – had, let – let, hurt – hurt, fed- fed, sold-sold.

Explanation:

Answer:

•A verb that does not follow the normal pattern of inflection.

•Irregular verbs are common verbs in the English language that do not follow the simple system of adding “d” or “ed” to the end of the word to form the past tense. ... Some irregular verbs follow patterns, such as drink–drank, spring–sprang, know-knew, and blow-blew but you can't use those patterns with predictability.
