It is a virtue that refers to knowledge about things to be done and things that need to be avoided.

According to traditional interpretations of his Nichomachean Ethics, Aristotle (384-322 B.C.E.) emphasizes the role of habit in behavior. Virtues are commonly supposed to be habits, and the virtuous life to be a mindless routine, as Aristotle claimed.

These ideas of Aristotle's ethics are the result of erroneous translations from the original Greek text. Aristotle coined the term hexis to express moral virtue. The phrase does not, however, only apply to passive habituation. Hexis, on the other hand, is a state in which anything must actively maintain its integrity.

As a result, the virtuous habit manifests in action. According to Aristotle, an action is virtuous when one maintains a constant soul equilibrium in order to choose the action.
