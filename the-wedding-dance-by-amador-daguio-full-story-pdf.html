Answer:

Awiyao and Lumnay were husband and wife for seven years, but now the husband has to marry another woman, Madulimay, because Lumnay was not able to give him a child. (In their culture in the mountains during those times, having a child to follow after the husband’s name was a must.)

Awiyao goes back to the wedding, to the wedding dance, after being fetched by some friends. Lumnay wants to follow, partly because of the dance, and partly because she wants to put a stop to their tribe’s tradition of having to marry another partner just to have a child.

"The Wedding Dance" by Amador Daguio is a poignant short story that uses various literary devices to create a powerful and memorable reading experience. Here are some of the literary devices used in the story:

Imagery: The story is full of vivid descriptions that paint a picture in the reader's mind. For example, the description of the moon as "a ghostly bride" creates a haunting image that foreshadows the story's tragic ending.

Symbolism: The story uses various symbols to represent deeper meanings. For example, the dance represents the couple's relationship, and the blood from the pig's throat represents the violence and sacrifice inherent in their cultural traditions.

Irony: The story employs situational irony to create contrast and highlight the characters' misguided decisions. For example, Awiyao's decision to leave Lumnay to find a new wife is ironic because it ultimately leads to his realization that he still loves her.

Foreshadowing: The story uses hints and clues to suggest what is to come. For example, the description of the bride in the moonlight foreshadows the tragedy that will occur at the end of the story.

Flashback: The story uses flashback to provide the reader with the backstory of Awiyao and Lumnay's relationship. This technique allows the reader to understand the depth of their connection and the significance of their cultural traditions.

Metaphor: The story uses metaphor to compare the characters and their actions to other things. For example, the dance is compared to a ritual, and the pig's blood is compared to the couple's emotional pain.

Overall, "The Wedding Dance" is a masterful piece of storytelling that uses literary devices to convey the complexity of human relationships, cultural traditions, and the struggle between individual desires and social expectations.

29. literary selection critique in the wedding dance by amador daguio ​


"The Wedding Dance" by Amador Daguio is a short story that explores the complexities of love and cultural traditions. The story follows Awiyao and Lumnay, a married couple from the Kalinga tribe in the Philippines, who are struggling to conceive a child. In an effort to fulfill his desire for a son, Awiyao decides to leave Lumnay and seek out another wife. The story depicts the emotional turmoil that both characters experience as they confront the reality of their situation.

One of the strengths of this story is its portrayal of the cultural practices and traditions of the Kalinga tribe. Daguio effectively captures the nuances of their customs and beliefs, such as the importance of fertility and the role of the dance in courtship. This attention to detail gives the story a sense of authenticity and depth.

Another strength of the story is the way it portrays the emotional complexity of the characters. Awiyao is torn between his love for Lumnay and his desire for a son, while Lumnay struggles with feelings of abandonment and heartbreak. These conflicting emotions are depicted with sensitivity and nuance, making the characters feel like real people with real struggles.

One potential weakness of the story is its abrupt ending. The story ends with Lumnay joining in the wedding dance of Awiyao and his new wife, leaving the reader to wonder about the characters' fates. While this open-endedness can be effective in some cases, it may leave some readers feeling unsatisfied or confused.

Overall, "The Wedding Dance" is a poignant and beautifully written story that explores the complexities of love, culture, and tradition. It is a story that resonates with readers on a universal level, even as it offers a window into a specific cultural context
