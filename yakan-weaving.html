Answer:

The Yakan people are among the major indigenous Filipino ethnolinguistic groups in the Sulu Archipelago.

Also known as dream weaver having a significant number of followers of Islam,it is considered one of the 13 Moro groups in the Philippines.

The Yakans mainly reside in Basilan but are also in Zamboanga City.

They speak a language known as Bissa Yakan, which has characteristics of both Sama-Bajau Sinama and Tausug (Jundam 1983: 7-8).

It is written in the Malayan Arabic script, with adaptations to sounds not present in Arabic (Sherfan 1976).
