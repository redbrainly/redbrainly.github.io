12. Interesting facts of Mayon, Taal volcano, Bulusan and Canlaon volcano​


Answer:

Mt.Mayon- Mayon Volcano, active volcano, southeastern Luzon, Philippines, dominating the city of Legaspi. Called the world's most perfect volcanic cone because of the symmetry of its shape, it has a base 80 miles (130 km) in circumference and rises to 8,077 feet (2,462 metres) from the shores of Albay Gulf.

Taal Volcano - The Taal volcano is located on a small island about 65 miles south of Manila, the capital of the Philippines. While the Taal volcano it is one of the world's smallest volcanoes, it is the Philippines' second most active volcano. It has recorded at least 34 eruptions in the past 450 years

Canlaon volcano - Canlaon volcano (also spelled Kanlaon) on Negros Island is one of the most active volcanoes of the Philippines and the most active one of the Central Philippines. It forms the highest peak of Negros Island and is located west of Canlaon about 36 km southeast of Bacolod City and belongs to Canlaon National Park.

Bulusan, Kanlaon, and Smith Eruptions

So far, the latest eruptions of Bulusan (2017), Kanlaon (2017), and Smith (1924) did not result to any fatalities. The last time someone died from the eruption of these volcanoes was in 1996, when three hikers were killed while climbing Kanlaon volcano. It suddenly had a phreatic eruption which killed the hikers.

What are phreatic eruptions?

Phreatic eruptions are a type of volcanic eruption that is steam-driven. It is dangerous because it can happen anytime. In 1996, three hikers were killed after Kanlaon erupted, spewing extremely hot volcanic materials which immediately led to fatalities.

What makes a volcano deadly?

The extrusion of hot materials from a volcanic vent can burn and kill someone. In addition, related hazards to volcanic eruptions such as ash fall, debris avalanche, pyroclastic density currents, and lahars can also contribute to the number of deaths.
