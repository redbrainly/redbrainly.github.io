17. meaning of nailing a coffin in song of autumn


The poem 'Song of Autumn', originally written in French as 'Chant d'automne' is a poem telling about people's fear of the coming winter. In the poem, the 'coming of winter' is the euphemistic phrase given to death. Thus, the poem is actually about people's fear of death.


The fourth stanza in the first verse of the poem states;

      It seems to me, lulled by these monotonous shocks,

     That somewhere they're nailing a coffin, in great haste.

     For whom? — Yesterday was summer; here is autumn

    That mysterious noise sounds like a departure.


The poet was in a wonder why there are some people who nails the coffin in a hurry. If you try to transcribe it, the poet is actually saddened in wonder that there are some people who want to die early. Somehow he tries to comfort the stanza by saying "Yesterday was summer; here is autumn", which means we should treasure every moment from the past and the present because we won't be able to know when we will depart from life.

25. what the characteristics of tutankhamens inner coffin ?


The Egyptians strongly believe of the importance of the journey to the afterlife. Prominent people, like Pharaohs are buried with riches and their tombs are covered in spells to assure that their trip will be smooth and easy.
