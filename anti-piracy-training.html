3. REPUBLIC ACT NO. 8293 is also known as the World Anti-Piracy Observatory


REPUBLIC ACT NO. 8293 is also known as the World Anti-Piracy Observatory → TRUE

RA 8293 refers to the Republic Act 8293. It contains various sections:

Section 1: The act's title is found. It is recognized as the Philippines' intellectual property code.

Section 2: The second section of this act addresses the Declaration of State Policy. This Act is intended to preserve and secure the unique intellectual property rights of scientists, investors, artists, and other talented persons.

Section 3: The third section of this statute addresses international conventions and reciprocity. Any individual who is a national of, or has a real and effective industrial establishment in, a country that is a party to the convention.

Section 4 concerns the rights to intellectual property. It consists of industrial designs, patents, trademarks, copyright, and associated services.

Section 5 contains the intellectual property office's responsibilities.

Section 6 includes the intellectual property office's organizational structure.

Section 7 includes the roles of the Director General and Deputy Directors General are outlined in.

Section 8 outlines the Bureau of Patents's responsibilities.

Section 9 includes the Bureau of Trademarks as the subject.

Section 10: Legal Affairs Bureau.

This was some information regarding intellectual property. Next, the second section covers patent law and patentability. The next sections discuss patent rights, patent applications, patent grant procedures, etc.

13. Differentiate plagiarism and intellectual piracy


Plagiarism is the illegal act of taking other’s words or ideas as your own instead of giving the credit to the real owner.  This is done to gain honor.

Usually, plagiarism takes place among students who wish to accomplish their literary assignments in the most convenient way.  The easy access to the internet can provide them with good essays that is used for their term paper and the like.

On the other hand, intellectual piracy is the illegal act of copying other’s works for reselling or redistribution in a much lower price.  As opposed to plagiarism, this involves earning of money by using other’s works.

One of the very common example is the free downloading of movies, music, and books online.  It seems free but the website earns from the advertisements.  And the reselling of pirated CDs or DVDs along the walkways.

18. The _____ lists 15 types of works that are covered by copyright protection. Select one: a. Intellectual Property Code of the Philippines b. Anti-Piracy Law of the Philippines c. World Anti-Piracy Observatory d. Copyright Protection Act


The correct answers is B. Anti-Piracy Law of the Philippines.

The Anti-Piracy Law of the Philippines lists 15 types of works that are covered by copyright protection.

EXPLANATION:

1. In 1982

Piracy and Counterfeiting Amendments Act. This is about increasing criminal penalties for infringing records, ando penalty of jail.

2. In 1984

Record Rental Amendment. This outlawed music rentals (have you ever wondered why there were no Blockbusters or Netflixes for music?)

3. In 1990

Copyright Remedy Clarification Act. This is allowed copyright holders to sue the states for the copyright infringement.

4. In 1990

Computer Software Rental Amendments Act. The outlawed software rentals.

5. In 1992

Audio Home Recording Act: Mandatingon certain digital audio devices, it is added a royalty on like devices.

6. In 1994

Uruguay Round Agreements Act. It seize works out of the public domain and putting them from copyright. It made it a criminal offense to bootleg concerts (video or audio).

7. In 1995

The Digital Performance Right in Sound Recordings Act. It is created for copyright holders concerning digital “performances.”

8. In 1996

Anticounterfeiting Consumer Protection Act of 1996. This Expanded racketeering laws to include criminal copyright infringement, as well as “trafficking” in documentation or packaging, computer software, as well as trafficking in movies or audiovisual works.

9. In 1997

No Electronic Theft (NET) Act. (such as taking out the monetary profit requirement). Which means decreasing the threshold for what counts as criminal infringement

10. In 1998

Sonny Bono Copyright Term Extension Act. Expanded copyright terms  about 20 years.

11. In 1998

DMCA /Digital Millennium Copyright Act. Again, we may have heard of it. Created the notice-and-takedown system and anti-circumvention rules   for online infringement, between many things.

12. In 1999

Digital Theft Deterrence and Copyright Damages Improvement Act. This might massively increased statutory damages for infringement.

13. In 2004

Intellectual Property Protection and Courts Amendments Act. To set up penalties especially for civil and criminal for counterfeit labels, documentation and packaging in association with copyrighted goods (yes, separate from the content itself).

14. In 2005

Family Entertainment and Copyright Act. It criminalizes lets theaters detain people solely suspected of recording in theaters and recording of movies in theaters. Criminalizes releasing a work online before it's been officially released (if it's “prepared” for commercial things).

15. In 2008

It is prioritizing the Organization and Resources for the Intellectual Property. Increasing government seizures & forfeiture powers (which is how the government currently justifies its questionable domain seizures) and created a job in United States.
