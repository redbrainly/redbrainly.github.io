Allow students to interact with one another to develop critical thinking and use their mobile gadgets for the academic purposes.​


Explanation:

Strong relationships between teachers and students can be very beneficial for academic performance. A positive relationship can create a supportive and engaging learning environment, which can lead to increased motivation, engagement, and achievement.

Providing computers to schools can certainly improve technology skills for both teachers and students. In today's world, technology skills are becoming increasingly important for success in many fields, so providing access to computers and other technology resources can help students prepare for the future.

It's important for students to use mobile gadgets in a responsible and appropriate way. This may include using them for academic purposes and following guidelines for their use in the classroom or school.

Giving students time to improve their performance while using mobile gadgets can be helpful, as it allows them to explore and learn at their own pace. It's also important for students to have a balance between using technology and other types of learning activities, such as hands-on projects or group discussions.

Allowing students to interact with one another and use their mobile gadgets for academic purposes can be a great way to develop critical thinking skills. Collaboration and communication are important skills in many fields, and using mobile gadgets can be a useful tool for facilitating this type of learning. It's important to set clear guidelines for how mobile gadgets should be used in this context to ensure that they are being used effectively and responsibly.

13. what are the reason for not allowing student to use mobile phones in plants when they are not read by the teacher​


Answer: Let me begin this essay by making two points quite clear. First, I am not suggesting that any other professors follow my practice of taking students’ cellphones in class. Second, and related to that, professors have a wide variety of reasons to want students to have their cellphones.

Some believe that students should learn to self-regulate (or even sink or swim, depending on one’s perspective), often reminding them that “this isn’t high school.” Others find that students can and do use cellphones in productive ways in their courses -- whether by looking up information and resources on the spot or through live Twitter feeds, to name a few.

Explanation: That said, something changed in my classes and me that has led me to take away students’ cellphones. For years, I did what many professors do: I made vague warnings about cellphones, explaining to students that their distraction would cause them to do worse in the course than if they were more focused. At times, I became so bothered that I would call students out during class, though I would usually try to say something to them away from most students, often when they were in groups doing work. But one event caused me to finally adjust my approach completely.

This verse highlights the importance of keeping information confidential, and it can be used to address the misconduct of Jake sharing the linkages to his friends without teacher Mary's permission.

Additionally, sharing personal information without permission can also be considered as an invasion of privacy and respect on teacher Mary's decision.
