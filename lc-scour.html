It is a small pad of metal or plastic mesh used for scouring a surface?

A scouring pad, also known as a scourer, is a small metal or plastic mesh pad used to scour a surface. One side of some scouring places is formed of a soft sponge-like material, while the other comprises the mesh discussed before. Over the years, the scouring pad has been resurrected in several ways. In 1928, R. B. Kingman patented the scouring ball, which was one of the first metal mesh scouring pads. On September 30, 1941, Joseph R. Crockford of Chicago, IL received the US patent 2,257,456 for a scouring device for "cleansing and scouring pots, pans, and other household implements." The invention included a "plate of non-corrosive metal, arched or curved to fit the fingers." David J. Kelman patented a toroidal metal scouring pad in 1942. This was and is still used as an abrasive scouring pad; however, it is becoming less popular because it removes non-stick surfaces from cookware.[1] Alfred Benjamin patented the stainless-steel scouring pad on June 19, 1962 (US patent 3,039,125).
