1. The Ifugao’s wedding with the festival dance is called ____.


Uyaoy Dance is an Ifugao wedding festival dance accompanied by gongs and is performed by the affluent to attain the second level of the wealthy class. Wealthy people who have performed this dance are entitled to the use of gongs at their death.

5. A courtship dance of the Ifugaos, that shows imitation of fowls.


The tribes from the Philippines usually have rituals and celebrations as part of their culture. They have it for different purposes and dancing is an activity that plays a huge role and can never be disregarded.

The Ifugaos from Banaue are famous for their large feasts called the Cañaos. There are various types like for harvests, success and death but the one for courtship is called “Talip”, in which the steps resemble those of the fowls.

Answer:

Monogamy is the norm, but the wealthy sometimes practice polygyny. The incest prohibition extends to first cousins; more distant cousins may be married only on payment of livestock penalties. Ifugao courtship takes place in the girls' houses (agamang).

Marriage among the Ifugao is traditionally arranged by parents and elders of the community. The Ifugao wedding is a well–established ritual in Chaja interwoven with traditional ceremonies and rites and requires material and financial offerings in the observance of the ceremonies.

21. What dance among the ifugaos imitates the high flying birds?


The dance is called Lumagen or Tachok, which is a Kalingan festival dance.  

The Ifugao tribe in Kalinga, specifically the maidens, perform this dance during events of happy celebrations such as births, weddings, and peace pacts. The music accompanying the dance comprises of gongs or gangsa. The dance has a moving characteristic of high flying birds.
