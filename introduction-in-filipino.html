Answer & Explanation:

Introduction:

The Philippines is one of the fastest growing economies in Southeast Asia, yet poverty remains a persistent challenge. Despite efforts to improve the standard of living for Filipinos, the economic outlook for 2023 suggests that poverty among the population will only continue to worsen.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the future of poverty in the Philippines in 2023 is bleak. With rising costs of living, high levels of unemployment, and an inadequate social safety net, many Filipinos will find it increasingly difficult to make ends meet. Without significant policy interventions and a more robust response from the government, the number of poor Filipinos is likely to increase, leaving a growing number of families struggling to access basic necessities such as food, housing, and healthcare. While it is important to acknowledge the progress that has been made in recent years, much more work is needed to ensure that all Filipinos have a chance at a better future.

29. what is the introduction of filipino food culture​


Answer:

The origins of Filipino food lie with the Malayo-Polynesians, who were responsible for its most common ingredient: rice. Around 3200 BC, they settled in the Philippines and brought farming and cooking methods that included steaming, boiling, and roasting over a fire.
