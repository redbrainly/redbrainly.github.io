19. blue eyes are recessive in human while brown eyes are dominant what is probably that a child of a blue eye father and homozygous brown eyed mother can have blue eyes? ​


Answer:

In the given scenario (one parent is heterozygous for brown and blue eye alleles and one is homozygous for blue eyes), their child has a 50% chance of having blue eyes.

Explanation:

For the eye color to be blue, alleles from both parents have to be 'b' & 'b' In this case, eye color of the progeny will be always brown. Hence, there is zero percent chances of blue eye color in the progeny.
