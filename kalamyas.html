5. Jose was exploring the library in his new school in Manila. He wanted to study the history of Calamba, Laguna during the nineteenth century. In one of the books, he saw an old photograph of a woman standing in front of an old church, clipped among the pages. At the back of the photo was a fine inscription that says: “Kalamba, 19 de Junio 1861”.Is the photograph a primary, secondary, or a tertiary source? Explain.​


The old photograph of a woman standing in front of an old church that Jose found in a book is considered as the primary source. Why was it a primary source? It is considered a primary source because photographs document the setting, the history of the place, people, community, and other things during a certain period of time. It showed the history of Calamba, Laguna during the 19th century.  The year 1861 was under the 19th century since the 19th century began in 1801 up to 1900.

Documents, photographs, or artifacts that provide personal witness or direct proof about a historical issue under inquiry are referred to as primary sources. Original materials, regardless of format, are considered primary sources.  

Examples of primary sources letters diaries minutes pictures artifacts interviews sound or video recordings
