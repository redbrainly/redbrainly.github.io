Answer:

The most characteristic features of critical reading are that you will: examine the evidence or arguments presented; check out any influences on the evidence or arguments; ... decide to what extent you are prepared to accept the authors' arguments, opinions, or conclusions.

11. What I Have LearnedCheck your understanding of the lesson by completing the following statements:What is a counterclaim?OWhat is the difference between claim and counterclaim?o What are the four parts of an argument?o What does rebut mean?OWhat is a characteristic of a good critical reader?​


Answer:

• What is a counterclaim?

A counterclaim is an argument (or a set of reasons) that contradicts your claim.

• What is the difference between claim and counterclaim?

The primary argument is a claim. The contrary of the argument, or the opposing argument, is called a counterclaim.

• What are the four parts of an argument?

claims, counterclaims, reasons, and evidence are the four parts of an argument.

• What does rebut mean?

It implies to dispute or debunk, especially by presenting a counter-argument or dispute.

• What is a characteristic of a good critical reader?

Critical readers comprehend facts, get a better knowledge of concepts that link to specifics, assess concepts, and make informed conclusions.
