7. What is the influence of the Art of cultures 200 CE - 1400 CE on the present?


Answer:

The Medieval Period (400 CE-1400 CE)

In contrast to the Classical Period, which saw tremendous growth and innovation in the study of communication, the Medieval Period might be considered the dark ages of academic study in our field. During this era, the Greco-Roman culture was dominated by Christian influence after the fall of the Roman Empire. The church felt threatened by secular rhetorical works they considered full of pagan thought. While the church preserved many of the classical teachings of rhetoric, it made them scarce to those not in direct service to the church. A secular education was extremely hard to obtain during the Medieval Period for almost everyone.

Even though Christianity condemned communication study as pagan and corrupt, it embraced several aspects of the Classical Period to serve its specific purposes. The ideas from the Classical Period were too valuable for the church to completely ignore. Thus, they focused on communication study to help them develop better preaching and letter writing skills to persuade people to Christianity. Emphasis was placed on persuasion and developing public presentations, both oral and written. Like the Classical Period, those in power continued to stifle women’s participation in communication study, keeping them largely illiterate while men served as the overseers of the church and the direction of academic inquiry.

One of the most recognizable people from this era was Augustine (354 CE-430 CE), a Christian clergyman and renowned rhetorician who actually argued for the continued development of ideas that had originated during the Classical Period. He thought that the study of persuasion, in particular, was a particularly worthwhile pursuit for the church. Augustine was a teacher by trade and used his teaching skills as well as knowledge of communication to move “men” toward truth, which for him was the word of God (Baldwin).

With the exception of Augustine, the formal study of communication took a back seat to a focus on theological issues during the Medieval Period. Fortunately, the study of communication managed to survive as one of the seven branches of a liberal education during this period, but it remained focused on developing presentational styles apt for preaching. Boethius and the Archbishop Isidore of Seville made small efforts to preserve classical learning by reviving the works of Cicero and Quintilian to persuade people to be just and good. Nevertheless, aside from Augustine’s work, little progress was made during the remaining Medieval years; the formal study of communication literally plunged into the “dark ages” before reemerging during the Renaissance.

15. Does rostow theory of economic releva ce in providing answer to Africa problems


Answer:

The Rostow model, also known as the Rostovian take-off model or the stages of growth model, is a theory of economic development that outlines the stages that a country goes through as it develops from a traditional, agrarian society to a modern, industrial society. The model was developed by economist Walt Whitman Rostow in the 1950s and 1960s and has been influential in shaping development policies in many countries, including in Africa.

According to the Rostow model, there are five stages of economic growth: the traditional society, the preconditions for take-off, the take-off, the drive to maturity, and the age of high mass consumption. In the traditional society stage, a country is characterized by a low level of productivity and a narrow base of economic activity. In the preconditions for take-off stage, the country begins to experience economic growth and diversification, as well as an increase in the availability of capital and an improvement in education and health. The take-off stage is marked by a rapid increase in productivity and economic growth, as the country begins to industrialize and build a modern infrastructure. The drive to maturity stage is characterized by a continuing expansion of the economy and the development of a more complex and diverse economic structure. Finally, in the age of high mass consumption, the country reaches a high level of economic development and a high standard of living for its citizens.

While the Rostow model has been influential in understanding economic development and providing insights into the challenges facing developing countries, it has also been criticized for its linear and deterministic approach to development and its assumption that all countries will follow the same path to economic growth. Some have argued that the model does not adequately take into account the unique social, political, and cultural factors that shape economic development in different countries, particularly in Africa.

25. Ces is 15 years older than his cousim Mar. in ten years Ces will be twice as old as Mar. How old is Ces now?


Ces is 20 years old now.

Step-by-step explanation:

Age Word Problem

Age word problems refer to problems that deal with the ages of people currently, in the past or in the future.

Let us analyze the problem.

The problem above is asking for Ces' age now.

Let "x" be Mar's age.

Given:

Age Now:

"Ces is 15 years older than his cousim Mar."

Mar - x

Ces - x + 15

In 10 years:

"Ces will be twice as old as Mar."

Mar - x + 10

Ces - 2(x + 10) or x + 15 + 10

Equation:

2(x + 10) = x + 15 + 10

Solution:

2(x + 10) = x + 15 + 10

2x + 20 = x + 25

2x - x = 25 - 20

x = 5

Mar is 5 years old now.

Let us now find Ces' age. Plug in Mar's age.

x + 15

= 5 + 15

= 20

Final Answer:

Ces is 20 years old now.
