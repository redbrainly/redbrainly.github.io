7. Compare social science from natural science and humanities.​Definition: ?Sub-Branches: ? Methods of Inquiry: ?Professions: ?​


Answer:

Definition-Social science as a field of study is separate from the natural sciences, which cover topics such as physics, biology, and chemistry. Social science examines the relationships between individuals and societies, as well as the development and operation of societies, rather than studying the physical world. These academic disciplines rely more heavily on interpretation and qualitative research methodologies.

Sub-Branches-Natural science can be divided into two main branches: life science and physical science. Life science is alternatively known as biology, and physical science is subdivided into branches: physics, chemistry, Earth science, and astronomy.

Methods of Inquiry-The scientific method, as applied to social sciences, includes a variety of research approaches, tools, and techniques, for collecting and analyzing qualitative or quantitative data. These methods include laboratory experiments, field surveys, case research, ethnographic research, action research, and so forth.

Professions-Humanities focus mostly on the tradition, culture, and heritage of the society on how human beings are developed. On the other hand, Social Science critically studies the factual differences between Humanities and Pure Sciences.

29. What branch of public health is concerned with all aspects of the natural and built environment affecting human health?


Environmental health is the area of public health that deals with how the built and natural environments affect people's health.

The area of public health known as environmental health is focused on observing or reducing environmental elements that have an impact on human health and disease.

Environmental epidemiology, toxicology, exposure science, environmental engineering, and environmental law are the five fundamental disciplines that most commonly make contributions to the topic of environmental health.

The area of public health known as "environmental health" is concerned with how people and their environment interact, as well as how to encourage safe and healthy communities.

Municipalities and the health department are in charge of things like garbage management and hygiene. River contamination is a concern for the departments of agriculture, water, and tourism as well as the environment.

30. What is the branch of philosophy that studies the nature and means of human knowledge?​


Epistemology is the branch of philosophy that considers how people come to learn what they know. Derived from the Greek word episteme, meaning knowledge or understanding, epistemology refers to the nature and origin of knowledge and truth.
