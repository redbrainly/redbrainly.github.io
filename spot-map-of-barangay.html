14. Why are latitude and longitude included on maps? It is the simplest way to give directions to travelers. It is a way to determine the exact location of any spot in the world. It is a tradition that mapmakers have been using for hundreds of years. It is an easy method to find the location of one spot relative to another spot.


Answer:

D. It is an easy method to find the location of one spot relative to another spot.

Explanation:

Maps and globes usually have lines on them to help locate places on Earth. These lines are called latitude and longitude lines. These lines are not actually on the planet, but are imaginary lines used to help us find our way around the curved surface of Earth. Lines of latitude and longitude crisscross to form a grid.
