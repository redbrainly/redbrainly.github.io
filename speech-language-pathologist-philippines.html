1. Speech language pathologist


Answer:

Speech-language pathologists (sometimes called speech therapists) assess, diagnose, treat, and help to prevent communication and swallowing disorders in children and adults. Speech, language, and swallowing disorders result from a variety of causes, such as a stroke, brain injury, hearing loss, developmental delay, Parkinson’s disease, a cleft palate, or autism.

29. Pathologists specialize in different diseases that may affect individuals. If you are a pathologist, how do you think the compound microscope will be helpful in your chosen field? explain your answer. if you help me, i'll give you a lot of points and i'll mark you the brainliest​


Answer:

A pathologist helps in the identification of the cause and in the diagnosis of a medical condition through the scientific examination of minute samples of body issues and specimen through the use of a microscope. A compound microscope greatly magnified the specimen, which allows the pathologist to identify the abnormalities of the cell structure and growth pattern in cancer, presence of a specific type of bacteria in urinary tract infection, identification of the specific strain of bacteria present in the bloodstream for antibiotic therapy in sepsis. The cellular changes and identifying characteristics of pathogens are too small for the naked eye to see but a compound microscope bends and refracts light that allows the pathologist to see these minuscule changes despite their size, paving way for an accurate diagnosis and treatment recommendations for the patient.
