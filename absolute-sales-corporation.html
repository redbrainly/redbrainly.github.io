What Is a Conditional Sales Agreement?

A conditional sales agreement is a financing arrangement where a buyer takes possession of an asset, but its title and right of repossession remain with the seller until the purchase price is paid in full.

The purchaser can take possession of the property as soon as the agreement is in force, but does not own the property until they have fully paid for it, which is usually done in installments. If the business defaults on its payments, the seller will repossess the item.

Conditional sales agreements are often put in place during the financing of machinery and equipment, as well as various forms of real estate.

FORMS OF BUSINESS ORGANIZATION

A business organization is an entity or institution formed for the purpose of having profit. There are four main forms of business organizations:  

sole proprietorship,partnership,corporation, and  cooperative. Sole Proprietorship

A sole proprietorship is a business organization that has only one owner. It has low capital since the source of funds is from one person only. Sales would be lower because it is the smallest form of business organization.

Partnership

A partnership is a business entity with two or more owners. The owners are called partners. They pool resources to a common fund for the purpose of earning profit. Its form is bigger than a sole proprietorship but smaller than a corporation.

Corporation

A corporation is a business organization that has multiple owners. The owners may be individuals or other corporations. It is the biggest of all the forms. The basic structure and characteristics of a corporation are discussed here: https://brainly.ph/question/219852  

Cooperative

A cooperative is a special business organization that is owned and managed by its members. Cooperatives provide benefits and services to their members.
