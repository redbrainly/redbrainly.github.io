3. Margie is three times older than Lilet. In 15 years the sum of their age is 38 years Find the present age?


AGE PROBLEM is oftentimes the most common worded problem in algebra that are sometimes so easy and sometimes so difficult to answer.

One type of worded problem that is similar to age problem is the NUMBER PROBLEM- in a way how they are being stated. But age problem is totally different from other worded problems for it needs an understanding of how the PAST, PRESENT and FUTURE work.

One technique to solve age problems easily is to make a table of the past, present and future and compare the given in all the timelines.

Refer to the table provided. See attached photo.

As presented, notice that only the present and the future were used. That is because the problem only tells about the present and the future.

Note that we let “M” as Margie’s age and “L” as Lilet’s age.

To avoid confusion, we take the present as the reference. That is we make the present ages as the single variables (table shows).

When the problem says before/after “x” years, we have to take note that we should subtract/add the corresponding years to the original ages. Do not proceed directly to making any equations because you have to understand first what the number says about their future ages, or else you’ll substitute the wrong value.

Therefore, after 15 years, their ages will become “M+15” and “L+15”

But note that the problem says the sum of their ages after 15 years is 38. So if we add the future ages of Margie and Lilet, we get

(M+15) + (L+15) = 38 (eq. 1)

However, we need to convert the equation into one variable equations. So we make use of the given that Margie is 3 times older than Lilet. The phrase is actually referring to their present ages.

Then we have

M = 3L (eq. 2)

Substitute eq. 2 to eq. 1 to get

[(3L) + 15] + (L+15) = 38

4L + 30 = 38

4L = 38-30

4L = 8

L = 2

M = 3L = 3(2) = 6

Therefore, Margie’s present age is 6, and Lilet’s present age is 2.

To check, just substitute the values to eq. 1. If it equates to 38, the it is correct.

18. Direction:Read and understand the following problem and solve it. show your steps on your answer sheet.AGE PROBLEM:Margie is 3 times older than Lilet. In 15 years, the sum of their ages is 38 years .FIND THEIR PRESENT AGES.​


✒️AGE PROBLEMS

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \rm Margie \: is \: 6 \: years \: old [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \rm Lilet \: is \: 2 \: years \: old [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

Let x and y be the present ages of Margie and Lilet, respectively. Make two equations based on the statements.

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3y \\ \small (x+15)+(y+15) = 38 \end{cases} \quad \begin{align} \tt{(eq. \: 1)} \\ \tt{(eq. \: 2)} \end{align}[/tex]

Substitute x in the second equation from the first equation in terms of y.

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3y \\ (3y+15)+(y+15) = 38 \end{cases} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3y \\ 3y+15+y+15 = 38 \end{cases} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3y \\ 4y + 30 = 38 \end{cases} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3y \\ 4y = 38 - 30 \end{cases} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3y \\ 4y = 8 \end{cases} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3y \\ 4y/4 = 8/4 \end{cases} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3y \\ y = 2 \end{cases} [/tex]

Therefore, the value of y is 2. Substitute it to the first equation to find the value of x.

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 3(2) \\ y = 2 \end{cases} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases} x = 6 \\ y = 2 \end{cases} [/tex]

Therefore, Margie's present age is 6 years and Lilet's present age is 2 years.
