2. What is the mass in kilograms of a 30ml substance if the density is 2.125 g/ml?


Answer:

M = 0.06375 kg

Explanation:

The formula for getting the mass

[tex]M=DV[/tex]

Given:

D = 2.125 g/ml

V = 30ml

Plugging the given to the formula

[tex]M=(2.125\frac{g}{ml} )(30ml)[/tex] (the ml will be cancelled)

[tex]M=(2.125g)(30)\\M=63.75g[/tex]

Convert it to kilograms. 1000g = 1kg

[tex]63.75g(\frac{1kg}{1000g} )=0.06375kg[/tex]

The formula for solving volume is V = m/d

Now we can divide 8.92g/mL by 5000g.
