CONGRUENT ANGLES

[tex]{\underline{\underline{\bold{Directions:}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{In the figure, m$\angle$ADB is four times the measure of}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{m$\angle$BDC . Find the measure of each.}[/tex]

==============================

[tex]{\underline{\bold{Solution:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{\small{Notice that $\angle$ADB and $\angle$BDC}}\\\textsf{\small{form a linear pair, they must be supplementary.}}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{\small{Let x be the measure of $\angle$BDC.}}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{\small{If $\angle$ADB is four times the measure of $\angle$BDC,}}\\\textsf{\small{then $\angle$ADB = 4x.}}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{Thus, we have}\\\sf{m\angle ADB + m\angle BDC = 180^{o} }\\\sf{4x+x=180^{o}}\\\sf{5x=180^{o}}\\\sf{x=180\div 5 }\\\sf{x = 36^{o}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\angle ADB = 4x}\\\sf{x=36}\\\sf{4(36)}\\\sf{\angle ADB=144^{o}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\angle BDC=36^{o}}[/tex]
