1. literary analysis of noli me tangere​


Answer:

Noli Me Tangere is a novel written by Filipino polymath Jose Rizal, and it is considered a landmark in Philippine literature. The novel was originally written in Spanish and was first published in 1887. The title Noli Me Tangere is a Latin phrase which means "Touch Me Not" and is taken from the Gospel of St. John 20:17, where Jesus says to Mary Magdalene after his resurrection.

The novel is a social commentary on the injustices, corruption, and ignorance present in Filipino society during the Spanish colonial period. Through its characters and their experiences, the novel presents a vivid picture of the struggles of the Filipino people, as well as the conflicts between the Spanish colonizers and the native population.

One of the key themes in Noli Me Tangere is the hypocrisy and corruption of the Spanish colonial government and the Catholic Church. The character of Padre Damaso serves as a symbol of the oppressive and corrupt religious establishment, while Padre Salvi represents the hypocrisy and greed of the Spanish colonial administration.

Another important theme in the novel is the importance of education and enlightenment. The character of Maria Clara represents the oppressed and uneducated masses, while the character of Elias represents the enlightened and educated few. Through their experiences, the novel highlights the need for education and enlightenment in order to break free from the shackles of ignorance and oppression.

The novel also explores the theme of love and sacrifice, as seen in the characters of Maria Clara and Elias. Their tragic story is a poignant reminder of the sacrifices that must be made in the pursuit of justice and freedom.

In conclusion, Noli Me Tangere is a powerful literary work that sheds light on the injustices and inequalities present in Filipino society during the Spanish colonial period. Through its vivid characters, engaging story, and insightful commentary, the novel remains a relevant and important work of Philippine literature to this day.

8. create a maximum of 7 paragraph analysis of the novel Noli Me Tangere. critical evaluation​


Answer:

Noli Me Tangere, written by Jose Rizal, is a novel that was published in Berlin in 1887. The novel was a groundbreaking work that exposed the corruption, injustices, and inhumanity of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines. Rizal wrote the novel as a way to inspire change and promote nationalism among Filipinos. The novel is considered a classic of Philippine literature and is widely read in the Philippines and among Filipinos around the world.

The novel tells the story of a young Filipino man named Juan Crisostomo Ibarra, also known as "Juanito," who returns to the Philippines after studying in Europe. Ibarra is shocked to find that the conditions in the Philippines have worsened since he left. He discovers that the Spanish friars and colonial officials are exploiting the native population and suppressing their rights. Ibarra becomes involved in a movement to reform the injustices in the Philippines, but his efforts are met with resistance from the Spanish authorities.

One of the main themes of Noli Me Tangere is the corruption and injustices of the Spanish colonial regime in the Philippines. Through the eyes of Juanito, the reader is able to see the cruelty and exploitation of the Spanish friars and colonial officials. The novel also shows how the friars and colonial officials use their power and influence to maintain their hold on the Philippines. They manipulate the law and the Catholic Church to control the native population and maintain their power.

Another important theme in Noli Me Tangere is the power of education. Juanito, who was educated in Europe, returns to the Philippines with new ideas and a desire to bring change to the country. He becomes involved in a movement to reform the injustices in the Philippines and promote nationalism among the native population. The novel shows how education can be a powerful tool for change and how it can help to awaken people to the injustices around them.

The novel also explores the theme of religion. The Spanish friars use religion as a tool to control the native population and maintain their power. They manipulate the beliefs of the native population to maintain their control over the Philippines. The novel shows how religion can be used for good or evil and how it can be a force for change or a means of oppression.

The character of Maria Clara, Juanito's childhood friend and fiancée, is also an important aspect of the novel. Maria Clara is a symbol of the oppressed and suffering Filipino people. She is suffering from tuberculosis, a symbol of the suffering of the Philippines under Spanish rule. Her illness and death are a testament to the cruel conditions in the Philippines and the injustices of Spanish rule.

In conclusion, Noli Me Tangere is a powerful and thought-provoking novel that exposes the corruption, injustices, and inhumanity of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines. The novel is a call to action for Filipinos to awaken to the injustices around them and work towards change. The themes of corruption, the power of education, religion, and oppression are still relevant today, making Noli Me Tangere a timeless work of literature.
