3. describe briefly your business rice breeds​


Rice is the seed of the grass species Oryza sativa (Asian rice) or less commonly Oryza glaberrima (African rice). As a cereal grain, it is the most widely consumed staple food for a large part of the world's human population, especially in Asia and Africa. It is the agricultural commodity with the third-highest worldwide production (rice, 741.5 million tonnes in 2014), after sugarcane (1.9 billion tonnes) and maize (1.0 billion tonnes).[1]

Oryza sativa with small wind-pollinated flowers

Since sizable portions of sugarcane and maize crops are used for purposes other than human consumption, rice is the most important food crop with regard to human nutrition and caloric intake, providing more than one-fifth of the calories consumed worldwide by humans.[2] There are many varieties of rice and culinary preferences tend to vary regionally.

 

Rice can come in many shapes, colors and sizes.

Rice, a monocot, is normally grown as an annual plant, although in tropical areas it can survive as a perennial and can produce a ratoon crop for up to 30 years.[3] Rice cultivation is well-suited to countries and regions with low labor costs and high rainfall, as it is labor-intensive to cultivate and requires ample water. However, rice can be grown practically anywhere, even on a steep hill or mountain area with the use of water-controlling terrace systems. Although its parent species are native to Asia and certain parts of Africa, centuries of trade and exportation have made it commonplace in many cultures worldwide.

Oryza sativa, commonly known as Asian rice

The traditional method for cultivating rice is flooding the fields while, or after, setting the young seedlings. This simple method requires sound irrigation planning but reduces the growth of less robust weed and pest plants that have no submerged growth state, and deters vermin. While flooding is not mandatory for the cultivation of rice, all other methods of irrigation require higher effort in weed and pest control during growth periods and a different approach for fertilizing the soil.

The name wild rice is usually used for species of the genera Zizania and Porteresia, both wild and domesticated, although the term may also be used for primitive or uncultivated varieties of Oryza.

Answer:

Western business is similar to hunting while on the east, it is more like planting rice. Is this attributed to space, time, touch, or sound?

When someone compares western business to hunting and eastern practice to planting rice I think he or she is mostly hinting on time so the answer would be the letter (b).

Explanation:

There is a big difference between the western system in running a business and it is understandable why it is compared to hunting, and this can be seen throughout history. While western companies are mostly determined to make the most profit by any means, business in the East is usually done through just plain old manufacturing of what is basically needed by many and doing it at a low price, hence the comparison to planting rice.  

The western practice of doing business:

They pursue monopoly They create needs  They have a more aggressive approach to business Playing fair is not in the books

The western business value monopoly to assure success, this is evident through their years of practicing imperialism around the world. In the past, when a certain resource is available in great abundance in one part of the world, they will then make laws to legitimize claiming or conquering the nation. This is done with the use of propaganda that targets their own people and everyone else to assure continued support. In many cases, there is a collusion between corporations and politicians in the form of bribes and lobbying.  

The eastern practice of doing business:

Manufacturing Retailing

Most businessmen from the east just go through these steps daily:

Manufacture  Sell Save Invest

Here at least in the Philippines, it is normal to see an old business establishment like a hardware supply store, a bakery, an old factory that manufactures something behind its walls. Most of us have memories of an elder Chinese man and his family sitting behind the front of the store or standing outside of his establishment to get a glimpse of the street. In my opinion, they represent the best example of the rice-growing comparison in terms of their patience and wisdom in handling their finances. They’ve labored in their business for years making little profit per unit of their product, at the same time, they save and reinvest their earnings on their business or on land. They have gotten wealthier while being absolutely discrete.
