12. it is an integral part of all cultural activities in JAVA​


Javanese Gamelan

Gamelan

Almost everywhere in Yogya, Solo, Semarang and other cities in Central Java, when you’re at Karatons, markets, villages, or the hotel’s lobby, you should hear the magical melodious percussion music – the gamelan Javanese orchestra.

In his book ‘Music of Java‘ Jaap Kunst says, “Gamelan is comparable to only two things, moonlight and flowing water…mysterious like moonlight and always changing like flowing water …”

Gamelan music is an integral part of all cultural activities in Java such as wayang kulit (leather puppets) performance, traditional dance, uyon-uyon (symphony orchestra performance), etc. There are two kinds of laras (tuning) in gamelan, namely Slendro (comparable to minor key in Western music) and Pelog (major key). A complete set of gamelan consist of two sets of different instruments of Slendro and Pelog, such as:

Kendang (double ended drum, beaten by hands)

It is a leading instrument. The pengendang (drummer) is the conductor of the gamelan orchestra. There are five (5) different sizes of kendang from 20 cm to 45 cm.

Saron

A glockenspiel with bronze bar struck with wooden mallet. There are three kinds of Saron : Saron Barung, Saron Peking, Saron Demung.

29. Explain the Culture is integrated at times unstable​


Answer & Explanation:

Culture refers to the shared values, beliefs, practices, and customs that define a group or society. It is an integral part of human society, shaping our perceptions, behaviors, and social interactions. However, culture is not a static entity and is subject to change and evolution over time. This can make it integrated at times, but also unstable at other times.

Culture is integrated because it serves as a unifying force that brings people together around shared values, norms, and practices. It provides a sense of identity, belonging, and coherence within a group or society. The integration of culture can be seen in the way that people share common language, traditions, and customs, which helps to create a sense of social cohesion and mutual understanding.

However, culture can also be unstable, particularly in times of rapid social change or cultural conflict. When new ideas or values are introduced, they can challenge existing cultural norms and practices, leading to social tension and instability. For example, the introduction of new technologies or social movements can disrupt existing cultural practices and lead to conflict and social fragmentation.

Moreover, different cultural groups may have competing values and beliefs, which can create cultural conflicts and instability. This can be seen in situations where different groups hold divergent views on issues such as gender roles, religion, or politics.

In conclusion, culture is integrated at times because it provides a unifying force that brings people together around shared values and practices. However, it is also unstable at times due to the introduction of new ideas or social conflicts that challenge existing cultural norms and practices.
