Fluttering helps create propulsion. For sprint swimmers, a strong flutter kick is critical. The kick is one of the main drivers of how well a swimmer can hydro-plane (skim across the surface of the water). Sprinters need a 6-beat kick that uses no recovery phase—unlike the arm, which get a brief recovery motion

25. Name at least 7 ways or steps to practice flutter kicks​


Explanation:

Step 1: Try the flutter kick in a stationary position.

Step 2: Begin moving one leg up and down.

Step 3: Repeat step 2 with the opposite leg.

Step 4: Now try alternating legs to kick.

Step 5: Start slowly letting go of the pool wall or edge.

Step 6: Try moving in the water by practicing the flutter kick with a kickboard.

Step 7: Try to flutter kick without any water equipment.
