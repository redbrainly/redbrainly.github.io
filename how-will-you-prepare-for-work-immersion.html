9. How work immersion program develop you as a person?


Answer:

Work Immersion Program is one of the requirements for graduating in the Senior High School Level. Work immersion refers to hands on experience consisting of 80 hours which Grade 11 and 12 will be exposing themselves to actual places, actual tools and actual works provided by the school.

As a person, work immersion program will develop your:

1. Understanding

Doing actual things rather that just listening to your teacher would help you understand more about your lessons. You will be able to observe differences between theories and actual things.

2. Cognitive Skills

Your flexibility and attention will further be enhanced. It will help you have a better memory, attention control and higher rate of problem solving skills.

3. Creativity

It will lead you to discover things you've never seen before. It will help you not just to rely on what you hear and see rather it will improve your development skills to obtain solutions to your questions.

3. Expectations

You'll be satisfied with all your expectations if you've done and seen the process yourself which can increase your confidence in your knowledge.

Answer:Since 2017, the Work Immersion Program for senior high school students has been implemented by the Filipino Government’s Department of Education (DepEd) and supported by The Asia Foundation’s Coalitions for Change (CFC), working alongside national and local government agencies, private and public enterprises and nongovernmental organizations. The Program is offered as a pre-requisite to graduation to Grade 11 and 12 students, aged 15–18, of DepEd public schools, private schools, state colleges and universities, and technical-vocational institutions. Due to the lack of knowledge, skills, and work experience that puts students at a disadvantage, as part of the programme, DepEd has made it a requirement for them to attain hands-on experience through work simulation of their chosen track to give them greater opportunities to apply their knowledge and capabilities. Participating students are trained, having close mentorship in their work environment with industry supervisors for a period of 80–320 hours during their two years of study. Learners cannot exceed eight working hours per day, anytime between 06.00-18.00 hours. To prepare for Work Immersion, learners have to take four specialized subjects where they can gain experience in a work environment related to their field of specialization. This aims to better equip senior high school students for college, work or business; develop life and career skills; and have attitudes, appreciation and respect for work. It also allows them to have a smooth transition from school to work after they gain valuable experience.

30. how would you describe an ideal work immersion supervisor


Answer:

In conclusion, an effective supervisor is one who offers leadership and brings the team together. It is someone with good communication skills, neutral, trustworthy, flexible and who knows how to delegate effectively. The leader should also be a learner and should treat their subordinates as partners.

*Honest and Empathetic,

Near-perfect bosses are honest about everything. They see secrets and lies as anunethical waste of time.

*Motivational and Nurturing

To grow in skills, you have to be motivated. Some high achievers are motivated internally by their own ambition, but even they need to be motivated by specific goals and accomplishments, not just by the desire to get ahead.

*Creative and Inspiring

Great bosses are creative. They devise different training methods and development goals for each employee. They don’t assume that the usual way is the best way.

*Guiding and Supportive,

The last thing best bosses do is boss people around. They get it that no one likes being bossed around and told what to do all the time. Instead of bossing, they guide.
