18. Advantages and disadvantage of using collaborative strategic reading in elementary learners?


Answer:

Initially, the teacher presents the strategies (preview, click and clunk, get the gist, and wrap up) to the whole class using modeling, role playing, and teacher think-alouds. After students have developed proficiency applying the strategies through teacher-facilitated activities, the teacher asks them to form heterogeneous groups, where each student performs a defined role as students collaboratively implement the strategies.

Although CSR was designed to be used with expository text, it can also be used with narrative text. It is highly compatible with a range of reading programs, including literature-based instruction, basal reading programs, and eclectic or balanced approaches.

The goals of CSR are to improve reading comprehension and increase conceptual learning in ways that maximize students' involvement. Developed to enhance reading comprehension skills for students with learning disabilities and students at risk for reading difficulties, CSR has also yielded positive outcomes for average and high average achieving students (Klingner & Vaughn, 1996; Klingner, Vaughn, & Schumm, in press).

Explanation:

Collaborative Strategic Reading (CSR) teaches students to use comprehension strategies while working cooperatively. Student strategies include previewing the text; giving ongoing feedback by deciding "click" (I get it) or "clunk" (I don't get it) at the end of each paragraph; "getting the gist" of the most important parts of the text; and "wrapping up" key ideas. Find out how to help students of mixed achievement levels apply comprehension strategies while reading content area text in small groups.

20. explain the basic concept in elementary map reading​


ANSWER:

What is the basic concept of elementary map reading?

Map reading (also sometimes written as map-reading) is the act of interpreting or understanding the geographic information portrayed on a map. Through map reading, the reader should be able to develop a mental map of the real-world information by processing the symbolized information shown on the map.

27. sample detailed lesson plan in elementary level​


Answer:

Subject: English

Grade level: 4th grade

Duration: 45 minutes

Objectives:To introduce the concept of nounsTo identify common and proper nounsTo practice writing sentences using proper nouns

Materials:Whiteboard and markersChart paper and markersHandouts (worksheet on nouns)

Introduction (10 minutes):Greet the students and ask them how their day is going.Write the word "nouns" on the whiteboard and ask the students if they know what it means.Explain to the students that nouns are words that describe people, places, things, or ideas.Write a few examples on the whiteboard (e.g. dog, city, love, book, etc.).Ask the students if they can guess which ones are proper nouns.

Direct Instruction (20 minutes):Explain the difference between common nouns and proper nouns.Write "Common Nouns" and "Proper Nouns" on the chart paper and place it on the board.Write examples of common nouns (e.g. cat, street, happiness, etc.) under "Common Nouns".Write examples of proper nouns (e.g. Siamese, Main Street, Joy, etc.) under "Proper Nouns".Explain that proper nouns always start with a capital letter and refer to a specific person, place, or thing.Write a few sentences on the board and ask the students to identify the proper nouns in each sentence.

Guided Practice (10 minutes):Distribute the handouts to the students and explain that they are going to practice writing sentences using proper nouns.Give the students 5-7 minutes to complete the worksheet.Walk around the classroom to monitor the students and offer help if needed.

Closure (5 minutes):Call on a few students to share their sentences and highlight the proper nouns in each sentence.Summarize what the students learned during the lesson.Remind the students that proper nouns are important to use in writing to make their sentences clear and specific.Assessment:Observation of students during the guided practice activityCompleted handoutsTeacher-made quiz on proper nouns next lesson.
