In a diocese neighboring the one in which I live there was a recent controversy when the archbishop insisted that one parish regularize its canonical status by submitting to him authority over its finances, authority previously held by a lay board. The parish refused, and ultimately the archbishop removed its priest. What was most interesting and disturbing about the situation was not the problem with the status of the parish in itself, but the fact that many Catholics in the area sincerely could not understand why this "detail" mattered, and why the archbishop would make so much fuss over it. 19. The central ____________ government introduced an educational decree fusing sectarian school run by the friars into a school called the philippine institute.


To fill in the blank sentence, the word that must be filled is: SPANISH

EXPLANATION:

"The history of Philippines education is divided into several periods"

Pre-Spanish Period

In pre-Spanish, education in the Philippines was informal, unstructured, and methodless. Children are provided with academic vocational training by their parents and in the homes of tribal mentors.

Spanish period

Major changes in the education system occurred during the Spanish colonization. The tribal tutors were replaced by the Spanish Missionaries. This religious education was for the elite, especially in the early years of Spanish colonization. Access to education with Filipinos was later liberalized through the enactment of the Education Decree of 1863 which provided for the establishment of at least one primary school for boys and girls in each city under the responsibility of the municipal government and the normal establishment of a male teacher school under supervision. the Jesuits. Main instruction is free and Spanish teaching is mandatory. Education during that period was inadequate, suppressed, and controlled (sectarian). By 1898, enrollment in schools at all levels exceeded 200,000 students.

First Republic

The defeat of Spain by American forces paved the way for the Republic of Aguinaldo under the Revolutionary Government. The Spanish-run school for more than three centuries was temporarily closed but reopened on 29 August 1898 by the Minister of the Interior. The Burgos Institute at Malolos, the Malolos Military Academy, and the University of the Philippines Literature were founded. A system of free and compulsory basic education was established by the Malolos Constitution.

American period

This adequate secular and free public school system was established in the first decade of American administration on the recommendation of the Schurman Commission. The main free instruction that people are trained for civic and vocation duties is enforced by the Taft Commission per President William McKinley's instructions. Non-commissioned officers are assigned to teach using English as the language of instruction.
