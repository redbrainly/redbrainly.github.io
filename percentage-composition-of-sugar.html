16. A. Percentage Composition Refer to the examples below to compute for the percentage composition of the following: 1) Lye (NaOH) 2) Rust (Fe2O3) 3) Boric Acid (H3BO3) 4) Simple Sugar (C6H12O6) 5) Calamine (Zn2SiO4 H₂O)​


Answer:

Calculations for the percentage composition of each compound:

:Lye (NaOH):

Molecular weight of NaOH = 23 g/mol

Mass of Na = 23 x 1 = 23 g

Mass of O = 16 x 1 = 16 g

Mass of H = 1 x 1 = 1 g

Molecular weight = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40 g/mol

% of Na = (23 / 40) x 100 = 57.5%

% of O = (16 / 40) x 100 = 40%

% of H = (1 / 40) x 100 = 2.5%

Rust (Fe2O3):

Molecular weight of Fe2O3 = 159 g/mol

Mass of Fe = 55.8 x 2 = 111.6 g

Mass of O = 16 x 3 = 48 g

Molecular weight = 111.6 + 48 = 159.6 g/mol

% of Fe = (111.6 / 159.6) x 100 = 70%

% of O = (48 / 159.6) x 100 = 30%

Boric Acid (H3BO3):

Molecular weight of H3BO3 = 61 g/mol

Mass of B = 10.8 x 1 = 10.8 g

Mass of O = 16 x 3 = 48 g

Mass of H = 1 x 3 = 3 g

Molecular weight = 10.8 + 48 + 3 = 61 g/mol

% of B = (10.8 / 61) x 100 = 17.7%

% of O = (48 / 61) x 100 = 78.7%

% of H = (3 / 61) x 100 = 4.9%

Simple Sugar (C6H12O6):

Molecular weight of C6H12O6 = 180 g/mol

Mass of C = 12 x 6 = 72 g

Mass of H = 1 x 12 = 12 g

Mass of O = 16 x 6 = 96 g

Molecular weight = 72 + 12 + 96 = 180 g/mol

% of C = (72 / 180) x 100 = 40%

% of H = (12 / 180) x 100 = 6.7%

% of O = (96 / 180) x 100 = 53.3%

Calamine (Zn2SiO4 H2O):

Molecular weight of Zn2SiO4 H2O = 247 g/mol

Mass of Zn = 65.4 x 2 = 130.8 g

Mass of Si = 28 x 1 = 28 g

Mass of O = 16 x 4 = 64 g

Mass of H = 1 x 2 = 2 g

Molecular weight = 130.8 + 28 + 64 + 2 = 247 g/mol

% of Zn = (130.8 / 247) x 100 = 52.9%

% of Si = (28 / 247) x 100 = 11.3%

% of O = (64 / 247) x 100 = 25.9%

% of H = (2 / 247) x 100 = 0.8%

28. aspartame is an artificial non-saccharide sweetener that is commonly used as a substitute to sugar. it is usually consumed by people suffering from diabetes, enabling them to enjoy sweet-tasting foods without carbohydrates. calculate the percentage composition of all elements present in aspartame, if its chemical formula is C14H18N2O5


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of C₁₄H₁₈N₂O₅.

Note that the molar masses of C, H, N, and O are 12.011 g, 1.008 g, 14.007 g, and 15.999 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass of} \: \text{C}_{14}\text{H}_{18}\text{N}_2\text{O}_5 & = \text{14(12.011 g) + 18(1.008 g) + 2(14.007 g) + 5(15.999 g)} \\ & = \text{294.307 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the percentage composition of C₁₄H₁₈N₂O₅.

• For C

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%\text{C} & = \frac{n \times \text{molar mass of C}}{\text{molar mass of} \: \text{C}_{14}\text{H}_{18}\text{N}_2\text{O}_5} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{14 \times \text{12.011 g}}{\text{294.307 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{57.14\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For H

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%\text{H} & = \frac{n \times \text{molar mass of H}}{\text{molar mass of} \: \text{C}_{14}\text{H}_{18}\text{N}_2\text{O}_5} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{18 \times \text{1.008 g}}{\text{294.307 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{6.16\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For N

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%\text{N} & = \frac{n \times \text{molar mass of N}}{\text{molar mass of} \: \text{C}_{14}\text{H}_{18}\text{N}_2\text{O}_5} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{2 \times \text{14.007 g}}{\text{294.307 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{9.52\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For O

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%\text{O} & = \frac{n \times \text{molar mass of O}}{\text{molar mass of} \: \text{C}_{14}\text{H}_{18}\text{N}_2\text{O}_5} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{5 \times \text{15.999 g}}{\text{294.307 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{27.18\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, aspartame is composed of 57.14% C, 6.16% H, 9.52% N, and 27.18% O by mass.
