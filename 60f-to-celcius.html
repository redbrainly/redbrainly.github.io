4. what is the formula of fahrenheit to celcius and celcius to fahrenheit ?​


MEASURING TEMPERATURE

Gabriel Fahrenheit (1686-1736) devised a scale for the mercury thermometer such that the freezing point of water is 32° F and the boiling point of water is 212° F.

Shortly after Fahrenheit developed his scale, Anders Celsius (1701-1744), developed another scale. Celsius developed his scale such that the freezing point of water was 0° and the boiling point was 100°. We are familiar with the Celsius thermometer as the centigrade thermometer. Remember that centi means 1/100 or 0.01. In the Celsius thermometer, there are 100 intervals between 0° centigrade and 100° centigrade. Since the scale that Celsius developed was so convenient to use, the centigrade thermometer was adopted by scientists. All the countries using the metric system have also adopted this thermometer which was formerly known as the centigrade thermometer. It is now officially known as the Celsius thermometer in honor of the man who developed the scale. Each unit in the Celsius thermometer is called a degree Celsius (°C).

This are the formula to convert from Fahrenheit to Celsius and vice versa.

1. Fahrenheit to Celsius

C = 5/9 (F - 32)

2. Celcius to Fahrenheit

F = 9/5C +32

17. the C1 has 60F and C2 has 20F and C3 has 12F connected to a 120V.​


Answer:

The C1, C2, and C3 are capacitors and their capacitances are given in Farads (F). The voltage across all the capacitors is 120V.

The total capacitance of the circuit is the sum of the individual capacitances.

So, Total Capacitance = C1 + C2 + C3 = 60F + 20F + 12F = 92F

The capacitance of a capacitor is inversely proportional to the voltage across it. That means as the voltage across the capacitors increase, the capacitance decreases and vice versa.

If the voltage across the capacitors is constant, the amount of charge stored in a capacitor is directly proportional to its capacitance. Thus, the more capacitance a capacitor has, the more charge it can store.

It is important to note that the voltage across the capacitors is the same. This means that the current flowing through the circuit is the same for all the capacitors.
