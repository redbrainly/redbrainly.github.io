3. give the defferences of chinese peking opera face painting and kabuki face painting​


Answer:

Differences of Kabuki, Wayang Kulit, and Peking Opera

The differences of Kabuki, Wayang Kulit, and Peking Opera lie in the way they are performed, their origins, and their many key characteristics. The differences of Kabuki, Wayang Kulit, and Peking Opera are basically noted by the places they came from. Kabuki is from Japan. Wayang Kulit is founded in Indonesia. Meanwhile, Peking Opera is established in China. Aside from their origins, their characteristics also differ from one another.

Differences of Kabuki, Wayang Kulit, and Peking Opera in Details

Kabuki

This is a traditional Japanese form of theater that came from the period of Edo.  

Kabuki is known to be extravagant, unrestrained, and rough.  

Within the play, there are some interactions between the actors and the audience.

It is immensely focused on showmanship more than the literary aspect of the drama.

Kabuki literally means sing (ka), dance (bu), and skill (ki).

Kabuki has become a theater for the common people and farmers.

Interestingly, Kabuki actors are all male but it was created by a woman.

Wayang Kulit

The most notable difference of Wayang Kulit from the other two is that it is a form of puppet theater.

Aside from puppet, it also utilize shadow (indo: wayang) and light.

In doing so, the puppet controller - usually one or few - uses his voice while the puppets perform on the designated stage.

The Wayang Kulit puppets are made from bison skin then glued to a bamboo stick.

This is also performed in other Asian countries such as Malaysia and Thailand.

Peking Opera

While face-painting in Kabuki has little significance, Peking Opera face paints signify the character of each role.

The stage used in Peking Opera is far less extravagant as it is inclined to depict real-life experiences.

It  has four major role classifications: Shen (male lead), Jing (female lead), Jing (painted face), and Chou (comedian).

Further, Peking Opera has two general types: civil and martial.

This type of theater is a combination of song, dance, martial arts and acrobatics.

15. peking opera face painting done with different colors.​


Answer:

In the Peking Opera, different actors play specific roles and the meaning of those roles is conveyed by specific colors and patterns of face painting and costume. A mostly red face, for example, stands for courage and loyalty. White represents brutality and cruelty, yellow represents fearfulness, and gold indicates godliness. Other colors also have specific meanings when they are the primary color. Pattern is also extremely important. The specific combination of color and pattern is especially important in pantomime, where the actors use no words.
