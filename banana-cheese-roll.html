BANANA MUFFINS

INGREDIENTS: 1/2 cup unsalted butter, (8 Tbsp), softened2/3 cup granulated sugar2 large eggs, room temp, lightly beaten3 bananas, very ripe, coarsely mashed with a fork1 tsp vanilla extract1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, measured correctly1 tsp baking soda1/2 tsp salt1 cup semisweet chocolate chips, divided

US Customary - Metric

INSTRUCTIONPreheat oven to 350˚ F and line a 12-count muffin tin with paper liners.In a mixing bowl, cream together 8 Tbsp softened butter with 2/3 cup sugar. Add 2 lightly beaten eggs.Add 3 mashed bananas and 1 tsp vanilla extract and mix until blended.

In a separate bowl, whisk together dry ingredients: 1 1/2 cups flour, 1 tsp baking soda and 1/2 tsp salt. Mix the dry ingredients into wet ingredients until incorporated.Fold in 3/4 cup chocolate chips. Divide batter evenly into your 12-count muffin tin and sprinkle the remaining 1/4 cup chocolate chips evenly over the tops. Bake at 350˚F on the center rack for 25-30 minutes or until the top is golden and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out.

Which menu description for an Enchilada has correct GPS (grammar, punctuation and spelling)? * O Three soft rolled tortilla stuffed in your choice of meat, dipped in red sauce and topped with melted cheese. Three soft-rolled tortila stuffed with your choice of meats, dipped in red sauce and topped with melted cheese. Three soft rolled tortilas stuffed in your choice of meat, dipped in red sauce and topped with melted cheese. Three soft-rolled tortillas stuffed with your choice of meat, dipped in red sauce and topped with melted cheese. None of the above​


The description with the correct grammar, punctuation, and spelling is "Three soft-rolled tortillas stuffed with your choice of meat, dipped in red sauce and topped with melted cheese"

Distinguishing the correct answer among the four choices can be tricky, however there are few standout differences in terms of their GPS (grammar, punctuation and spelling): with vs in, meats vs meat. Well, the correct option is the one using with and meat (last option). Here's why:

Since the tortilla is the outer part of the stuffing, then the use of "in" here is a grammatical mistake. In addition, the adjective "stuffed" is enough to explain that the tortilla will contain the stuffing of our choice of meat. Thus, the correct conjunction is "with" instead of "in".The use of meats in the second choice is wrong because meat is an uncountable noun that doesn't need the "-s" addition to indicate that it is plural. Meanwhile, in terms of pronunciation and spelling, all of those four choices are all correct.
