1. Differentiate SIMPLE ANNUITY,GENERAL ANNUITY &DEFERRED ANNUITY (give example each annuity with solutions)​


Simple Annuities Due are annuities where payments are made at the beginning of. each period and the compounding period is EQUAL to the payment period (P/Y = C/Y) The formula for determining the present value of an annuity is PV = dollar amount of an individual annuity payment multiplied by P = PMT * [1 – [ (1 / 1+r)^n] / r] where: ... PMT = Dollar amount of each payment. r = Discount or interest rate. n = Number of periods in which payments will be made.
P=PMT • 1-(1/(1+r)^n)/r
Example: most car leases are simple annuities due, where payments are made monthly and interest rates are compounded monthly.

A general annuity is an annuity where the payments do not coincide with the interest periods. You will be able to see that it is very easy to deal with general annuities once an equivalent interest rate is determined with that equivalent rate being compounded as often as the payments are made. A = 1(1 + i)2 ** n = 2, the number of times interest is compounded per year. Now find the amount of the annuity using the annuity formula. ... Here the payment interval( 1 year ) is different than the interest period ( ¼ year). This is a general annuity.
Example: Monthly payments of $500 where interest is 6%/a, compounded monthly. Here the payment interval and the interest interval are the same – 1 month.

A deferred annuity is an insurance contract that guarantees retirement income at a future date. Deferred annuities differ from immediate annuities in that income payments are delayed until the date specified in the insurance contract. Earnings on the premium grow tax-deferred until the money is withdrawn. The formula of deferred annuity is P = Annuity Payment · r = Rate of Interest · n = Number of Periodic Payments · t = Period of Delay.
Example: Someone who is 50 years old might purchase a deferred annuity with the intention of receiving income at the age of 65 or even at 80. The greater the length of time between your annuity purchase and the payout, the more time the value will have to potentially grow.

28. draw the time diagram for a deferred annuity​


Deferred Annuities

A deferred annuity is a financial deal in which payments from an annuity are put off until a certain amount of time has passed. Most annuities have two stages, as shown in this picture.

→ You can see the image below.

Every day, you use money. To get what you need, you have to do transactions with money. Most of the time, when you buy a house or car, you have to make a series of payments at different times. This kind of deal is called an ANNUITY.

An ANNUITY is a series of payments (or deposits) that are all the same amount and are made at regular intervals.

There are two stages:

Accumulation Stage. A single payment can earn interest for a certain amount of time. During this time, which is called the period of deferral, there are no payments from the annuity.Stage of payments. The financial contract says that the annuity can start at the beginning of this stage as any of the four types of annuities. Note that the value at maturity for the "accumulation" stage is the same as the "payments" stage's "principal."

KINDS OF ANNUITY

Depending on how often you pay and how long you pay interest

Simple Annuity

– An annuity in which the time between payments is the same as the time between interest payments

Annuity in General

– An annuity in which the time between payments is not the same as the time between interest payments.

Depending on when the money was paid

Ordinary Annuity (Annuity Immediate)

– A type of annuity in which payments are made at the end of each payment period

Annuity Certain

– Annuity Certain is a type of annuity where payments start and stop at set times.

The term you need to know with this lesson:Terms of Annuity (t)

The amount of time between the first and last payment intervals.

Regular or Periodic Payment (R)

How much each payment is.

Amount (Future Value) of an annuity (F)

The total value in the future of all the payments that will be made over the life of the annuity.

Present Value of an annuity (P)

The total present value of all the payments that will be made over the course of the annuity's entire term.
