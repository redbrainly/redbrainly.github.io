This means that the ratio of the number of teeth on the two gears is 0.603:1 = x:y.

Since the smaller gear is driving the larger gear, the angular velocity of the smaller gear must be greater than the angular velocity of the larger gear. Let's say the angular velocity of the smaller gear is w and the angular velocity of the larger gear is z. The ratio of the angular velocities of the two gears is w / z.
