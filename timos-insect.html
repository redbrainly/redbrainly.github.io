12. the ideal timo for applying fetilizer is twelve noon.​


Fertilizing your plants typically coincides with your watering regimen at particular times of the year. Helping the nutrients find their way into the deep soil, fertilizers need to dissolve in the flowing water and move from the surface to the thirsty feeder roots. Choosing the best time of day for fertilizing your plants contributes to a much healthier garden in the long run.

Ideal Time

Applying fertilizer in the morning allows you to take advantage of the cool temperatures and reduced winds. As you spread the nutrients evenly across the topsoil, you water them in without much evaporation or wind loss, so necessary elements reach the roots quickly and trigger absorption almost immediately. Additionally, plants are not stressed from midday heat, so they are more receptive to absorbing the nutrients as they move down through the soil.

Evening Application

Fertilizing in the evening is an alternative, if it is applied through fertigation. A system of tubes with strategically placed emitters, called a drip system, allows you to inject the fertilizer into the flowing water to reach roots without wetting foliage; this process is called fertigation. Although plants are not stressed from daytime heat, a typical fertilizing strategy of applying it from above contributes to standing water on foliage. If moisture remains on plants overnight, they are vulnerable to fungal infections. Fertigation guarantees that the foliage is not dampened overnight and that each plant receives its necessary fertilizer for optimum growth.Preparation

The proper time of day also needs to correspond to the right time of year. In general, you apply slow-release fertilizers in the spring and fast-release types in the autumn. If you water fertilizers into the soil during the morning hours, you contribute to lush springtime and summer growth. The fall fertilizer application provides plant strength and nutrient support to withstand the dormant winter season. By applying the fertilizer incorrectly at midday, your plants do not receive the same even amount of nutrients for their best growth and protection.

Foliar Application

If you apply fertilizers directly to leaves, such as on fruit trees, you need to follow the same time of day guidelines as ground fertilizing. Referred to as foliar spraying, you apply a fine mist of water and water soluble fertilizer to the foliage for immediate absorption. Typically used for supplemental nutrient deficiencies, you do not want to lose any of the spray to wind gusts or evaporation. In addition, the foliage still needs the entire day to dry out from the foliar application so that the leaves are not threatened by fungal infections.
