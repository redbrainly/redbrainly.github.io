15. 1. Among the statements below, what best describes intersubjectivity?a. Intersubjectivity is about how humans treat plants.b. It is a state of sharing of subjective states by two or moreindividuals.c. It is about how we judge other persons.d. We treat others as objects in intersubjective relationships.​


From the statement given, the best statement to describe intersubjectivity is B. It is a state of sharing subjective states by two or more individuals.

Explanation details:

Simply put, intersubjectivity can be defined as the subjective influence that one person gives or receives on another because they are in a close relationship that can influence each other.

The meaning of subjective itself is a view that is based on personal feelings, not facts or actual things.

Examples of intersubjectivity are:

Since childhood, Anna was always told by her mother that holding a stray cat was dirty and that there might be germs in its fur that could cause illness. From then until adulthood, Anna did not like and did not want to be near stray cats.

From here we can see that Anna's dislike of the stray cat because she is afraid that the stray cat is dirty, is influenced by the subjective view she received from her mother and she believed it. In fact, not all stray cats are dirty and have germs that can cause illness. Anna's view of this cat is a form of intersubjectivity.

So the best statement to describe intersubjectivity is that it is a state of sharing subjective states by two or more individuals.

25. examples of intersubjectivity?


What is intersubjectivity?

It is the shared concept, idea, or view between two or more people. Intersubjectivity shows that even though individuals are unique and have their unique experiences, they can still share or agree on a subject.

Let us take a look at the next question for an example.

How intersubjectivity present in respect?

Since there are more than thousands of different cultures in the world, the definition and views surrounding respect may differ from one person to another.

A Filipino child may be expected to look straight into his parents' eyes as a way to show respect and sincerity while a Japanese child is taught that looking straight into the eyes of an elder is disrespectful and a show of scorn. These two acts are opposites but are considered acceptable in their own cultures.

If we take away the act, we are left with the concept of respect. In both cultures, they do agree that respect is due to elders and parents. So while they both have a different way of doing things, they still have something in agreement. This is the same for many other things in the world.

What are other examples of intersubjectivity?

Another example is education. Every country sets a curriculum to be followed by local schools. Some countries will have students finishing high school by the time they are 18 while others will finish their basic education by 16-years old. Some schools allow students to choose electives or subjects they are interested in learning more about while others have a mandatory set in order to graduate.

So while there are many different ways that the schooling experience varies from one student to another, one thing is sure. They need to complete and pass their subjects or courses in order to gain their diploma. They need to attend classes, study and understand what is taught, and pass examinations.

Another way of seeing intersubjectivity in relation to respect is that by keeping an open mind and accepting that people will always be different from one another due to their own experiences and learned behavior, people can still get along by agreeing to respect one another and agreeing to disagree on certain things.
