2. what is the difference between physical freedom and moral freedom?​


Answer:

Moral freedom

Moral freedom is not the absence of moral restraint nor the freedom to live your life without morality. Moral freedom is the freedom to choose a set of moral values and to live your life in accordance with those values.

An example of moral freedom is a person choosing to live his or her life bound by adherence to virtues such as honesty, loyalty, forgiveness, and self-discipline.

Living within such a moral framework provides freedom to act without fear or uncertainty when faced with difficult decisions. Every decision made is tested against the virtues that form this moral framework.

Am I being honest?

Am I being loyal?

Am I being forgiving?

Am I exercising self-discipline?

Without such a framework to test against, our lives can become an agony of indecision and doubt. We can be held prisoner by doubt and uncertainty and real freedom comes not from an absence of restraint but from a clearly marked path to follow.

Physical freedom

Physical freedom is about possibility; travel, adventure, doing fun things with my family and friends, being excited and engaged with my work. It’s about being able to hike up the mountains to see the gorillas, getting through my workday, where I sit a lot, without aches and pains, lifting my bags into the overhead storage compartment with ease, being excited about implementing a new idea and having the energy to kick it up a gear when I need to.

17. GIVE AN EXAMPLES - psychological freedom - moral freedom ​


Explanation:

Psychological freedom refers to the sense of personal agency and autonomy that an individual feels in their life. It means that a person is able to make choices and decisions for themselves without being excessively influenced by external factors or by their own psychological limitations. For example, a person who feels psychologically free might be able to resist the temptation to procrastinate, or might be able to overcome their fear of public speaking in order to give a presentation.

Moral freedom refers to the ability to act in accordance with one's own moral values and principles, rather than being constrained by external demands or expectations. For example, a person who feels morally free might be able to stand up for their beliefs even if it means going against the wishes of their family or community. Alternatively, a person who feels morally free might be able to make a difficult but ethical decision, even if it means facing consequences or backlash.

26. GIVE AN EXAMPLES - psychological freedom - moral freedom ​


Answer:

Psychological freedom refers to a person's ability to think, feel, and act independently, without being influenced or constrained by external factors such as social norms, expectations, or other people's opinions.

Here are a few examples of psychological freedom:

A person who is free to express their own thoughts and feelings, even if they differ from the dominant viewpoint, is experiencing psychological freedom.

A person who is able to make their own choices and decisions, without feeling pressure or coercion from others, is experiencing psychological freedom.

A person who is able to pursue their own interests and goals, without feeling limited or hindered by external factors, is experiencing psychological freedom.

Moral freedom refers to a person's ability to act in accordance with their own moral principles, without being constrained by external factors such as laws or social norms.

Here are a few examples of moral freedom:

A person who is free to act on their own conscience, even if it means disobeying laws or social norms, is experiencing moral freedom.

A person who is able to choose to do the right thing, even if it means facing consequences or backlash, is experiencing moral freedom.

A person who is able to live according to their own moral values, without feeling pressure to conform to the expectations of others, is experiencing moral freedom.
