14. What i the rate of markup baed on cot? An eyeware hop ell wrap around unglae for $86. If they wanted to lower the price to $75, what rate of markdown would they have to offer?.


Step-by-step explanation:

To calculate the rate of markup based on cost, you need to know the cost of the product and the price at which it is being sold. In this case, you are given the price of the wrap-around glasses at $86 and are asked to determine the rate of markdown needed to lower the price to $75.

To find the rate of markdown, you can use the following formula:

Rate of markdown = (Original price - Sale price) / Original price

Plugging in the values from the problem, you get:

Rate of markdown = ($86 - $75) / $86

Solving for the rate of markdown gives:

Rate of markdown = $11 / $86

Converting both amounts to decimal form gives:

Rate of markdown = 0.127906977

This means that the eyewear shop would need to offer a 12.79% markdown to lower the price of the wrap-around glasses from $86 to $75.

20. (cot A - cos A)/(cot A + cos A) = (1 - sin A)/(1 + sin A) ​need it ASAP


The equation you provided can be simplified as follows:

(cot A - cos A)/(cot A + cos A) = (1 - sin A)/(1 + sin A)

Taking the common denominator of the two sides, we get:

cot A - cos A = (1 - sin A) * (cot A + cos A) / (1 + sin A)

Expanding the right-hand side and canceling the common terms, we get:

cot A - cos A = cot A * cos A / (1 + sin A)

Dividing both sides by cos A, we get:

cot A = cot A * cot A / (1 + sin A)

Isolating cot A on one side, we get:

cot A * (1 + sin A) = cot^2 A

Dividing both sides by (1 + sin A), we get:

cot A = cot^2 A

Solving for cot A, we get:

cot A = ±√(cot A)

Therefore, the solution is cot A = ±√(cot A).

DepEd national enrollment data as of October 26, 2020 showed that there are 25.03 million students enrolled for School Year (SY) 2020-2021 which formally opened on Oct. 5. DepEd latest data showed that highest numbers of enrollees for this SY are from Region IV-A (Calabarzon) with 3.4 million followed by National Capital Region (NCR) with 2.6 million and Region III (Central Luzon) with 2.5 million.

30. Find the fundamentals identity of[tex] \frac{tan \: x \: + \: cot \: x}{cot \: x} [/tex]​


SOLUTION

In order to find the Fundamental Identity given trigometric expression, first we need to express it with [tex] cos [/tex] and [tex] sin [/tex]. Then, we use Pythagorean and Trigometric Identity.

[tex] \rm{\frac{tan \: x \: + \: cot \: x}{cot \: x}} \\\\ \rm{= \frac{\frac{cos \ x}{sin \ x} + \frac{sin \ x}{cos \ x}}{\frac{cos \ x}{sin \ x}}} \\\\ \rm{= \frac{\left(\frac{cos \ x}{sin \ x}+\frac{sin \ x}{cos \ x}\right)sin \ x}{cos \ x}} [/tex]

Join the two fractions in numerator

[tex] \rm{= \frac{\frac{cos^2 \ x + sin^2 \ x}{sin \ x \ cos \ x} sin \ x}{cos \ x}} \\\\ \rm{= \frac{cos^2 \ x + sin^2 \ x}{cos \ x \ cos \ x}} \\\\ \rm{= \frac{cos^2 \ x + sin^2 \ x}{cos^2 \ x}} \\\\ \rm{= \frac{1}{cos^2 \ x}} \\\\ \boxed{\rm{= sec^2 \ x}} [/tex]
