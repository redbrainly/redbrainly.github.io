14. differentiate corporate governance from corporate social responsibility ?​


Answer:

Corporate governance is concerned with holding the balance between economic and social goals and between individual and communal goals. It focuses on the actions of the corporation and the board toward its shareholders and other stakeholders, how corporate governance serves or fails to serve their interests.

23. write your reflection about the video "why companies practice corporate social responsibility".help pls po ​


The video explains the concept of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) and why companies practice it. CSR refers to a company's responsibility to operate in an ethical and sustainable way, taking into account the impact of its operations on the environment, society, and the economy. Companies practice CSR for various reasons, including building a positive reputation, reducing costs, attracting and retaining employees and customers, and complying with regulations.

The video emphasizes that CSR is not just a philanthropic activity but a business strategy that creates shared value for both the company and society. By incorporating social and environmental concerns into their operations, companies can create long-term benefits for themselves and society. The video also highlights some examples of companies that have successfully implemented CSR practices, such as Patagonia, a clothing company that prioritizes environmental sustainability, and Ben & Jerry's, an ice cream company that supports social justice causes.

Overall, the video emphasizes the importance of CSR in today's business landscape, where consumers are increasingly demanding companies to act responsibly and make a positive impact on society. Companies that prioritize CSR not only contribute to a better world but also create a competitive advantage for themselves.
