18. what are its types? suicide and para suicide


Answer:

There are four different types of suicide on the basis of different types of relationship between the person and his society according to Emile Durkheim:

Egoistic Suicide – A person destroys himself when he becomes isolated or feels that he has no place or value in the society. This person is a self-centered person who lacks altruistic feelings. Altruistic Suicide – This suicide occurs when there is so much integration of the person to the group (social). They became too close and intimate. Anomic Suicide – This happens when there is a breakdown of social equilibrium like bankruptcy or after winning a lottery. Meaning, things happened suddenly Fatalistic Suicide – This happens because of over over regulation in society like a suicide of a slave or of a barren woman.

Durkheim concludes that suicide happens because of social disorganization or lack of social integration or social solidarity.

Nationally, the suicide rate for teens and young adults has nearly tripled since the 1940s. Throughout the United States, suicide is the third leading cause of death for youth between the ages of 10 and 24 (approximately 4,600 lives lost each year). Of the reported suicides in the 10 to 24 age group, 81% of the deaths were males and 19% were females. Boys are more likely than girls to die from suicide while girls are more likely to attempt suicide (CDC, 2017b).

Among youth, non-suicidal, self-injurious behaviors such as cutting, burning, hitting oneself, scratching to the point of bleeding, or interfering with healing can occur together with suicidal behaviors. It is important to consider the nature of the link between these two types of behavior (Grandclerc et al., 2016).

In recent years, suicide rates for middle-aged women have increased by more than 30%, exceeding the increases observed among middle-aged men. However, using data from the Nurses’ Health Study, researchers determined women who were socially well integrated* had a more than 3-fold lower risk for suicide over 18 years of followup (Tsai et al., 2015).

30. 11. Why is suicide for others called sacrificial suicide?​


Altruistic suicide is sacrifice of one's life to save or benefit others, for the good of the group, or to preserve the traditions and honor of a society. It is always intentional. Benevolent suicide refers to the self sacrifice of one's own life for the sake of the greater good.[1] Such sacrifice may be for the sake of executing a particular action, or for the sake of keeping a natural balance in the society. It is a theme or concept of a custom of sacrifice typically found within certain types of science fiction stories. However, real examples of these customs have been recorded to exist among some indigenous people, such as certain Inuit tribes. This was seen by Émile Durkheim in his study Suicide as the product of over-integration with society.[2][3]

In contrast a "sacrifice" committed by force of a state is instead referred to as eugenics or mass murder, but may be otherwise referred to as "enforced population limits" or "population control". In literature, examples may promote the concept as a means for ending enduring types of social conflict, or else deride the concept as an example of a dystopian future society.[4]
