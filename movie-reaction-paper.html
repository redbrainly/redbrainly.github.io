29. hacksaw ridge movie reaction paper


Hacksaw Ridge Reaction

Hacksaw Ridge is one of those movies that immediately caught my attention. Because it's a war film or based on an incredible true story. Astonishing cinematography was used. From the ominous cliff to the muddy, destroyed landscape to the thick war fog with barely discernible figures in the distance.

It's a visual triumph, and the equally impressive soundtrack supports it at every turn. Like in all of Gibson's films, he doesn't hold back on the brutality and misery of war. There are some incredibly gory scenes with various body parts flying and severe physical harm. Gibson is a master at transporting viewers to the battlefield, and Saving Private Ryan and Hacksaw Ridge are both in the same league.

I was concerned about the Hacksaw Ridge because it is based on a genuine story that is almost too amazing to be true. Instead of allowing reality to carry the story, as it does in most "actual" stories, I was concerned that they would oversell the events. But I'm glad to report that it was mostly grounded, except for a few sequences where one guy went Rambo against dozens of foes. Its moral and religious principles were a tad overt, but that is to be anticipated with any Mel Gibson movie.

30. reaction paper to the movie the lovely bones


The movie “The Lovely Bones” was about a fourteen-year old girl who was brutally raped and murdered by their neighbor. Her soul has never been at peace and she continues to watch her family live and interact with her perpetrator.


I have actually watched it a few times, and I admit that I didn’t quite get it the first time. I knew that dear susie (the protagonist) was burdened by something, but I didn’t fully understand the details until after my third or fourth time. I genuinely felt sad for her as her youth was taken away in such an abrupt manner. She wasn’t able to feel and experience the joys and pains of her first love. As a student, I actually felt her loss and longing not only for her life but also for her family. She was also torn between her feelings about revenge and letting go. Eventually, she was able to move on and accept her fate.


Overall, the film was great but it may come as a bit boring at first. It also leaves us a valuable lesson about the importance of life, family and the little things in between. It was a happy ending as all the open issues were tied up. Susie’s soul was finally laid to rest. She was not able to get back to her old life, but in the end, the culprit finally got what he deserved.
