14. when is the right time to cull birds?​


Fall and winter are good times for a backyard flock. In spring, you may have young chicks or fledglings that need your help with feeding and protection from predators until they can fly on their own. Summertime can be too hot for chickens to survive in some areas. If you’re having trouble deciding when it's best to put them down, there are several things we'll discuss below, but first let me offer one more piece of advice - don't do this alone! It should always be done by someone who knows how to handle poultry safely (and even then only if it’s absolutely necessary). I know that sounds obvious, but many people think putting an animal down humanely requires a veterinarian or other professional. This isn't true at all; animals respond very differently depending on what happens around them as well as how quickly they're killed. You wouldn't want to just shoot a dog without training, so why would you kill something you love simply because it needs to go somewhere else where no one will care about its welfare? The same goes here. A trained person is needed to ensure the bird doesn't suffer unnecessarily before death. So please consider hiring a local chicken farmer or farmhand to assist you during these difficult decisions. They'll also likely appreciate a little extra money in return for helping out.

16. what are the reason for culling hens​


Answer:

Culling hens refers to the identification and removal of the non-laying or low producing hens from a laying flock. Unless the birds are diseased, they are suitable for marketing or home cooking. The following topics will address the molting process:

Sight Culling

Culling by Individual Inspection

General Condition

Body Characteristics

Identification of Poor Layers

Molt

Estimating Duration of Molt

Checking Your Culling Technique

Removing the inferior birds reduces the cost of producing eggs, reduces the incidence of disease, and increases the available space for more productive hens. Hens eat feed whether or not they are laying. Removing the cull birds will make more feed and space for more productive birds.

Two types of culling are usually used to remove the inferior birds: (1) sight culling at the time of housing and (2) culling by individual inspection, which evaluates the bird's ability to lay or her past productive performance.

29. differentiate from voluntary culling to involuntary culling​


Answer:

Voluntary culling occurs when the farmer chooses to remove a healthy, fertile, cow due to poor milk production. Involuntary culling, on the other hand, occurs when the farmer is forced to remove a productive, profitable cow due to illness, injury, infertility, or death
