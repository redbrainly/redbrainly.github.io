Nobody is in charge of globalization means globalization is inevitable and all countries in the world must prepare themselves, and no one is specifically responsible for this.

EXPLANATION:

Globalization is reinforced by the existence of various standards that apply internationally. One of the leading standardization bodies, the International Organization for Standardization, has produced many standards related to the management of organizations, products and activities of organizations. The issuance of ISO 26000 on Social Responsibility raises the question, will this standard benefit or harm the Philippines? If it is unavoidable, how should the Philippines prepare itself? No one is responsible, because globalization like it or not, we have to accept it.

In 2010, the ISO is planned to be inaugurated. The ISO that will be produced covers a wide range of issues, which are related to how organizations in society manage themselves and interact with their environment. ISO 26000 on Social Responsibility moves from a collection of ideas about regulating interactions by an organization-whether government, business, or social organizations-with various groups outside itself.

There are no numbers as limits and each organization is only given direction on how to adopt it according to the capacity of the organization. ISO 26000 is an ambitious idea of ​​a better life for more groups. The purpose of this standard is a welfare as well as sustainability (sustainability). Like other international standards, this standard reflects the accumulated concern about an issue.

In ISO 26000 there are several welfare sectors that are considered important to be addressed, namely human rights, labor relations, natural resource management, fair organization implementation, consumer issues, and community development. There are around 90 countries and 40 international or broad-based organizations involved as stakeholders in formulating the standard text. 7. difference between nobodys and nobodies​


Answer:

When Can We Use 'Nobodies'?

'Nobody' as a Pronoun

'Nobody' can both be a pronoun and a noun.

As a pronoun, we cannot use the plural form of 'nobody', i.e. 'nobodies'.

'Nobody' is a singular pronoun and always comes with a singular verb.

Nobody understands what I'm going through.

I keep asking the same question, but nobody cares enough to answer.

'Nobody' as a Noun

However, as a noun, 'nobody' can be treated as any other countable nouns and we can use its plural form with a plural verb.

I'm sure there are more somebodies than nobodies in the world.

I was hanging out with a bunch of nobodies.

16. How does the speaker feel about discovering another nobody? in the poem Nobody​


Answer:

Put generally, the speaker believes that it's better to be "Nobody" and that it's actually quite boring—"dreary"—to be "Somebody." This is where the anaphora comes in—it helps create this sense of boredom and weariness, as though the speaker can hardly put up with the idea of another "Somebody" in the world.
