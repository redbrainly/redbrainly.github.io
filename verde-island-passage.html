20. sino ang nakatuklas ng cape verde islands? ​


Answer:

Henry the Navigator at mg kasamahan

Explanation:

Noong 1456, sa serbisyo ng prinsipe na si Henry the Navigator, si Alvise Cadamosto, Antoniotto Usodimare (isang Venetian at isang kapitan ng Genoese, ayon sa pagkakabanggit) at isang hindi pinangalanan na kapitan ng Portuges, ay magkasamang natuklasan ang ilan sa mga isla. Sa sumunod na dekada, natuklasan nina Diogo Gomes at António de Noli, na mga kapitan din sa serbisyo ng prinsipe Henry, ang natitirang mga isla ng kapuluan. Nang ang mga marinero na ito ay unang lumapag sa Cape Verde, ang mga isla ay baog ng mga tao ngunit hindi sa mga halaman. Bumalik ang Portuges pagkalipas ng anim na taon sa isla ng São Tiago upang matagpuan ang Ribeira Grande (ngayon ay Cidade Velha), noong 1462 — ang kauna-unahang permanenteng pamayanan ng Europa na lungsod sa mga tropiko.

27. Kasunduan ito kung saan nahati ang gagalugaring bahagi ng daigdig ng likhang-isip na guhit na bumabagtas sa Atlantic Ocean, 370 leagues sa kanluran ng Azores island at Cape Verde Islands.​


Answer:

The Treaty of Tordesillas was a 1494 agreement between Spain and Portugal, which divided the non-Christian parts of the world into two spheres of influence. The treaty was based on an imaginary line that was drawn 370 leagues (about 1,185 miles or 1,910 kilometers) west of the Azores islands and Cape Verde islands, which divided the world into two parts. Spain was given the right to claim and colonize the lands to the west of the line, while Portugal was given the right to claim and colonize the lands to the east of the line.

The treaty was a response to the increasing competition between the two countries for land and resources in the New World, following the voyages of Christopher Columbus. The treaty was intended to prevent conflicts between the two countries and to establish a framework for the division of the newly discovered lands. However, the treaty was not always honored and the line of demarcation was often disputed. The treaty was later superseded by the Treaty of Saragossa in 1529, which clarified the dividing line further to the west
