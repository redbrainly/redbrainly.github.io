11. The sum of Cora's age and Rose's age is 40. Cora is 4 years older than Rose. How old is Cora?


Answer:

Cora is 22 years old.

Step-by-step explanation:

Age Word Problem

Age word problems are problems dealing with the ages of people currently, in the past or in the future.

Let us analyze the problem.

Given:

Rose's age - x

Cora's age - x + 4 (4 years older than Rose)

sum of Cora's age and Rose's age - 40

Equation:

x + x + 4 = 40

Solution:

x + x + 4 = 40

2x + 4 = 40

2x = 40 - 4

2x = 36

2x/2 = 36/2

x = 18

Rose is 18 years old.

x + 4 = 18 + 4 = 22

Cora is 22 years old.

Checking:

x + x + 4 = 40

18 + 18 + 4 = 40

40 = 40 ✔

29. Coby and Cora have an average age of 19. If Coby is four years older than Cora, how old is Cora?


Answer:

Cora is 17 years old.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:  Coby is four years older than Cora

Cora (a): xCoby (b): x+4Average of their ages: 19

To solve for average, add the the given values then divide by the total number of items.  In this problem, add the ages then divide by the total number of people.

Average (or Mean) = (a + b)/2

Equation:

[(x) + (x+4)]/2 = 19

x + x +4 = (19)(2)

Find x, Cora's age:

2x + 4 = 38

2x = 38 - 4

2x/2 = 34/2

x = 17 ⇒  Cora's age

Cora is 17 years old.

To solve for Coby's age, x+4 when x=17:

17 + 4 = 21  

Coby is 21 years old.

Check:

(17 + 21)/2 = 19

38/2 = 19

19 = 19  (True)

The Manor System

Manorialism, which had its roots in the villa system of the Late Roman Empire, was a fundamental component of feudal society and served as the guiding principle of the rural economy.

Each manor was dependent on the other manors for pasture and workers is a False statement.

The portion of the demesne that the lord directly controls and uses for the advantage of his household and dependents;Dependent holdings that require the peasant household to supply the lord with a set amount of labor services or a portion of its output;Free peasant land that is not subject to this responsibility but is nonetheless governed by manorial laws and customs and that is leased for a fixed amount of money.

The lord of a manor held both legal and financial authority throughout manorialism. The lord was financially sustained by his own direct landholding in a manor (sometimes referred to as a fief) and by the mandatory payments of the peasant population that was under his and his court's control.

These commitments may be repaid in cash, in kind, or, in exceptional circumstances, in work. In medieval Western and Eastern Europe, manorial systems may be found in Italy, Poland, Lithuania, the Baltic states, Holland, Prussia, England, France, and the Germanic kingdoms.
