3. What are the possible reasons why the iraya mangyans no longer speak the iraya language?


Answer:

Although there remains several number of Iraya speakers to this day, certain alarming developments could jeopardize the future of the language in some parts of Mindoro. A specific example would be the Irayas of Baclayán. Many of the children can only muster a smattering of Iraya, as Tagalog is slowly replacing their native language. And few show interest in preserving their language, as perhaps it is no longer useful to them.

Explanation:

The Iraya language is an Austronesian language belonging to the northern Mindoro group. The known dialects of Iraya are Abra-de-Ilog, Alag-Bako, Pagbahan, Palauan-Calavite, Pambuhan, and Santa Cruz. Each dialect is distinct, but the basic vocabulary and grammar of everyday speech are the same. Tagalog words are also used by the Irayas living in the border areas between the foothills.
