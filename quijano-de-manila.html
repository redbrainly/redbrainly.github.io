3. Why did Alonso Quijano decide to be a knight?


Alonso Quijano (spelled Quixano in English and Early Modern Spanish) is the personal name of the famous fictional hidalgo or knight better known as Don Quijote, the leading character of the novel Don Quijote de la Mancha, written by Miguel de Cervantes.

Other names or nicknames of Alonso Quijano that go appearing in the story are: the Knight of the Sad Face, that puts him alongside his own squire Sancho Panza, or the Knight of the Lions, adopted by Don Quijote after an encounter with lions (in the second part, at chapter 17).

[tex] \begin{aligned}& \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula:} \\& \small\boxed{PV = R\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1 + i)^{ \text -(k + n)} }{i} \bigg] -R\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1 + i)^{ \text -k} }{i} \bigg] } \end{aligned}[/tex]

- Use the scientific calculator to solve this.

[tex] \begin{aligned}\small{PV = 480\text, 000\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1 + 0.05)^{ \text -(5 + 8)} }{0.05} \bigg] -480\text,000\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1 + 0.05)^{ \text -5} }{0.05} \bigg] } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{aligned}\small{PV = 480\text, 000\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1.05)^{ \text -13} }{0.05} \bigg] -480\text,000\bigg[ \frac{1 - (1.05)^{ \text -5} }{0.05} \bigg] } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]PV = 2\text,430\text,766.232[/tex]
