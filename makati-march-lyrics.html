18. Math Problem. How long is the Troop? In the last December coup attempt, a troop soldiers march 6 km going to Fort Bonifacio from Makati, after they agreed to go back to barracks. at the same time , the General supervising the march rode from the rear of the troop to the front and back at once to the rear. If the distance covered by the general is 10 km and both the troop and the general traveled a uniform rate. How long is the troop?


answer: 3.20 kilometers

A troop soldiers march 6 km going to Fort Bonifacio from Makati, after they agreed to go back to barracks. At the same time, the General supervising the march rode from the rear of the troop to the front and back at once to the rear. If the distance covered by the general is 10 km and both the troop and the general traveled a uniform rate.

Thus, the ratio will be 10:6 = 5:3

Actually, I am not so confident with my formula and solutions. I just tried it. 

To solve, we should know what we're looking for. Apparently, "how long the troop" is refers to the length of the troop column.

Length of the troop column = 10 - x km 

Solve x first:

Time taken by general = x/(5-3) + x/(5+3) 

Time taken by troops = (x+6)/3 

Equation is x/2 + x/8 = (x+6)/3 

12x +3x = 8x + 48 

7x = 48 

x = 48/7 km 

x = 6.8


Therefore: 

Length of the troop column = 10 - 6.8km 

Length of the troop column = 3.2km
