Quasi-experimental Design

A quasi-experimental design resembles an experimental design but lacks the crucial ingredient – random assignment. The lack of random assignment, as well as the possibility of nonequivalence between the groups, complicates the statistical analysis of the nonequivalent groups design.

What is the quasi-experimental research method?

Quasi-experimental methods are research designs that compare treated units (households, groups, villages, schools, firms, etc.) to control units in order to determine the impact of a specific intervention, program, or event (a "treatment").

Attitude and opinion of high school students towards academic cheating​


Answer:

CorrelationalExperimentalCausal-ComparativeCorrelationalDescriptive

Explanation:

Correlational research is a non-experimental research method used to determine the relationships between two variables, such as gender and academic performance. This type of research can only determine the correlation (relationship) between the variables but the the causation.

In Experimental research, the researcher uses a scientific research design to answer the research question. It follows steps such as creation of hypotheses, identification of variables, and the collection of the needed data to determine is the experiment results support or reject the null hypotheses.

Causal-Comparative research determines the cause and and effect relationship between the independent and dependent variables.

Descriptive research to describe or explain the characteristics of a population or phenomenon being studied is required. The aim of this research is to addresses the "what" questions but not the how/when/whys.
