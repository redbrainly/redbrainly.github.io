21. a 200 ml sample of wine was found to contain 40 ml of ethanol what is the percentage by volume concentration of ethanol in the wine sample​


Given:

  volume of solute (ethanol) = 40 mL

  volume of solution (wine) = 200 mL

Asked: % by volume

Formula: % by volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

Solution:

  % by volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

  % by volume = [tex]\frac{40 mL}{200 mL}[/tex] x 100%

   % by volume = 0.2 x 100%

   % by volume = 20%

Answer: The percentage by volume of ethanol in the wine sample is 20%.

Explanation:

Solutions are made up of solute dissolved in a solvent. The amount of solute in a given solution describes the solution's concentration. It can be expressed through percent by mass, percent by volume, or percent by mass/volume. If the volume of solute and solution is given, we can make use of percent by volume to determine the concentration of the solution. This can be computed using the formula below:

  % by volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

The higher the percent concentration, the higher the concentration of the solution. Solutions can be diluted or unsaturated, saturated, or supersaturated solution.
