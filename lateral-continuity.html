2. how is the principle of lateral continuity described ?​


Answer:

The principle of lateral continuity states that layers of sediment initially extend laterally in all directions; in other words, they are laterally continuous. As a result, rocks that are otherwise similar, but are now separated by a valley or other erosional feature, can be assumed to be originally continuous.


3. briefly describe the principle of lateral continuity​


The principle of lateral continuity states that layers of sediment initially extend laterally in all directions; in other words, they are laterally continuous. As a result, rocks that are otherwise similar, but are now separated by a valley or other erosional feature, can be assumed to be originally continuous.

29. Why do you think humans continue to live near volcanoes despite the possibility of an eruption at some later time?


Life near Volcanoes

A huge population of humans continue to strive and live near volcanoes despite the imminent hazards that awaits them, maybe because of the livelihood and subsistence the volcano provides.

Aside from being a tourist attraction, the traces and aftermath of past eruptions of the volcano gives a healthy land to the surrounding areas, it fertilizes the soil that helps the farmers grow prosperous crops, and provides assistance to farmers so they can survive their day to day living.

Topography is also one of the reasons why many of us still chooses to live near volcanoes, this might be because there are nearby bodies of water such as lakes and streams that could provide food and livelihood. The best example for this is the case of Taal Volcano and the lake within its crater or caldera.
