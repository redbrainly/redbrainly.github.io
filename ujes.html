GUAN JU

This started to happen in the early 1800s. It has parts of the five main types of drama in China today, which are historical drama, spoken drama, song drama, dance drama, and ballet. The main color of a character's face paint shows how they feel about themselves.

Guan Yu Red Mask Red

is a sign of commitment, courage, bravery, honesty, and loyalty. Guan Yu, a general from the time of the Three Kingdoms (220–280) who was known for being loyal to his Emperor, Liu Bei, is a typical "red full face."

Tu Xingsun Is Yellow

Yellow is a sign of strength, ambition, and a level head. In the opera "Three Mountain Pass," Tu Xingsun is a dwarf general with special powers who has a yellow symbolic face.

Xia Houdun Blue

Blue is a sign of strength, bravery, and intelligence. In the opera "Bowang Slope," Xiahou Dun is Cao Cao's most trusted general. He has a blue face with three sharp points.

Zheng Wun Green Color

A green face tells the audience that the character is violent and impulsive, and it shows that the character has no self-control at all. In the opera "Green Dragon," Zheng Wun plays a general. His face is green and has three sharp points.

Hou Yi Purple Color

Purple is a sign of dignity, sophistication, and a level head. The reddish-purple face is also a sign of a good and fair person. In the opera "Green Dragon Pass," Hou Yi, who had a purple three-tile face, was a grain officer who knew how to use black magic.

Zhang Fei Black

Black is a sign of roughness and strength. The black face could mean that the person is rough and brave or that they are fair and selfless. General Zhang Fei with a black cross butterfly face from Romance of the Three Kingdoms and Li Kui from Water Margin are good examples of the former, while Bao Gong (also known as Bao Zheng), a fearless and fair judge from the Song Dynasty, is a good example of the latter.

Cao Cao White

White is associated with being sneaky, sneaky, suspicious, and crafty.
