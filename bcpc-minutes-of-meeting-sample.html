8. ActivityAnswer the following questions briefly:1. What is the importance of writing minutes of the meeting?2. What will happen if a company does not write minutes of the meeting?3. What is the most difficult part in writing the minutes of the meeting?​


Answer:

1. Meeting minutes are written, accurate accounts of the proceedings that take place at meetings. They should record important details, decisions and assignments. Meeting minutes provide references for future meetings and clarification of previous meeting details.

2. Not taking meeting minutes can be costly in terms of both time and money, for example when you and your colleagues have different recollections of what was agreed during a meeting. In the worst case, if meeting minutes are not written you may end up having to repeat the meeting.

3. One of the most difficult things about taking minutes is knowing what to write down and what to leave out. Keep these two central points in mind: Don't try to write everything down – it's impossible and not useful. Minutes are not a blow-by-blow description of what was said.

-Pre-Planning.

-Record taking at the meeting.

-Minutes writing or transcribing.

-Distributing or sharing of meeting minutes.

-Filing or storage of minutes for future reference.
