The message of the poem AMBAHAN

AMBAHAN is a traditional genre of poetry used by the Hanunó'o Mangyan people of Mindoro, Philippines.

The AMBAHAN has several distinct characteristics. To begin, it is a form of rhythmic poetry with seven-syllable lines and rhythmic end-syllables. It's also often sung as a chant, with no defined melodic pitch or instrument accompaniment. Finally, it is meant to describe certain occurrences or characteristics alluded to by the person reciting the poem in an allegorical fashion, lavishly employing poetic language.

The occurrence of seven syllables in a single line distinguishes AMBAHAN from other varieties of Hanunó'o Mangyan poetry. There are certain exceptions to this rule; the first half of the AMBAHAN may have more than seven syllables, but this portion is not considered poetry and just serves as an introduction. This might be due to a shortage of acceptable word combinations for a certain line, or the line may no longer be shortened. Although it is unusual, a line might be shorter than seven syllables in order to retain the meaning of the line, which would have been altered by the addition of syllables AMBAHAN.
