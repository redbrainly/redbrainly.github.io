13. Aball set on a flat surface rolls to the right whcih statement dose not correctly describe the motion of the ball​


Motion of a Ball in Flat surface

Take a look at friction. A completely smooth ball rolling on a perfectly level surface does not require any friction to slow it down, hence it does not require any friction to slow it down. Work demands that the active force (which in this case would be friction) generate relative motion between the bodies, which is certainly paradoxical (that is, force from friction would need to cause the ball to move sideways relative to whatever it is rolling on). The ball is spinning around an axis in pure rolling, but it is not translating relative to the point of contact between the ball and the ground (in pure rolling that point of contact is instantaneously stationary)

30. Explain how conservation of mechanical energy occur; 1 kicking aball, 2. Biking downhill, 3. Riding a swing. Explanation​


#BrainlyFast

Conservation of Energy states that energy will never be lost in the process. It can be transformed to another form of energy or it is converted to some other physical damages like the glass break when it falls to the ground.

Mechanical energy = P.E. + K.E

Upon kicking the ball, biking downhill, and riding a swing, the Mechanical Energy never change. What changes only is the Potential energy and Kinetic energy. This two is inversely proportional, when P.E. increases, then kinetic energy decreases and when you total them no matter what position is the object in, the result will always have the same mechanical energy.
