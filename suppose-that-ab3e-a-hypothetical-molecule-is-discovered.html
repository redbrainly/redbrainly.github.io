13. What is hypothetical


A hypothesis is a temporary explanation for a specific phenomena. It's like an "educated guess" about an occurrence.

Therefore, when something is "hypothetical", it is speculative, assumed, or arrived at by making an educated guess.

E.g. "I'm just being hypothetical, but what if the reason she's mad is because I forgot her birthday?"

14. A neutral molecule having the general formula AB3 has two unshared pair of electrons on A. What is the hybridization of A?


Explanation:

Hybrid Orbitals:

Valence Bond Theory states that the valence atomic orbitals (s, p, d, or f) of an atom overlaps with the valence atomic orbitals of another atom to form a covalent bond. However this simple notion is not consistent with experimental data such as the existence of methane,

4

.

Due to this inconsistency, the concept of hybrid orbitals was introduced. A hybrid orbital is basically a mixture fo two or more atomic orbitals which has lower energy in terms of the corresponding atomic orbitals making it more stable.

Answer and Explanation:

We can draw the structure as A being the central atom and is singly bonded to 3 B atoms. We also have 2 lone pairs around A. Thus, its structure is:

To determine the hybridization of the central atom A, we determine the number of electron groups (bonds and lone pairs) around it. There are 3 single bonds and 2 lone pairs around A. Hence, there are 5 electron groups around A and, thus, A requires 5 hybrid orbitals. This implies that we need to hybridize 5 atomic orbitals. We have a maximum of 1 s orbital, 3 p orbitals, 5 d orbitals, and 7 f orbitals to mix. Thus, we hybridize 1 s, 3 p and 1 d atomic orbitals to produce 5 hybrid orbitals. This gives hybrid orbitals.
