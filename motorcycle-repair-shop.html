1. a shop repaired 40 vehicles (cars and motorcycles) in a month. the total number of wheels was 100 how many cars and motorcycles were repaired?​


Answer:

10 cars and 30 motorcycles

Step-by-step explanation:

Pabrainliest po :)

Let x be the number of cars

Let y be the number of motorcycles

Total vehicles = x + y

40 = x + y

x = 40- y **Equation 1**

Number of wheels = 4x(for cars) + 2y(for motorcycle)

100 = 4x + 2y **Equation 2**

Substitute Equation 1 to 2

100 = 4(40 - y) +2y

100 = 160 - 4y +2y

100 = 160 -2y

2y = 60

y = 30

and substitute y to Equation 1 gives us

40 = x +30

x = 10

Therefore the answers are x = 10 and y = 30

5. principle of timeliness communication in auto repair shop​


Answer:

The principle of timeliness in communication in an auto repair shop refers to the importance of keeping customers informed about the status of their repair work in a timely manner. This can involve providing regular updates on the progress of the work, letting customers know when their vehicle is ready for pickup, and promptly addressing any questions or concerns that may arise.

Effective communication is an important aspect of customer service in any business, and this is especially true in the auto repair industry. Customers rely on their vehicles for transportation, and any delays or issues with the repair process can be stressful and inconvenient. By keeping customers informed and communicating promptly, auto repair shops can help alleviate these concerns and build trust with their customers.

There are several ways that auto repair shops can ensure timely communication with their customers:

Use a customer management system: Many auto repair shops use software to manage customer information, appointments, and repair orders. This can make it easier to track progress and communicate with customers in a timely manner.

Set clear expectations: It's important to set clear expectations with customers about how long their repair work is expected to take and how they will be kept informed about the process.

Provide regular updates: Keep customers informed about the progress of their repair work by providing regular updates, either in person, via phone, or through email or text.

Respond promptly to customer inquiries: If a customer has a question or concern, it's important to respond promptly to ensure that they feel valued and reassured.

By following these principles, auto repair shops can ensure that their communication with customers is timely and effective, helping to build trust and maintain customer satisfaction.

24. After your motorcycle breaks down, you start pushing it to the nearest repair shop. How will you compare the force you exerted on the motorcycle to the force exerted by the motorcycle on you?


Answer:

In order for the motorcycle to reach the nearest repair shop by pushing, we need to take a look at Newton's three laws of motion to understand the force exerted on the motorcycle and the force exerted by the motorcycle.

First Law (Inertia). Depending on the terrain, the amount of friction on the motorcycle wheels against the ground may require more force to push the vehicle. The greater the friction, the more force is needed to push the motorcycle as it will also exert greater force against the person (this is the third law of motion).

Second Law (Law of Force and Acceleration). The motorcycle wheels decreases the overall resistance of the motorcycle against the ground. When force is acted on the vehicle, the force will come to a point when it it greater that the total friction the wheel is exerting against the ground. This unequal distribution of forces moves the vehicle forward. Largely dependent on the size of the vehicle, more force would be needed to make it accelerate towards the shop. Greater force is needed to accelerate an Ninja than a Mio 125.

Third Law (Law of Action and Reaction). The net force exerted by the motorcycle against the force of the person pushing it depends on the mass the motorcycle, the friction on the wheels against the ground and even the lubrication of the wheels. The direction of this force is directly opposite to the push exerted by the person when pushing the vehicle.
