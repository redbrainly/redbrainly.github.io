7. 3. Faye wants to survey all the parents in Cagayan de Oro who opt to enroll their elementary children to an online class. All in all there 26,000 parents. Faye decided to have 450 from the target population sampling procedure justification ​


Answer:

Probability Sampling

Explanation:

Probability sampling involves random selection, allowing you to make strong statistical inferences about the whole group. Probability sampling is defined as a sampling technique in which the researcher chooses samples from a larger population using a method based on the theory of probability. For example, if you have a population of 100 people, every person would have odds of 1 in 100 for getting selected.  

Determine the sampling interval (K) by dividing the number of units in the population by the desired sample size. For example, to select a sample of 100 from a population of 400, you would need a sampling interval of 400/100 = 4. Therefore, K = 4. Therefore, Faye used probability sampling which involves random selection from stratified groups. There are no data of variable involved for other criteria.

11. sample detailed lesson plan in elementary level​


Answer:

Subject: English

Grade level: 4th grade

Duration: 45 minutes

Objectives:To introduce the concept of nounsTo identify common and proper nounsTo practice writing sentences using proper nouns

Materials:Whiteboard and markersChart paper and markersHandouts (worksheet on nouns)

Introduction (10 minutes):Greet the students and ask them how their day is going.Write the word "nouns" on the whiteboard and ask the students if they know what it means.Explain to the students that nouns are words that describe people, places, things, or ideas.Write a few examples on the whiteboard (e.g. dog, city, love, book, etc.).Ask the students if they can guess which ones are proper nouns.

Direct Instruction (20 minutes):Explain the difference between common nouns and proper nouns.Write "Common Nouns" and "Proper Nouns" on the chart paper and place it on the board.Write examples of common nouns (e.g. cat, street, happiness, etc.) under "Common Nouns".Write examples of proper nouns (e.g. Siamese, Main Street, Joy, etc.) under "Proper Nouns".Explain that proper nouns always start with a capital letter and refer to a specific person, place, or thing.Write a few sentences on the board and ask the students to identify the proper nouns in each sentence.

Guided Practice (10 minutes):Distribute the handouts to the students and explain that they are going to practice writing sentences using proper nouns.Give the students 5-7 minutes to complete the worksheet.Walk around the classroom to monitor the students and offer help if needed.

Closure (5 minutes):Call on a few students to share their sentences and highlight the proper nouns in each sentence.Summarize what the students learned during the lesson.Remind the students that proper nouns are important to use in writing to make their sentences clear and specific.Assessment:Observation of students during the guided practice activityCompleted handoutsTeacher-made quiz on proper nouns next lesson.

12. Simple Events Any event which consists of a single outcome in the sample space is called an elementary or simple event.​


Simple or Elementary Event: If there be only one element of the sample space in the set representing an event, then this event is called a simple or elementary event. For example; if we throw a die, then the sample space, S = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}. Now the event of 2 appearing on the die is simple and is given by E = {2}.

Answer:

It sounds like Faye is conducting a survey to gather information about the opinions and experiences of parents in Cagayan de Oro who have chosen to enroll their elementary-age children in online classes. Faye has identified a target population of 26,000 parents and has decided to use a sample size of 450 for her survey.

This sampling procedure allows Faye to gather data from a representative group of parents and draw conclusions about the larger population based on the results of the survey. By selecting a sample that is representative of the target population, Faye can increase the accuracy and reliability of her findings.

It is important to note that the sample size of 450 is just a small fraction of the total target population of 26,000 parents, and the results of the survey may not be perfectly representative of the entire population. However, by using statistical techniques, Faye can estimate the degree of uncertainty associated with her conclusions and determine the level of confidence she has in her findings.
