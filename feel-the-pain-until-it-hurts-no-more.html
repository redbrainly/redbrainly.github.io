3. why do we feel pain when we are hurt??​


Answer:

When your body is injured in some way or something else is wrong, your nerves (cells that help your body send and receive information) send millions of messages to your brain about what's going on. Your brain then makes you feel pain. So if you put your hand on a hot stove, your nerves call your brain, and your brain quickly sends the message that your hand hurts. You get this message and pull your hand away from the hot stove, which saves your hand from further injury.

20. In 8 sentences, explain the relation to The Man with the Hoe of the phrase : "There are two kinds of pain, the pain that hurts and the pain that changes a you forever."​


Answer:

The poem talks about the unfairness of treatment during the earlier years in America. The poet focuses on how spiritually exhausted the man is.  Feeling as though the world has taken his soul, this pain doesn't seem to be eased. Trying times could either change a person or make them suffer for eternity. The pain of these circumstances can help the person realize what he lacks, allowing him to improve himself. However, if the person refuses to look at a different point of view other than himself, it may traumatize the person from ever trying again. Humans are selfish beings, so we can't help but pity ourselves and give up when we're intimidated by the surrounding events. The poem, in conclusion, gives examples of what kind of situations could build a person from the pain they experience, but it's ultimately up to the man himself to decide what he wants to do.
