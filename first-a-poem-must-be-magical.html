2. in the first poem "Must be magical", what does the pairing of musical and seagull in the lines "First a poem must be magical/then musical as a seagull"achieve?​


Answer:

The pairing of "musical" and "seagull" in the lines "First a poem must be magical/then musical as a seagull" creates an image in the reader's mind that evokes a sense of freedom, grace, and fluidity. Seagulls are often associated with the sea, and the movement of the sea is often described as musical. By pairing "musical" and "seagull," the poet may be suggesting that a poem should be like a seagull in flight - free, fluid, and graceful. The use of the word "magical" also adds to the sense of wonder and enchantment that a poem should convey. Together, the pairing of "musical" and "seagull" helps to create a vivid and memorable image in the reader's mind, which may help to reinforce the poet's ideas about what a poem should be like.
