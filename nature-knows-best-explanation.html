Answer:

Paragraph 1:

Biased word: "best"Explanation: The word "best" is a biased word because it implies that the Philippines is superior to other countries as a tourist destination. While it is possible that Amira may have a personal preference for her home country, the statement does not acknowledge that different people may have different opinions about what makes a place the "best" to visit.

Paragraph 2:

Biased word: "all"Explanation: The word "all" is a biased word because it implies that every mother has an innate understanding of their child's needs through their crying. While it is true that mothers often develop a better understanding of their child's communication over time, not every mother may have the same level of intuition or experience.

Paragraph 3:

Biased word: "forgotten"Explanation: The word "forgotten" is a biased word because it implies that the youth of this era are entirely at fault for neglecting the virtue of obedience. It fails to consider external factors such as changing cultural values or societal pressures that may contribute to the lack of obedience in younger generations.

Paragraph 4:

Biased word: "outstanding"Explanation: The word "outstanding" is a biased word because it suggests that Roberto's public service was exceptional and noteworthy. While he may have been a good public servant, the statement does not provide any evidence or objective criteria to support the claim that he was "outstanding." Additionally, the statement portrays Roberto's family as dedicated to public service without acknowledging any potential biases or privileges that may have contributed to their success.
