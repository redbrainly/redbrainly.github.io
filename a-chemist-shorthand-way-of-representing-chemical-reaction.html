To solve this question, we first need to know the mass of one mole of mercury. This can be done by checking the periodic table.

From the periodic table, we can see that:

molar mass of mercury = 200.59 grams/mole.

From the measurements, the chemist found that the participated amount of mercury is 0.02 moles.

We can simply determine the mass of 0.02 moles by doing cross multiplication as follows:

mass of 0.02 moles = (0.02 x 200.59) / 1 = 4.0118 grams

mass of 0.02 moles = 4.01 gram

15. Suppose that a chemist performs a chemical reaction in which a gas is formed and finds 65.9 mL of the gas formed at 276 K and 0.987 atm. How many moles of the gas has this chemist produced?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{0.987 atm} \\ & V = \text{65.9 mL = 0.0659 L} \\ & T = \text{276 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.987 atm})(\text{0.0659 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{276 K})} \\ & = \boxed{2.87 \times 10^{-3} \: \text{mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the chemist produced 2.87 × 10⁻³ mol of the gas.

18. why is it useful for a chemist to know if a chemical is an acid or a base? ​


Answer:

To determine whether a substance is an acid or a base, count the hydrogens on each substance before and after the reaction. If the number of hydrogens has decreased that substance is the acid (donates hydrogen ions). If the number of hydrogens has increased that substance is the base (accepts hydrogen ions).
