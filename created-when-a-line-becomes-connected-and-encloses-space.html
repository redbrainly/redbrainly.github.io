2. created when a line becomes connected and encloses space​


Shape

When a line joins another and encloses a space, shape is produced. It is a thing's outline or exterior look. Because shapes are two-dimensional, you can measure their height and width.

In contrast to other characteristics like color, texture, or material type, a form or figure is a graphical depiction of an item or its exterior border, outline, or external surface. In contrast to solid 3D objects, a plane shape or figure is required to lie on a plane. On a more general curved surface, a two-dimensional form or figure may be identified.

Further explanation

Simple shapes can be grouped into a variety of general groups. For instance, polygons are categorized as triangles, quadrilaterals, pentagons, etc. depending on how many edges they have. Each of them is further broken down into smaller groups: quadrilaterals can be rectangles, rhombi, trapezoids, squares, etc., while triangles can be equilateral, isosceles, obtuse, acute, or scalene. Points, lines, planes, and conic sections like ellipses, circles, and parabolas are other frequent forms.

Polyhedra, which are things with flat sides, ellipsoids, which are objects with an egg or spherical form, cylinders, and cones are some of the most typical 3-dimensional shapes. We can use one of these categories to describe an object's form if it precisely or even roughly fits into one of them. In light of the fact that a manhole cover roughly resembles a geometric disk, we refer to it as having a disk form.

When position, size, orientation, and reflection are subtracted from the description of a geometric object, only the geometric information that remains is referred to as a geometric shape. In other words, a shape that has been moved about, made larger, rotated, or reflected in a mirror retains its original shape rather than changing.

A collection of points or vertices, lines joining the points in a closed chain, and the interior points that result can all be used to form several two-dimensional geometric shapes. Triangular, square, and pentagonal forms fall within the category of polygons. Other forms, like the circle or the ellipse, may have curves defining their edges.
