Ang pagta-trabaho nang night shift ay maaaring magdulot ng mga malulubhang karamdaman tulad ng diabetes, hypertension, at mga cardiovascular disease.

Dahil sa sapilitang pagbago sa circadian rhythm o ang takdang oras ng pagtulog, humihina ang katawan at tumataas ang posibilidad ng mga sakit. Narito ang ilan sa mga karamdamang maaaring makuha sa pagta-trabaho nang night shift.

Diabetes

Hindi pa man natutuklasan ang mismong mga sanhi ng diabetes, napag-alaman na konektado rito ang hindi pagtulog sa takdang oras. Ayon sa isang pag-aaral, mas mataas ng 1.09 beses ang probabilidad ng diabetes kapag patuloy na magtatrabaho sa labas ng nakasanayang oras ng pagtulog.

Nakita rin ng pagsusuring iyon na mas mataas ang panganib ng diabetes para sa mga lalaki, ngunit hindi rin ligtas sa sakit ang mga babae. Kinakailangang maging mas aktibo sa opisina at gumawa ng mga simpleng ehersisyo upang makaiwas sa pagiging overweight, na isa sa mga sanhi ng type 2 diabetes at mga cardiovascular disease.

Ang rotating shift o ang pabago-bago ng oras ng trabaho ay hindi rin nakabubuti. Ayon sa report ng Time, ang panganib ng type 2 diabetes ay mas mataas para sa mga may rotating shift. Ang abilidad ng katawan gumawa ng insulin ay maaaring makompromiso dahil sa tuloy-tuloy na pag-a-adjust ng body clock.

Atake sa Puso

Ang pagtatrabaho nang night shift ay maaaring maka-apekto sa sirkulasyon ng dugo sa puso at utak. Kapag hindi natanggap ng puso ang wastong dami ng dugo, maaari itong mauwi sa atake sa puso.

Ayon sa isang pananaliksik sa Canada, konektado ang pagtatrabaho ng night shift sa pagkakaroon ng heart attack. Sa pagitan ng 2010 at 2011, napag-alaman na 7 porsyento ng nagkaroon ng heart attack ay nagtatrabaho nang night shift.

Stroke

Hindi lang atake sa puso ang panganib sa pagtatrabaho nang gabi, tumataas din ang probabilidad ng stroke. Dahil apektado ang daloy ng dugo sa katawan, maaaring hindi matanggap ng utak ang kinakailangang dami nito.

Hypertension at High Cholesterol

Ang katawan ng tao ay nakaprogramang maging aktibo kapag may araw at magpahinga kapag madilim na. Dahil dito, marami sa mga nagtatrabaho nang night shift ay kulang sa tulog o nanghihina habang nasa opisina. Ang kawalan ng gawaing pisikal kasabay ang pagkonsumo ng mga maaalat at matatabang pagkain ay nagdudulot ng altapresyon at pagdami ng cholesterol sa katawan.

10. what is night shift differential


Night shift differential means the differential paid for work performed when the majority of a prevailing rate employee's regularly scheduled nonovertime hours fall between 3 p.m. and 8 a.m. It is computed as a percentage of the employee's rate of basic pay.

23. Bill and Sue both work a night shift. Bill has every seventh night off and Sue has every fourth night off. If they both have night off, how many nights will it be before off at the same time


WORD PROBLEM (LCM)

Bill and Sue have 27 nights before off at the same time because they are off at the same time on day 28.

FURTHER EXPLANATION

What is Least Common Multiple?

In mathematics, the value that is equally divided by the two supplied numbers is known as the LCM of any two. Least Common Multiple is the phrase's whole form. Another name for it is the Least Common Divisor (LCD). As an illustration, LCM (3, 5) = 15. In this case, the LCM 15 may be divided by both 3 and 5, hence these two numbers are referred to as the divisors of 15.

GIVEN

Bill and Sue both work a night shift. Bill has every seventh night off and Sue has every fourth night off.

Bill ⇒ 7th nightSue ⇒ 4th night

QuestionIf they both have a night off, how many nights will it be before off at the same time?

Solution:

Let:

Bill’s next nights off will be Day 7, 14, 21, 28, 35, and so on.Sue’s next nights off will be Day 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, and so on.

The smallest number which is both a multiple of 4 and a multiple of 7 is what you are seeking. The least common multiple is what we refer to as here. Since 4 and 7 don't have any factors, 4*7 = 28 is the lcm.

Therefore, Bill and Sue have 27 nights before off at the same time because they are off at the same time on day 28.

27. marco and enzo both work a night shift. marco has every sixth night off and enzo has every fifth night off. if they have both tonight off, how many nights will it be before they both off at the same time?


Answer:

30 nights.

Step-by-step explanation:

Marco goes off every 6 nights, while Enzo goes off every 5 nights. Their off nights present a pattern and they will both eventually take the night off at the same time.

The least common multiple, lowest common multiple (LCM) of two integers is the smallest integer that is divisible by that two integers. That means that the LCM of two integers can both be divided by the two integers with no remainder.

In more detail, a multiple of an integer is a product of that integer and another integer. For example, 21 is a multiple of 7 because the product of 7 and 3 (another integer) is 21.

Marco goes off every 6th night. At the 12th night, he also goes off. 12 is a multiple of 6 since 6*2 is 6.

Enzo goes off every 5th night. At the 10th night, he also goes off. 10 is a multiple of 5 since 5*2 is 6.

There are 2 practical ways of finding the LCM of 2 numbers. For smaller numbers, we can just list down the multiples of those 2 numbers. We can multiple each number by 1,2,3... and so on until we see common multiples.

Let us list the first few multiples of 6.

6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48, 54, 60.

Let us then list the first few multiples of 5.

5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50, 55, 60

We can see that 30 and 60 are both multiples of 5 and 6. We are finding for the least common multiple, so we compare 30 and 60. Since 30 is smaller, it is the least common multiple of 5 and 6. After the 30th night, both Enzo and Marco will take the night off.

The second practical way of getting the LCM of 2 numbers is getting its prime factorization. This works for larger numbers which have many factors and whose first few multiples are already large.

To get the LCM using this method, we list the prime factors of each number. We take the product of multiplying the highest power of each prime number, that will be our LCM.
