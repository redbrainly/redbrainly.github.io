14. what is the difference of coop with a business enterprise?​


Answer:

A co-op business is a type of corporation. Instead of being owned by shareholders, a cooperative is owned by the people who use the company's services or by its employees. Nobody owns a larger portion of the business than anyone else, so all votes are weighted equally.

Explanation:

How do cooperatives differ from businesses?

Having more in common with a traditional business than, say, a nonprofit organization, a cooperative distinguishes itself by a member ownership, benefits, and control model which puts power in the hands of the customers rather than a single owner or small group of partners.

16. how can good ventilation be attained in making a chicken coop?​


Good ventilation in a chicken coop is important to maintain the health and well-being of the chickens. The following are ways to achieve good ventilation in a chicken coop:

Install windows or vents: Install windows or vents on opposite sides of the coop to provide cross-ventilation and allow for fresh air to circulate. Make sure to position the windows or vents high enough to avoid drafts.

Provide adequate roof ventilation: A roof vent, ridge vent, or cupola can provide excellent ventilation in the coop. This will allow hot air to escape and fresh air to circulate.

Consider an exhaust fan: An exhaust fan can help to remove stagnant air and keep the coop well-ventilated. You can either install a fan in the roof or near the floor.

Plan the location of the coop: The location of the coop can also affect ventilation. Place the coop in a location that receives plenty of sunlight and air flow.

Keep the coop clean: Regular cleaning of the coop can help to keep the air fresh and prevent the buildup of harmful bacteria and ammonia. Make sure to remove bedding and other debris regularly.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your chicken coop has good ventilation, providing a healthy and comfortable environment for your chickens to live in.

24. mang lino is planning to construct a rectangular chicken coop with width 2m shorter than thrice the lenght.He wants to use at least 4m of chicken wire but at most 20 m of the materialWhat is the possible integral dimensions of coop?​


Answer:

Length (m) = x

Width (m) = 3x - 2

Perimeter (m) = x + (3x - 2) + x + (3x - 2) = 8x - 4

"He wants to use at least 4 m of chicken wire but at most 20 m of the material."

So 4 ≤ 8x - 4 ≤ 20

Add 4 to each part:

8 ≤ 8x ≤ 24

Divide by 8:

1 ≤ x ≤ 3

Minimum dimensions (where x = 1): 1 m * 1 m

Maximum dimensions (where x = 3): 3 m * 7 m
