10. A ______ provides for the greatest degree of continuity: Select one: a. Sole proprietorship b. Corporation c. General partnership d. Joint venture


answer: b. Corporation

A CORPORATION provides for the greatest degree of continuity.


Here’s an explanation:

a. Sole proprietorship – there could be an unlimited liability which is why it cannot provide greatest degree of continuity.

c. General partnership – there’s liquidation and it cannot be continued by succession.

d. Joint venture – the business activity or sharing as well as the transfer of ownership in the deal can only be project based or according to a limited contract.


A CORPORATION provides for the greatest degree of continuity because of its powers and right of succession.

A CORPORATION provides for the greatest degree of continuity and the reason to this is most probably SUCCESSION.

In business law, the perpetual succession is in fact the continuation of the existence or life of the corporation or other organization despite the death, insolvency, bankruptcy, insanity, membership change, exit from business of any part of it, or any transfer of stock, etc.

A corporation, according to the law, is an artificial being created by operation of law. It possesses the right of succession and the powers, attributes and properties expressly authorized by law or by the incident to its existence.

This comes from the definition stated by the Corporation Code - as artificial being created by operation of law, having the right of succession and the powers, attributes and properties expressly authorized by law or incident to its existence. As an artificial being, corporation has personality separate and distinct from stockholders. The general rule is that a corporation may not be made to answer for acts or liabilities of its stockholders and vice versa. It is the corporation, and not its stockholders, is personally liable for the corporate debts and / or liabilities.

However, the above rule isn’t actually that absolute. There’s this veil of corporate fiction. It may be pierced, though, when the fiction of corporate entity is being used as a cover for the fraud and / or illegality or to ruin and defeat public convenience, validate the wrong, protect the deception and / or defend any misconduct and crime.

24. Generally, how long is the life of a corporation?


Answer:

The Corporation Code, which was established in 1980, allows corporations to exist for 50 years from the incorporation date. This can be extended by 50 years at a time by amending the articles of incorporation. This can be done within five years of the original expiration date.

The average life span of today's multinational, Fortune 500-size corporation is 40 to 50 years For anyone in leadership at a corporation of any size, that should be a sobering statistic. That means the company you're tasked with leading has a 50/50 shot at making it past what we mere mortals call middle age

26. Illustrate and explain the general structure of the management of a corporation.


Answer: A corporation is an entity apart from its shareholders, with entirely distinct rights and  liabilities; is a business or organization formed by a group of people, and it has rights and liabilities separate from those of the individuals involved. A corporation's structure is consists of board of directors, officers, employees, shareholders and owners.

Explanation:

A corporation may be formed only by substantial compliance with a State  incorporation statute.

Classification of corporation:

Public or Private

Public Corporation one created to administer a unit of local civil government or one created by the United States to conduct public business.Private Corporation one founded by and composed of private persons for private purposes; has no governmental duties.

Profit or Nonprofit

Profit Corporation one founded to operate a business for profitNonprofit Corporation one whose profits must be used exclusively for charitable, educational, or scientific purposes.

Domestic or Foreign

Domestic Corporation one created under the laws of a given State.Foreign Corporation one created under the laws of any other State or jurisdiction; it must obtain a certificate of authority from each State in which it does intrastate business.

Publicly Held or Closely Held

Publicly Held Corporation one whose shares are owned by a large number of people and are widely traded.Closely Held Corporation one that is owned by few shareholders and whose shares are not actively traded.

Structure of the management:

Owners / Shareholders- are the ultimate owners of a corporation. They have the right to elect directors, vote on major corporate actions and share in the profits of the corporation.Board of directors- is primarily responsible for the governance of the corporation. It needs to be structured so that it provides an independent check on management.Officers- are high-level management executives hired by the business's owner or board of directors.Employees- primary duties relate to corporate-level activities.
