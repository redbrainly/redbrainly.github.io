22. Introduction to industrial Arts​


Answer:

Industrial arts, also known as technology education or vocational education, is a field of study that focuses on the principles and practices of a variety of technical and vocational disciplines. It typically includes courses in areas such as manufacturing, construction, automotive repair, and electronic technology, and may be offered at the middle, high school, or post-secondary level. The goal of industrial arts education is to provide students with the knowledge and skills they need to pursue careers in technical or vocational fields, or to use their skills in their personal or professional lives. Industrial arts education may also include hands-on, experiential learning opportunities, such as internships or lab experiences, to give students practical, real-world experience in their chosen field.
