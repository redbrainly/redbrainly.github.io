5. How many grams of Fe are needed to react with sufficient oxygen to produce 28.8 moles FeO


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

How many grams of Fe are needed to react with sufficient oxygen to produce 28.8 moles FeO

Balanced Chemical Equation:

                         2 Fe  +   1 O₂  ⇒  2 FeO

                               ↓            ↓              ↓

                               2     :      1       :       2

Note:

The mole ration of Fe, O₂ and FeO is 2:1:2 which means, 2 moles of Fe will react to every 1 mole of O₂ to produce 2 moles of FeO.

Identifying molar mass:

Fe = 56 grams per mole

O₂ = 32 grams per moleFeO = 72 grams per moleBase on the mole ratio, 112 grams of Fe will react with 32 grams of  O₂ to produced 144 grams of FeO.

How many grams of Fe are needed to react with sufficient oxygen to produce 28.8 moles FeO?

Answer:

Since the mole ratio of Fe and FeO are the same, therefore you need 28.8 moles of Fe to produce 28.8 moles of FeO and 28.8 moles of Fe is converted to grams to get the answer.

Conversion of 28.8 moles of Fe =  ________grams

28.8 moles × 56 g / 1 mole = 1,612.8 grams

Answer:

You need 1,612.8 grams of Fe to produced 28.8 moles of FeO.

Answer:

Types of Internet Protocols

There's more to the Internet than the World Wide Web

When we think of the Internet we often think only of the World Wide Web. The Web is one of several ways to retrieve information from the Internet. These different types of Internet connections are known as protocols. You could use separate software applications to access the Internet with each of these protocols, though you probably wouldn't need to. Many Internet Web browsers allow users to access files using most of the protocols. Following are three categories of Internet services and examples of types of services in each category.

File retrieval protocols

This type of service was one of the earliest ways of retrieving information from computers connected to the Internet. You could view the names of the files stored on the serving computer, but you didn't have any type of graphics and sometimes no description of a file's content. You would need to have advanced knowledge of which files contained the information you sought.

FTP (File Transfer Protocol)

This was one of the first Internet services developed and it allows users to move files from one computer to another. Using the FTP program, a user can logon to a remote computer, browse through its files, and either download or upload files (if the remote computer allows). These can be any type of file, but the user is only allowed to see the file name; no description of the file content is included. You might encounter the FTP protocol if you try to download any software applications from the World Wide Web. Many sites that offer downloadable applications use the FTP protocol

26. If we burn the log in an enclosed system, how do the total mass-energy of the system before and after the burning compare?​


Answer:

The total mass-energy of the system before and after the burning will be the same. This is because the principle of conservation of mass-energy states that the total mass-energy of a closed system remains constant. When the log is burned, the chemical energy stored in the wood is converted into heat, light, and other forms of energy, such as the kinetic energy of the gases produced by the combustion reaction. But the total mass-energy of the system, which includes the mass of the log and the energy it contains, remains constant.

The principle of conservation of mass-energy states that the overall mass-energy in a closed system stays constant, even though it may change forms. In the case of burning a log, the chemical energy stored in the log is transformed into heat and light energy and released into the surrounding area. Therefore, the total mass-energy of the system before and after the burning will be the same.
